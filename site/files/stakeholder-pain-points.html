Stakeholder Map & Pain Points

stakeholder-pain-points v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ?Assumption ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗

Honest Framing

These pain points are inferred from literature and from the structure of the industry. They are NOT from interviews, because no interviews have been conducted. Every entry is a hypothesis with a question attached that Phase 0 discovery must answer. A stakeholder map built from desk research describes the industry an outsider imagines. This applies with equal force to the Phase 1/2 software stakeholders added in this revision as it does to the original Phase 3 hardware stakeholders — there are zero registered app users as of this writing (see traction.json), so the beginner-farmer and growing-farm pains below are no less speculative than the smallholder-cage-operator pains always were.

Stakeholders

Beginner / First-Time Farmer

Stakeholder Id
beginner-pond-farmer
Description

New to fish farming, typically starting a single pond or a small number of cages, often financed out of savings or a small loan. May not yet know what they don't know. The primary target of the free Phase 1 app.

Power
low
Interest
high
Is Customer
yes — the free Phase 1 acquisition audience
Pains
PainSeverity
No structured guidance on species choice, stocking density, siting, or pond preparation — decisions made on guesswork or whatever the input supplier says.high
No habit or easy tool for logging water conditions or feeding, so mistakes are never diagnosed and lessons are never retained.medium
No affordable or immediate way to get a sick fish looked at; waits for symptoms to worsen or for an extension visit that may never come.high
Works in isolation, with no peer community to ask a question before making an expensive mistake.medium
Fears that one bad cycle, caused by something they did not know to watch for, ends the venture before it starts.high
Gains Sought
  • Not failing in the first cycle
  • Knowing what a normal pond is supposed to look like
  • Someone to ask a question without feeling foolish
Willingness To Pay

Effectively zero at this stage — this is deliberately the free tier. The value exchange is engagement, retained data, and eventual candidacy for Phase 2 conversion, not present-day revenue. Treating this as a revenue segment would be dishonest; see traction.json.

The Hard Truth
This is the stakeholder the whole funnel strategy depends on, and we have never spoken to one about this product. Whether a free app is actually what a beginner farmer wants — versus a person, a cooperative, or a government extension officer — is unknown. If it turns out beginners do not engage with a digital tool at this stage, Phase 1 fails to acquire anyone and Phases 2 and 3 have no funnel beneath them.
Discovery Questions
  • What made you decide to start farming fish, and who did you ask for help first?
  • What was the first thing that went wrong, and how did you find out what to do about it?
  • Would you have used a phone app for this, or did you want a person?

Smallholder Cage Operator

Stakeholder Id
smallholder-cage-operator
Description

Operates a handful of cages in a lake, often financed by debt against the coming harvest. Frequently the family's sole income. Distinct from the beginner-pond-farmer above in that this stakeholder is specifically exposed to the catastrophic, tier-3 mass-mortality risk of shared-lake cage culture, not just the general guidance gap of starting out.

Power
low
Interest
very high
Is Customer
aspirationally, and via the free app in the near term
Pains
PainSeverity
A single kill event destroys a year of income and leaves the debt intact.existential
Cannot afford monitoring hardware, and would not have time to use it even if given some.high
Shares a lake with operators whose stocking decisions they cannot influence but whose consequences they absorb.high
Has no proof of good practice, so cannot access credit or insurance on decent terms.medium
Has been sold technology before that did not survive its first monsoon.medium
Gains Sought
  • Not losing everything
  • Sleeping through the night
  • Being believed by a lender
Willingness To Pay

Very low as capital for hardware. Possibly meaningful as a per-cycle service fee bundled into inputs or credit, and only if the value is legible in the first cycle. The free app costs them nothing and is the only near-term product this stakeholder should actually be offered.

The Hard Truth
This is the stakeholder with the most acute catastrophic-tier need and the least ability to pay for hardware. Every impact-oriented instinct says serve them first, and every commercial instinct says they cannot be the Phase 3 beachhead. The honest resolution, and the reason this repository now leads with a free app rather than a drone, is that this stakeholder is served at Phase 1 through free software and reached at Phase 3, if ever, through cooperatives, input suppliers, or public programmes — not through direct hardware sales — and pretending otherwise produces a business plan that fails in year two.
Discovery Questions
  • What did the last kill actually cost you, including the debt?
  • If you had known 12 hours earlier, what would you have done — concretely, with what equipment?
  • Who do you already pay every cycle, and for what?

Commercial Farm Manager

Stakeholder Id
commercial-farm-manager
Description

Runs a larger operation with staff, some existing instrumentation, aeration capacity, and a budget line for inputs. Sits at the top of the Phase 2 growth tier and is the realistic beachhead for Phase 3 hardware once the analytics case is proven.

Power
medium
Interest
high
Is Customer
yes — the realistic beachhead for both Phase 2 premium and Phase 3 hardware
Pains
PainSeverity
Manages many units and cannot personally observe them all, so relies on staff reports of variable quality.high
Has outgrown the spreadsheet or notebook that worked at a smaller scale, with no analytics layer to compare units or spot a trend across cycles.high
Feed is the largest cost line and is dispensed with no feedback loop.high
Aeration is either run continuously — an enormous electricity bill — or reactively and too late.high
Certification audits demand environmental records that are compiled by hand from paper.medium
Wants an aquaculturist or vet's opinion on a marginal case but retaining one is not justified at this scale, and finding one on short notice is hard.medium
Buys feed and equipment and sells output through personal networks and informal middlemen, with no transparent way to compare prices.medium
Losses are absorbable but they destroy the year's margin.high
Cannot answer the board's question of why survival was 68 percent this cycle and 84 percent last cycle, because nobody measured anything that would explain it.medium
Gains Sought
  • Predictable survival rates
  • Defensible FCR improvement
  • Lower aeration electricity
  • Audit-ready records without the clipboard
  • A trend flagged before it becomes a bad cycle
  • Affordable, on-demand access to expert judgement
Willingness To Pay

Real. Has a budget, can compute an ROI, and already spends on inputs and consultants. This is the stakeholder Phase 2's subscription and take-rate model is built around.

Why They Are the Beachhead

They have the things a first paying customer must have: money, response capacity — aerators they can actually switch on when warned — and a person whose job it is to care about the number. The smallholder has the greatest catastrophic-tier need but cannot act on the information or afford it; this stakeholder can act on both the Phase 2 analytics and, eventually, the Phase 3 hardware.

Discovery Questions
  • What does your aeration electricity cost per cycle, and how do you currently decide when to run it?
  • What would you pay to move FCR by 0.1?
  • Who compiles your audit records and how many hours does it take?
  • At what point did your paper or spreadsheet system stop being enough, and what did you do about it?
  • Would you pay a monthly fee for analytics and expert access, or only for hardware you can point to?

Farm Technician / Caretaker

Stakeholder Id
farm-technician
Description

The person who actually takes the readings, feeds the fish, and finds the mortalities. Often lives on site.

Power
low
Interest
high
Is Customer
no — but is the user, and can kill the deal
Pains
PainSeverity
Takes a boat out before dawn in bad weather. This is dangerous and everyone treats it as routine.high
Blamed when fish die, despite having no instrument that would have shown it coming.high
Records readings on paper that nobody reads.medium
Gains Sought
  • Not drowning
  • Evidence that it was not their fault
  • Less pointless clipboard work
Critical Insight

This stakeholder is where technology deployments die. If the technician perceives the drone as surveillance of their performance or as a threat to their job, they will not charge it, they will not clean the probe, and they will find that it was broken every time it mattered. The product must be framed and designed as their instrument — evidence that supports their judgement — not as management's monitor of them.

Design Implication

The dashboard must never surface a 'technician response time' metric. The moment it does, the technician becomes the adversary of the system and the data quality collapses. This is a genuine and commonly-made mistake.

Discovery Questions
  • What is the worst part of the pre-dawn round?
  • When fish died last time, what happened to you?

Fisherfolk Cooperative / Association

Stakeholder Id
cooperative
Power
medium
Interest
high
Is Customer
yes — plausibly the right unit of sale for smallholders
Pains
PainSeverity
Members suffer correlated losses; the co-op has no early warning to pass on.high
No credible data to represent members' interests in lake management disputes.medium
Cannot access group insurance because the risk is unquantified.medium
Why They Matter

A cooperative can buy one drone and a dock and amortise it across forty members, which converts an impossible per-farm capital cost into a viable per-member service fee. If there is a route to the smallholder, it runs through here. It also converts the shared-lake commons problem from a source of conflict into a shared asset — the co-op has a legitimate interest in lake-wide data that no individual member does.

Discovery Questions
  • What do members already pay dues for?
  • Who arbitrates stocking disputes today?

Stakeholder Id
bfar
Power
high
Interest
high
Is Customer
possibly — as a data buyer or programme funder
Pains
PainSeverity
Mandated to manage fish kills and carrying capacity with almost no continuous data.high
Responds to kills after the fact; cannot warn.high
Cannot enforce carrying capacity limits without measurements to justify them.high
Why They Matter

A regulator is both a potential customer and a potential existential threat. Aligned early, they are a distribution channel into every lake in the country. Ignored, they are the body that decides whether your BVLOS operation and your dispensing system are permitted at all.

Strategic Note

Engage from Phase 0, not when you need something. Regulators remember which companies showed up before they had an ask.

Strategic Note

A fascinating and under-explored channel. Feed companies already reach every farm, already extend credit, and already have field technicians. They also have a structural conflict: our product reduces feed waste, which reduces their volume. Whether that conflict is fatal depends on whether they compete on volume or on demonstrated FCR — and the premium players increasingly compete on FCR. Worth testing.

Insurer / Agricultural Lender

Stakeholder Id
insurer-lender
Power
medium
Interest
medium
Is Customer
strategically interesting
Pains
PainSeverity
Cannot underwrite aquaculture risk because it is unmeasured, so either does not lend or prices punitively.high
No way to verify a claim or detect moral hazard after a kill.high
Strategic Note

Potentially the most valuable relationship available. Continuous environmental records turn an uninsurable asset into an insurable one. If insurance access becomes contingent on monitoring, the monitoring stops being a discretionary purchase — which changes the entire adoption curve. This is a long game and should not be in the Series A story, but it should be in the founders' heads.

Why Not Now
It requires years of loss history to price against, which we do not have. Chicken and egg.

Why They Matter

Validation credibility. A claim that our profiles match a reference sonde carries little weight from us and substantial weight from a university fisheries department. They also have the historical lake data we lack and the students to do the paired sampling that Phase 3 requires.

This stakeholder has no commercial relationship with us and every ability to make our operation socially impossible. A drone flying over a lake photographs houses, boats, and people on shorelines. Caveat
CAAP appears in two quadrants deliberately. They have very high power and low interest, which is the textbook 'keep satisfied' position — but because they hold a veto on the core operating concept, treating them passively would be a mistake. Where the framework and the situation disagree, follow the situation.

The Alignment Problem

Observation

The stakeholder whose need is greatest at the acute end (smallholder cage operator) is not the stakeholder who can pay for hardware (commercial farm), who is not the stakeholder who controls access (LGU/BFAR), who is not the stakeholder who decides if you may fly (CAAP), who is not the stakeholder who will actually operate the machine (technician). The phased platform adds a sixth constituency underneath all of them: the beginner pond farmer, who is the easiest to acquire and the hardest to monetise, and who this whole strategy bets will eventually grow into the commercial farm manager above.

Implication

Six different constituencies must each say yes across the life of a single customer relationship, and each says yes for a different reason, on a different timescale, for a different product. This is not a product sale; it is a coalition stretched across three phases. Any go-to-market plan that models this as 'find farmers, sell drones' — or, equally naively, as 'get free users, they'll convert' — has not understood the sector.

Why It Is In the Problem Section

Because it is a problem — ours. It is the reason aquaculture technology has a poor adoption record despite good technology, and it deserves as much design attention as the winch, the onboarding flow, and the conversion funnel alike.
