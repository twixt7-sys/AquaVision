Regulatory Framework

regulatory-framework v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ✓Sourced ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗
⚠CRITICAL DISCLAIMER

THIS IS NOT LEGAL ADVICE. Every regulatory statement below derives from SECONDARY, SEARCH-RESULT SUMMARIES of public rules, not from primary regulatory texts read in full. Regulations change. The interpretations here may be wrong. Before ANY regulatory claim enters an investor deck, a contract, or a design decision, obtain a written opinion from a qualified Philippine aviation and fisheries lawyer and confirm current requirements directly with the agencies. Building on this file as if it were authoritative would be a serious error.

The Regulatory Reality

This product sits at the intersection of THREE regulatory domains — aviation, fisheries, and (for medicine) veterinary — and each has a different agency, a different logic, and a different veto. The BVLOS dependency alone can gate the entire business model. Regulatory work is not a compliance afterthought here; it is on the critical path and it should be resourced from Phase 0. This entire regulatory picture, however, belongs to Phase 3.

The Phased Regulatory Burden

Statement

The three-domain aviation/fisheries/veterinary regulatory load below is a Phase 3 (hardware) problem. Phase 1 and Phase 2 — the app, the free tier, the premium analytics and marketplace — carry a materially lighter regulatory burden, and that is a genuine competitive advantage worth stating plainly rather than treating as a footnote.

What Phase 1 2 Actually Engages
  • Data Privacy Act of 2012 — the same regime this repo already treats seriously for Phase 3 imagery, applied instead to farmer-entered account and farm data. See ethics-data-privacy.json.
  • Standard consumer app-store and consumer-protection terms of service.
  • Payment/marketplace transaction handling in Phase 2 (a commerce-law question, not an aviation one).
  • No CAAP airspace, registration, or pilot-certification exposure. No BFAR substance-dispensing question. No veterinary-medicine dispensing question.
Why This Matters For the Narrative

It means AquaVision can reach real users, generate real revenue signal, and start the CAC/activation/conversion learning loop years before the BVLOS and night-operations dependencies (below) are anywhere near resolved. The regulatory critical path gates Phase 3 revenue, not first revenue. That decoupling is a large part of why the phased structure de-risks the venture relative to the old drone-first story.

The Honest Caveat

Lighter is not zero. Data-custody claims made to thousands of Phase 1 users create real exposure if broken, and a content-moderation or marketplace-transaction failure in Phase 2 is a real (if smaller) liability question of its own. See ethics-data-privacy.json.

Civil Aviation — CAAP
Agency
Civil Aviation Authority of the Philippines

What We Understand
Point
Weight brackets
Detail

Obligations bracket at gross weight. At or below 7 kg: general safety rules, certification for commercial use. Above 7 kg to 150 kg: mandatory registration, stricter compliance, pilot licensing. Above 150 kg: special airworthiness certificates.

Our Exposure

The airframe targets under 7 kg specifically to sit in the lighter bracket — but commercial use requires certification regardless, so the 7 kg line is less decisive than it looks. See omnidrone-specifications.json#/top_level_targets.

Point
Registration and marking
Detail

Commercial drones and any over 7 kg must be registered; a unique marking number must be affixed.

Our Exposure
Applies to us as a commercial operator.
Point
Pilot certification
Detail

Commercial or >7 kg operation requires an RPA Controller Certificate via an ATO, knowledge test, and practical demonstration.

Our Exposure
Hard gate on revenue. See training-certification.json.
Point
VLOS default
Detail
Visual line of sight is the default permitted mode.
Our Exposure
Our early operations are VLOS and therefore need a pilot on site.
Point
BVLOS requires specific authorisation
Detail

Beyond visual line of sight needs a special permit, as do night operations, flight above 400 ft, flight within 10 km of airports, and flight over populated areas.

Our Exposure

THE BIG ONE. The autonomous dawn-sortie use case needs BVLOS AND night-operations permission. Both are on the critical path. The unit economics assume BVLOS. If it does not come, the model must be re-based on VLOS.

Point
Emerging Remote ID and spraying-drone circulars
Detail

Discussion of electronic identification and advisory circulars covering agricultural spraying permits (e.g., AC 02-2025 per public summaries).

Our Exposure
Our dispensing function may fall under a spraying-drone framing. Unconfirmed.
The Night Operations Problem

The flagship use case flies before dawn. Night operations require a separate permit ON TOP of BVLOS. This compounds the regulatory dependency — two permissions, both non-trivial, both gating the single most valuable sortie. This is not adequately surfaced in most versions of the pitch and it should be.

Domain
Fisheries — BFAR
Agency
Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources
What We Understand
  • BFAR governs aquaculture practice, fish health, and fish-kill response.
  • Anything touching aquatic animal health or the introduction of substances into culture water plausibly engages BFAR authority.
  • BFAR is simultaneously a potential customer, a data partner, and a regulator — see stakeholder-pain-points.json.
Our Exposure

The dispensing function and any health-related claim engage BFAR. Operating over municipal waters may require coordination. And BFAR's own carrying-capacity and fish-kill mandates are an opportunity as much as an obligation.

Verification Status
needs_verification
Action

Confirm which BFAR administrative orders apply to (a) monitoring services and (b) substance dispensing in culture water.

Environment — DENR / EMB

Department of Environment and Natural Resources / Environmental Management Bureau
What We Understand
  • DENR owns the water quality criteria our system reports against and the effluent standards farms are subject to.

Our Exposure

We report values against DENR criteria, so we must cite them correctly. Misstating a regulatory threshold in an alert is both a compliance and a liability problem.

Domain
Veterinary Medicine
Agency
BAI / FDA / professional veterinary regulation
What We Understand
  • Dispensing veterinary medicinal products is a fundamentally different regulated activity from dispensing lime or conditioner.
  • It plausibly brings prescription requirements, professional oversight, record-keeping, and withdrawal periods.
  • The drone becomes an application device for a regulated substance.
Our Exposure

The medicine-dispensing feature described in the founding concept may require veterinary supervision and may not be legal for autonomous or semi-autonomous application at all.

The Recommendation

Design the dispensing subsystem around conditioners, lime, and probiotics FIRST. Treat veterinary medicine as a separate, later, legally-gated capability. Do not build a medicine dispenser and then discover it needs a vet on site. See carriage-ejection-system.json#/regulatory_gate.

The Honest Summary

The business model as written assumes a BVLOS authorisation the company does not have, for night operations, dispensing substances whose legal classification is unconfirmed, over waters requiring per-site LGU permission. That is four unresolved regulatory dependencies, and two of them (BVLOS, night ops) gate the single most valuable capability. This is not a reason to abandon the venture — it is a reason to resource regulatory work from day one and to stress-test the model against the pessimistic regulatory outcome.

The Liability Question

Statement
If the system fails to warn and fish die, who is liable?
Why It Is Unresolved

A monitoring product that customers rely on for high-stakes decisions carries a duty-of-care exposure that is genuinely unclear. A missed warning, a false all-clear from a drifted sensor, or a dispensing error each raises a liability question we cannot answer from engineering intuition.

Our Position Pending Advice
  • The system advises; the human decides and is accountable. This is designed into the human-machine boundary, but whether it holds up legally is a lawyer's question.
  • Terms of service must be explicit that the system is decision-SUPPORT, not a guarantee.
  • The 'absence of an alert is not a clearance' principle must be legally, not just visually, established.
Action

A liability opinion before the first paid deployment. Not after. This is the kind of exposure that does not appear until the first bad outcome, and by then the terms are already set.
