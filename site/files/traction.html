ℹTHE DELIBERATE EMPTINESS OF THIS FILE
Statement:

This file is empty of traction because there is none, and that emptiness is a deliberate act of integrity, not an oversight. That discipline does not change just because the business model is now software-first and a free app is, in principle, faster to ship and adopt than a drone.

The Temptation Being Resisted:

It would be trivial to populate this file with an invented install count, a fabricated waitlist, imaginary letters of intent from cooperatives or commercial farms, or a 'early users love it' quote. Many pitch decks do exactly this, and a phased software story does not make the fabrication less discoverable in diligence.

Why We Refuse:

A pitch that invents traction is fraud, and it is the specific kind of fraud diligence is designed to catch. A single fabricated install number or LOI that an investor tries to verify ends the raise and the reputation. A pitch that shows honest zeros against a rigorous phased plan survives diligence and builds the trust the unverifiable later claims depend on. See funding-ask.json#/the_counterintuitive_bet.

The Principle:

Fill this file only from reality. Every field below is a real category of traction, currently and honestly at zero or 'not started', restructured to match the three-phase model. When real traction arrives, it goes here, dated and verifiable.

The Milestones That Will Constitute Real Traction

Note

What to put here as it actually happens. Each is a genuine de-risking event, ordered by the risk it removes, now sequenced across the three phases rather than a single hardware validation path.

In Order
  • Phase 0: farmer discovery interviews validating the free-tier feature set.
  • Phase 1: app launched publicly on at least one app store.
  • Phase 1: a measured activation rate and a real base of weekly-active users.
  • Phase 1: evidence that organic/community channels acquire users without paid marketing spend.
  • Phase 2: premium tier, marketplace, and consultation features launched.
  • Phase 2: a measured free-to-paid conversion rate — the single most important number this file is currently missing.
  • Phase 2: the first paying subscriber, and the first marketplace/consultation transaction.
  • Phase 3: a signed academic validation MOU and a documented BVLOS pathway from CAAP engagement.
  • Phase 3: winch 1000-cycle reliability demonstrated.
  • Phase 3: profile-vs-reference-sonde agreement demonstrated — the moment the core hardware claim becomes true.
  • Phase 3: a full-season validation dataset, peer-reviewed by the academic partner.
  • Phase 3: the first paying hardware customer with renewal intent, and measured (not modeled) Phase 3 unit economics.
  • Phase 3: BVLOS authorisation obtained.
The Most Important One

In the near term, the measured Phase 2 free-to-paid conversion rate is the single traction milestone that most changes this company's fundability, because it converts the central commercial claim of the phased pivot — that a free acquisition engine can be monetised — from an assumption borrowed from generic software benchmarks into a demonstrated number. Longer term, the Phase 3 profile-vs-reference-sonde agreement remains the milestone that converts the hardware layer's central technical claim from plausible to demonstrated, unchanged from the original plan.

How To Present No Traction

The Pitch

'We have no traction yet, and here is why that is the honest and correct state for a company that has just repositioned around a phased plan: we have not spent on hardware or a large team before validating that the free tier is worth building and that farmers will actually use it. Here is the small, cheap first phase that produces our first real traction — a launched free app and its first activation and retention numbers — and here is what would tell us to change course. Fund that, and watch us produce evidence, fast, because a launched app produces usage data in weeks, not seasons.'

Why This Works

It reframes the absence of traction from a weakness into evidence of capital discipline, and it is arguably a stronger pitch under the phased model than under the original hardware-only plan, because the first piece of real evidence (app usage) is now genuinely close and cheap to produce rather than a season away. Investors who reject honest zeros in favour of fabricated LOIs or install counts are selecting for dishonesty, and they are not the investors this company wants.
