Elevator Pitches

elevator-pitches v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

Note

The same argument at four lengths and for different audiences. All honest — none claims a flown product or a validated prediction.

The One Liner

Text

We make the water legible to the people who farm it — an autonomous drone that profiles a fish farm's water at depth, several times a day, and warns the farmer before the fish die.

Use
The first sentence to anyone.

The 15 Second

Text

Aquaculture just became the majority of the world's aquatic animal production, and its operators are flying blind — they find out their water has gone bad when the fish are already floating. The OmniDrone flies over the farm, drops a probe to measure the water column at depth, and turns that into a warning early enough to act on. We're building the observability layer for an industry that has none.

Use
A hallway, an intro, a conference.

The 60 Second

Text

In 2022, aquaculture passed capture fisheries for the first time — most of the world's farmed fish now grow in ponds and lakes that someone owns and could, in principle, instrument. But almost nobody does, because proper monitoring means putting a person in a boat with a sensor several times a day, including before dawn, at every cage. So operators guess, and every year fish kills destroy harvests — 343 tonnes in one Taal Lake event alone. The thing that kills the fish is usually invisible: oxygen-starved water sitting below the surface that a cold rain brings up overnight. A surface reading at 9 a.m. shows nothing wrong. The OmniDrone is an autonomous drone that lowers a probe to profile the whole water column, several times a day, at every cage, and it carries its own calibration so its aerial bloom maps are real measurements, not guesses. We sell it as a service, priced against the aeration electricity it saves — a number the farmer can verify in one cycle. We're pre-seed, nothing has flown yet, and we're raising a small first round to prove the two hard things: that the drone's profiles match a reference instrument, and that farmers will pay.

Use
A real meeting. Note it ends on the honest ask, not a claim.

The Pitch For A Farmer

Text

You know your water better than any machine. But you can't be at every cage at 4 in the morning, and you can't see what's happening three metres down. That's when and where the trouble starts. This drone does that round for you — measures the oxygen at every cage, at the depths that matter, before dawn, and tells you if something's building. It also spreads your treatment where you need it without you taking the boat out in bad weather. We're not asking you to trust an algorithm — the first thing you'll see is your aerator running fewer hours for the same result, on your own electricity bill.

Use

A farmer, a cooperative meeting. Respects their expertise; leads with the checkable benefit.

The Pitch For An Impact Investor

Text

In this sector a single night can wipe out a family's year of income, and the people most exposed are the smallholders who can least afford to see it coming — and the dangerous work of watching the water falls on someone in a small boat before dawn. We're building a platform that makes the water legible at depth, at scale, at near-zero marginal labour, and we reach smallholders through cooperatives and public programmes rather than pretending they can buy capital equipment. It aligns squarely with SDG 2, 14, and 12 — and we'll tell you the tensions too: better instruments could enable over-stocking, and we have only a partial answer to that. We measure our impact against a counterfactual, and we'll publish the null results if the fish don't do better. We think honesty about what we don't know is the thing that makes the rest believable.

Use
An impact fund. Leads with the human stakes, volunteers the tensions.

The Pitch For A Technical Investor

Text

The hard, defensible thing here is a tethered multiparameter sonde deployed from a hovering multirotor — a powered winch delivering a probe to depth, thousands of times, over a fouling lake full of nets, unattended. That's the moat, because anyone can fly a camera. The elegant part is that the probe generates the ground truth that calibrates the aerial multispectral retrieval, in the same sortie — which is exactly the calibration problem the UAV water-quality literature keeps identifying and solving with a boat. We're honest about the risks: the pendulum control problem could cap our wind envelope, the mass budget is tight, and a USV beats us on endurance. Our answer to the USV is transit speed across many stations plus simultaneous aerial imaging plus ignoring the pond topology entirely. We're at TRL 3 and the first dollar goes to the arithmetic that tells us whether the aircraft can carry its mission at all.

Use

An investor who will ask hard engineering questions. Leads with the moat and the honest hardest problem.

The Thesis Abstract Version

Text

This study addresses a persistent problem in tropical inland aquaculture: recurring mass mortality events driven by dissolved-oxygen depletion and post-overturn toxicity, whose precursor conditions develop at spatial and temporal resolutions far finer than conventional monitoring can observe. It proposes and specifies an autonomous aerial platform integrating a winch-deployed multiparameter sonde, calibrated multispectral imaging, and a metered dispensing system, feeding a fusion pipeline in which in-situ point profiles calibrate area-wide optical retrievals to drive a digital twin of the water body. The central contribution is an observation architecture in which the platform generates its own ground truth — addressing the calibration limitation repeatedly identified in the UAV water-quality literature. The study defines the concept, evidences the problem from published incident and statistical sources, specifies the system and its requirements, and pre-registers a validation methodology, while explicitly bounding the claims: no predictive capability is asserted absent seasonal validation, and the platform's limitations — weather-constrained availability, a benthic-species blind spot, and the affordability barrier for the most-affected smallholders — are stated as first-class findings.

Use
The thesis abstract. See thesis-outline.json for the full structure.
Caveat
Uses future/proposal framing ('proposes', 'specifies', 'pre-registers') because that is the honest tense at TRL 3. Do not shift to past-tense results language until results exist.

The Words Never To Use

Note

From brand-identity.json#/voice_and_tone. Guarded here because a pitch is where the temptation is strongest.

Banned
  • 'Prevents fish kills' — until validated, it's 'detects deteriorating conditions earlier'.
  • 'Detects toxins' — it detects pigments correlated with taxa that sometimes produce toxins.
  • 'Revolutionary / game-changing / disruptive' — say what it does.
  • 'Predicts' — until the model is validated, it 'reveals the mechanism'.
  • Any traction that doesn't exist.
