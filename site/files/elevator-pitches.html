Elevator Pitches

elevator-pitches v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

Note

The same argument at four lengths and for different audiences, rebuilt around the phased platform: free app first, premium software second, smart hardware third. All honest — none claims a shipped app, a measured conversion rate, a flown product, or a validated prediction.

The One Liner

Text

AquaVision is an aquaculture management platform built for every stage of a fish farmer's journey — free tools that get a beginner started, premium analytics and a marketplace once a farm is producing, and smart hardware for the operations that have outgrown manual tracking altogether.

Use
The first sentence to anyone.

The 15 Second

Text

Fish farming has almost no software built for it and almost no monitoring hardware anyone can afford, so most farmers run on notebooks and guesswork at every scale. AquaVision starts free: an AI farming assistant, water-quality logging, and photo-based disease identification that any beginner can install today. As a farm grows, a premium tier turns that logged history into predictive alerts and a marketplace. The farms that outgrow manual tracking entirely can add IoT sensors and an autonomous drone that measures what a phone camera can't. Free gets us the farmers; the data they generate is what makes the paid layers worth paying for.

Use
A hallway, an intro, a conference.

The 60 Second

Text

In 2022, aquaculture passed capture fisheries for the first time — most of the world's farmed fish now grow in ponds and cages someone owns, and almost none of it is digitally managed at any price point that fits the owner. We don't start by selling those owners a drone. We start with a free app: AI-guided farm setup, photo-based disease identification, water-quality logging, feeding schedules, and a farmer community — tools a smallholder needs and can actually afford, which is also the fastest way to build an engaged user base and real farm data. Once a farm has outgrown a notebook, a premium subscription turns that logged history into predictive health alerts, smart recommendations, a marketplace for feed and buyers, and paid expert consultations — this is where AquaVision becomes a real subscription business. And the operations that have outgrown even that — running more cages than a person can walk in a day — are the ones who add IoT water probes and the OmniDrone, an autonomous platform that profiles the water column at depth and feeds a live digital twin, priced against the aeration electricity it saves. It's land-and-expand: cheap acquisition, layered monetization, hardware sold as an upgrade into farms we already know rather than a cold sale to a stranger. We're pre-launch on the software and pre-seed on the hardware — nothing built, nothing flown — and we're raising to prove the two hardest things: that farmers will use and then pay for the software, and that the physical platform works at all.

Use
A real meeting. Note it ends on the honest ask, not a claim.

The Pitch For A Farmer

Text

You don't need to buy anything to start. Download the app and it helps you set up your pond, suggests what's probably wrong with a sick fish from a photo, and keeps the records you're probably already keeping on paper — for free. As your farm grows and you want to know what's coming instead of reacting to it, there's a paid tier with real predictions and a marketplace for feed and buyers. And if you ever get big enough that you can't walk every cage yourself, there's a drone that does the rounds you can't get to, measuring at the depths where trouble actually starts. You don't have to decide any of that today — just start with the free part and see if it's useful to you.

Use
A farmer, a cooperative meeting. Leads with zero cost and asks for no upfront trust.

The Pitch For An Impact Investor

Text

The smallholders who most need better information are the ones who could never afford the equipment that provides it, so we don't sell to them — we give them the app for free. That's not charity, it's the acquisition strategy: free tools reach thousands of small operators, build trust and a real usage base, and the same data that makes those farmers better-informed is what eventually funds the paid tiers and the hardware. It aligns with SDG 2, 14, and 12 — and we'll name the tension: a platform that scales on engagement can start optimizing for retention over the farmer's actual interest, and a 'free' product still has to make money from someone eventually, which is a pressure we have to manage honestly as we build the premium tier. We have zero users today, so every claim about reach is a plan, not a result, and we'd rather tell you that than show you a projection dressed up as traction.

Use
An impact fund. Leads with the free tier as the actual mechanism for equity, not a slogan.

The Pitch For A Technical Investor

Text

The technical bet has two layers. The near-term one is software: an AI assistant and a disease-identification model that has to be good enough on day one that a beginner with no track record trusts it over her own eyes — a cold-start ML problem before it's anything else, and it hasn't been built. The long-term one is hardware: a tethered multiparameter sonde on a powered winch, deployed from a hovering multirotor, generating its own calibration ground truth for aerial multispectral imagery in the same sortie — a genuinely hard mechanical and controls problem, and the reason a camera-drone competitor can't simply copy the Phase 3 offering. What makes the sequencing deliberate rather than convenient is that the software layers generate real farm-level water and production data before we ever build the hardware, so the eventual digital twin isn't calibrated cold. We're honest about where each layer actually is: the software is unbuilt, and the hardware is TRL 3 with nothing flown.

Use
An investor who will ask hard engineering questions on both software and hardware.

The Thesis Abstract Version

Text

This study addresses a persistent problem in tropical inland aquaculture: farm-level decisions are made with almost no digital observability, from smallholders who keep paper records to commercial operators whose water-quality monitoring cannot resolve the sub-surface, sub-diel conditions that precede recurring mass-mortality events. It situates its technical contribution within a phased software-to-hardware platform — a free farm-management application (Phase 1) and a premium analytics tier (Phase 2) intended to build an engaged user base and a longitudinal farm-data record — and undertakes a design-and-feasibility study of that platform's Phase 3 layer: an autonomous aerial system integrating a winch-deployed multiparameter sonde, calibrated multispectral imaging, and a metered dispensing system, feeding a fusion pipeline in which in-situ point profiles calibrate area-wide optical retrievals to drive a digital twin of the water body. The central engineering contribution is an observation architecture in which the platform generates its own ground truth, addressing the calibration limitation repeatedly identified in the UAV water-quality literature, while the phased business architecture is the reason that ground truth can eventually be gathered on farms the platform already knows rather than farms it is meeting for the first time. The study defines the concept, evidences the problem from published incident and statistical sources, specifies the Phase 3 system and its requirements, and pre-registers a validation methodology, while explicitly bounding the claims: no predictive capability is asserted absent seasonal validation, no free-to-paid conversion rate is asserted absent Phase 1 usage data, and the platform's limitations — weather-constrained hardware availability, an unproven software funnel, and an affordability barrier the free tier only partially resolves — are stated as first-class findings.

Use
The thesis abstract. See thesis-outline.json for the full structure.
Caveat
Uses future/proposal framing ('proposes', 'specifies', 'pre-registers') because that is the honest tense at TRL 3 for the hardware and pre-launch for the software. Do not shift to past-tense results or traction language until results exist for either layer.

The Words Never To Use

Note

From brand-identity.json#/voice_and_tone. Guarded here because a pitch is where the temptation is strongest.

Banned
  • 'Prevents fish kills' — until validated, it's 'detects deteriorating conditions earlier'.
  • 'Detects toxins' — it detects pigments correlated with taxa that sometimes produce toxins.
  • 'Diagnoses' disease from a photo — until validated, it 'suggests a likely cause, flagged with confidence, for a human to confirm'.
  • 'Revolutionary / game-changing / disruptive' — say what it does.
  • 'Predicts' — until the model is validated, it 'reveals the mechanism'.
  • 'Freemium flywheel' / 'network effects' — until conversion and retention are measured, say 'free tier' and 'unproven conversion assumption'.
  • Any user count, download count, subscriber count, or conversion rate that isn't measured.
  • Any traction that doesn't exist.
