OmniDock — Automated Docking Station

docking-station v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗

Platform Context

OmniDock is the shore-side infrastructure component of AquaVision Smart (Phase 3) — the premium hardware upgrade layer of the AquaVision platform for commercial and enterprise farms that have outgrown the app-based Phase 1 (free) and Phase 2 (subscription) tiers. It is not AquaVision's core product; it is the infrastructure that makes the Phase 3 hardware offering operable as an unattended service.

Why the Dock Is A Co Equal Product

Statement

Four of the system's five distinguishing claims depend on the dock, not the aircraft. It is treated in most drone pitches as an accessory. Here it is the thing that makes the product a product.

The Four Dependencies
ClaimDepends On
We measure the pre-dawn dissolved oxygen minimumAutonomous launch. A drone that needs a human to launch it will never fly at 04:30, which means it only ever measures the safest hour of the day — precisely the failure it was built to fix.
Our probe fouls less than a moored buoyThe dock rinse cycle. Without it we are just a slower buoy with worse uptime.
Our multispectral time series is comparable across daysThe calibrated reflectance panel living in the dock. Without it, every survey is a separate uncalibrated snapshot.
We operate across sites without spreading pathogensThe decontamination station.
The Fifth
Depth profiling is the only claim that belongs purely to the aircraft.
The Business Consequence

Coverage is measured in docks, not drones. The dock is the unit of network economics, the unit of capital deployment, and the thing a customer actually installs. Price and plan accordingly.

The Thermal Problem

Statement

A closed box in tropical sun exceeds the battery's safe storage temperature. This degrades pack life substantially and silently.

Why It Is Here

It is a dock requirement generated by an airframe constraint — exactly the cross-subsystem coupling that gets missed when subsystems are designed independently.

Mitigations
  • Passive ventilation with insect and vermin screening
  • White or reflective exterior
  • Shading structure
  • Active cooling if the arithmetic demands it, at a power cost the solar budget must carry
Note

Getting this wrong does not produce a dramatic failure. It produces batteries that quietly last half as long as the business model assumed, discovered a year in, in the operating cost line.

The Monsoon Problem

Sizing for average insolation is easy. Sizing for an extended overcast monsoon stretch is expensive — days of storage, or accept degraded cadence. And the degraded cadence lands exactly during the season of highest fish kill risk.

The Compounding Honesty

We cannot fly in the storm AND we cannot charge in the overcast. Two independent limitations pointing the same direction, during the season that matters most. A fixed buoy has neither problem. This is the strongest technical argument for the buoy hybrid that our own competitive matrix already pointed at, and it should be confronted rather than buried in a solar sizing appendix.

Dock Network Economics

The Unit

One dock covers the stations within the aircraft's operating radius. A large lake needs several.

The Implication

Deployment cost scales with docks, which scales with water body area and geometry — not with the number of cages or the customer's revenue. A big, sparse lake is expensive to cover and generates the same revenue per cage as a small dense one.

The Strategic Consequence

Dense cage clusters are dramatically better unit economics than dispersed ones. This should be a hard sales qualification criterion, and it independently reinforces the lake-cage beachhead choice over dispersed pond geography. The geography chooses the customer.

The Cooperative Synergy

A dock serving forty co-op members' cages around one bay is the best economics available to this business. A dock serving one farm is the worst. This is another reason the cooperative channel matters beyond the affordability argument.
