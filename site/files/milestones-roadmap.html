Development Roadmap & Milestones

milestones-roadmap v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ↗Projection ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗

Honest Framing

This is a plan, not a forecast. It reads faster for software than for hardware, and that is the whole point of sequencing Phase 1 and Phase 2 before Phase 3 — but faster to build is not the same as certain to work. The free app has zero users today, and nothing about writing code guarantees a beginner farmer opens it twice; that is an unproven behavioural assumption, not a technical one. Phase 3 hardware timelines slip on top of that, and aquaculture hardware timelines slip more than most, because the validation season is set by the weather and the fish, not the sprint board. Durations are stated as ranges and the gates are stated as evidence, not as dates. An investor should read the gates, not the calendar, at every phase.

Phase Overview

Note

The spine of this roadmap is the three product phases, not the six engineering stages of the earlier hardware-only plan. Phase 3 still contains that full hardware development sequence — TRL 2 through 8 — nested below, because that engineering rigor did not stop being true when the narrative changed. It is simply no longer the first thing that has to work.

Summary
PhaseIn One Line
Phase 1 — FoundationShip a free app, get beginner and smallholder farmers actually logging their ponds.
Phase 2 — MonetizationTurn logged data into a premium tier, a marketplace, and paid expert access that farmers who outgrow the free tier will pay for.
Phase 3 — Smart AquacultureSell IoT sensors and the OmniDrone as an automated-monitoring upgrade to commercial and enterprise farms, with PondTwin as the interface.

Trl Note

TRL is a hardware-maturity scale and is used elsewhere in this roadmap only for the Phase 3 hardware layer. Phase 1 has no equivalent formal scale; its honest status is 'specified, not built' — see company-profile.json#the_honest_position.

Objective

Ship a free app that a first-time farmer can use unaided, and begin accumulating the water-quality and stock data every later phase depends on. This phase is the acquisition and data-accumulation engine for the whole company, not a loss-leader to tolerate.

Workstreams
  • Define and build the MVP feature set: guided farm setup, an AI farming assistant, manual and photo-based water quality logging, feeding schedules, photo-based disease identification, community discussions, learning modules, and record keeping.
  • Design for offline-tolerant use on low-end Android devices and metered mobile data — the same 'design for the median farm, not the flagship' discipline the old hardware roadmap applied to the drone, applied here to the app.
  • Recruit and onboard the first cohorts of beginner and smallholder farmers through cooperatives, extension networks, and fish-farming community groups: organic, content- and community-led growth, not paid acquisition.
  • Instrument activation, retention, and logging-completion analytics from day one — Phase 2's models are only as good as what actually gets logged here.
  • Usability research on the onboarding flow itself, with farmers who have never used a farm-management app before, not just on the value proposition.
  • Content and learning-module production in the languages and literacy levels the beachhead farmers actually use.
Exit Gate
Criteria
  • App live in production (Android and web) and usable by a first-time beginner farmer without support intervention.
  • A repeat-logging cohort established in the beachhead geography — order of magnitude in the hundreds to low thousands of active farmers is the illustrative planning target, not a measured or committed number (provenance: design_target; see traction.json, which stays honestly empty until this is real).
  • A minimum logging-completion and week-over-week retention rate sustained across a defined observation window, so Phase 2 has enough structured, repeated data to model against rather than a single data point per farm.
  • An organic acquisition channel identified and repeatable, i.e. a CAC low enough that the free tier functions as a funnel rather than a subsidy with no path to return.
Kill Criteria
  • If beginner farmers cannot complete basic onboarding without support calls, the product has not solved the problem it claims to, and the UX must be re-architected before any acquisition spend follows it.
  • If farmers log data once and never return, the app is a form, not a habit, and the entire Phase 2 data assumption collapses — this must be caught and fixed here, not discovered after building predictive models on thin, non-repeating data.
Status
·In Progress
Status Note

Only product definition and this repository's specification work is in progress. No code has been written and no farmer has used the app — see company-profile.json#the_honest_position.

Trl Note

As with Phase 1, TRL does not apply. The equivalent honest status here is whether a single farmer has ever paid AquaVision for anything, which as of this revision is 'no'.

Objective

Convert the farmers Phase 1 acquired — once they have outgrown manual tracking or want more than the free tier gives them — into subscribers, marketplace participants, and consultation buyers, without breaking the trust the free tier built.

Workstreams
  • Train the first predictive health monitoring and smart-recommendation models on the water-quality and stock data logged in Phase 1 — the free tier is the training set, not just the funnel.
  • Design and build the premium analytics tier and the free-to-paid conversion funnel, instrumented so the conversion rate is measured rather than assumed.
  • Build the marketplace connecting farmers to feed, equipment, and buyers, including whatever take-rate and payments infrastructure that requires.
  • Recruit and vet aquaculturists and veterinarians for a paid expert-consultation feature, and define the liability and scope boundaries around advice given through the app.
  • Pricing research against farmers' actual willingness to pay, not against feature-list comparisons to competitors.
  • Churn and retention instrumentation for paying subscribers, from the first subscriber onward.
Exit Gate
Criteria
  • At least one validated, trained predictive model (health monitoring or smart recommendations) evaluated against held-out real farmer data, with an honest accuracy/precision report, not a demo cherry-pick.
  • A working conversion funnel from free to paid with a measured conversion rate, even if that rate is initially low.
  • First real paying subscribers and/or marketplace transactions (provenance for any stated figures: illustrative_synthetic until real, per traction.json's empty-until-earned policy).
  • Expert consultation feature live with a defined scope of advice and a named liability/escalation policy, reviewed against the disease-identification and biosecurity constraints already established for the platform.
Kill Criteria
  • If farmers who actively use the free tier will not pay for the premium tier at any tested price point, the monetization thesis for that segment is broken and must be reformulated — more feature-building will not fix a willingness-to-pay problem.
  • If the marketplace or consultation features erode trust in the free tier's advice (e.g. recommendations reading as sales pitches), the conversion funnel is cannibalising the acquisition engine and must be redesigned before scaling either.
Status
·Not Started

Objective

Extend the platform into automated, continuous monitoring for commercial and enterprise farms that have outgrown Phase 1/2 manual and semi-manual logging: IoT water probes, the OmniDrone, PondTwin digital-twin visualisation, and an enterprise dashboard. Sold as a premium hardware upgrade layer on top of the software platform, not as the company's first product. Everything in this phase is the same engineering programme the company's original hardware-first plan described in full; only its position in the sequence and its framing as an add-on, rather than the core offering, has changed.

Objective

Convert the OmniDrone/IoT concept into a defensible, evidenced specification and prove the science is sound before spending money on hardware.

Workstreams
  • Literature review and research corpus (this repository).
  • Requirements baseline with traceability to stakeholder needs.
  • Mass, power, and payload budget — the go/no-go arithmetic on whether the vehicle can exist.
  • Discovery interviews with the commercial and enterprise farms Phase 3 targets, distinct from the Phase 1 beginner-farmer interviews. Target: 25+ operators across at least three lake or pond systems.
  • Regulatory pathway map with CAAP and BFAR.
  • Academic partner MOU for validation.
Exit Gate
Criteria
Criteria (expand)
  • Payload budget closes with >= 20% margin, or the concept is re-architected. If a quadrotor cannot carry probe plus winch plus imaging plus dispensing on a useful endurance, that must be discovered NOW, on paper, not after tooling.
  • At least 25 commercial/enterprise operator interviews completed with a written synthesis, including at least five who say they would not buy it and why.
  • Named academic partner with a signed validation protocol.
  • Regulatory pathway documented, including the honest answer on BVLOS timeline.
Kill Criteria
Kill Criteria (expand)
  • If the payload budget cannot close on any credible multirotor configuration, stop and reconsider the platform — a tethered buoy network or an uncrewed surface vessel may be the correct answer, and the team must be willing to hear that.
  • If operator discovery reveals that farms will not pay for a hardware upgrade on top of the Phase 2 premium tier they already use, the Phase 3 value proposition is broken and must be reformulated before hardware.

Objective

De-risk the two hardest subsystems independently, before integration hides which one is failing.

Workstreams
  • Winch rig on a static gantry over water: 1,000 deploy/recover cycles, wind-simulated, with fouling introduced deliberately.
  • Sensor payload bench: probe accuracy against laboratory reference; multispectral radiometric calibration workflow.
  • Dispensing mechanism: dose accuracy and repeatability across granular and liquid loads.
  • Airframe hover testbed with mass simulators.
  • Data pipeline and telemetry schema against synthetic feeds, built to plug into the same platform data model Phase 1/2 already established.
Exit Gate
Criteria
Criteria (expand)
  • Winch achieves target cycle reliability with a documented failure analysis on every jam.
  • Probe agrees with laboratory reference within stated accuracy across the full range, including at the ugly end — high turbidity, low DO.
  • Multispectral calibration workflow produces reproducible reflectance across two different days and illumination conditions. If it cannot, the imaging value proposition does not exist yet.
  • Dose accuracy within specification.

  • Endurance meets the mission profile with reserve.
  • Zero uncommanded discharge events from the dispensing system.

Field Validation & Model Training

Stage Id
p3-3
Duration Months
Min
8
Max
14
Trl Entry
5
Trl Exit
6
Objective

Earn the right to make predictive claims from hardware, the same way Phase 2 had to earn the right to make them from logged app data. This is the longest stage and the one founders always underestimate, because you cannot rush a season.

Workstreams
  • Instrumented longitudinal deployment at the pilot site, minimum one full annual cycle to capture both the dry-season thermal stress window and the monsoon overturn window.
  • Paired reference sampling for every drone profile — the training set is only as good as its ground truth.
  • NDCI-to-Chl-a local regression, per site.
  • Anomaly detection model training on real, labelled surface imagery.
  • Kill-risk model development — and honest evaluation, including on the events it misses.
  • Digital twin (PondTwin) calibration against observed pond behaviour.
Exit Gate
Criteria
Criteria (expand)
  • A validation dataset spanning at least one full seasonal cycle, published or at minimum peer-reviewed by the academic partner.
  • Anomaly detection meets target precision and recall on a held-out site — not a held-out split of the same site, which would prove nothing about generalisation.
  • Kill-risk model evaluated against real events with an honest confusion matrix. If it cannot beat the farmer's existing intuition, say so and do not ship it.
  • At least one documented case where the system produced a warning that led to an intervention that a farmer judged useful.
Note

The last criterion is the real one. Everything else is instrumentation.

Critical Path

Note

The things that will actually determine whether this works, ranked. Phase 1 and Phase 2 now rank above the hardware risks, because nothing in Phase 3 matters commercially if the free app never builds a user base or that base never converts. Everything else is schedulable.

Items
Rank
1
Item
Phase 1 activation and repeat-logging retention
Why

If beginner farmers do not open the free app more than once, there is no acquisition engine, no accumulated data, and no funnel into Phase 2 — the entire phased thesis depends on this being true before anything else is built on top of it.

Rank
2
Item
Phase 2 free-to-paid conversion
Why

The monetization story assumes farmers who log data in the free tier will pay for premium analytics, the marketplace, or consultations. This is a standard and often-fatal freemium risk, and it is untested here.

Rank
3
Item
Payload budget closure (Phase 3)
Why

If the OmniDrone cannot carry the mission, nothing downstream in Phase 3 matters. This is a paper exercise available today — do it early, but only once Phase 1/2 have earned the right to fund it.

Rank
4
Item
Winch reliability over water (Phase 3)
Why
The novel mechanism. Highest technical risk with the least prior art to borrow from.
Rank
5
Item
One full seasonal validation cycle (Phase 3)
Why

Irreducible. You cannot buy your way past a year of weather, and every predictive hardware claim depends on it.

Rank
6
Item
BVLOS authorisation (Phase 3)
Why

Long lead, external dependency, gates the hardware margin. Start the conversation early, not at the commercial-pilot stage.

Rank
7
Item
Proof a farmer acts on a warning, at every phase
Why

The entire thesis, software and hardware alike, assumes information changes behaviour. If a warning arrives — from the free app's AI assistant, a premium alert, or an OmniDrone sortie — and the farmer has no affordable response available, the product informs people about their own losses in real time, which is not a business.
