Problem Taxonomy — The Full Space of Aquaculture & Fisheries Problems

problem-taxonomy v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 raw JSON ↗

Purpose

An exhaustive map of problems in the field, classified by whether the OmniDrone addresses them. The classification is the point: a technology repository that lists only the problems its product solves is a brochure. This file exists to show the committee and the investor that the team knows the boundaries of its own solution.

Coverage Legend

Core
The OmniDrone directly and substantially addresses this. This is what we are for.
Partial

The OmniDrone contributes meaningfully but does not solve it. Honest framing required in any claim.

Adjacent
The platform generates data relevant to this, but solving it needs other actors or tools.
Out of Scope

Real problem in the field. We do not touch it. Listed so nobody thinks we forgot, and so we never drift into claiming it.

Water Quality & Environmental Dynamics

Domain Id
water-quality
Problems

Dissolved oxygen depletion

Id
wq-do-depletion
Description

Respiration, decomposition, and algal die-off drive DO below survival thresholds, typically at night.

Coverage
core
Mechanism
Vertical DO profiling at multiple stations, several times daily including pre-dawn.
Citations
igs-do-aquaculture-2026 boyd-water-quality-2020

Thermal and saline stratification

Id
wq-stratification
Description
Density layering isolates the hypolimnion, which goes anoxic and accumulates toxicants.
Coverage
core
Mechanism
Temperature and salinity profiles yield thermocline depth and Schmidt stability.

Water column overturn / upwelling

Id
wq-overturn
Description

Sudden mixing delivers anoxic, sulfide- and ammonia-rich bottom water to the stock. The classic catastrophic kill mechanism.

Coverage
core
Mechanism
Stratification index plus oxygen debt index plus weather forecast integration.
Citations
seafdec-laguna-fishkill

Ammonia toxicity

Id
wq-ammonia
Description
Un-ionised ammonia crosses the gill; toxicity rises steeply with pH and temperature.
Coverage
core
Mechanism
Simultaneous TAN, pH, and temperature at depth, with NH3 computed rather than assumed.

Hydrogen sulfide toxicity

Id
wq-h2s
Description
Sediment-generated H2S, lethal at low concentration.
Coverage
partial
Mechanism

Inferred from near-bottom ORP and anoxia rather than measured directly. Direct H2S sensing on a flying probe is not currently practical — say so.

Honest Note
We infer. We do not measure. The distinction matters.

Nitrite toxicity / brown blood disease

Id
wq-nitrite
Coverage
partial
Mechanism

ISE nitrite where fitted; sensor drift makes this the least trustworthy channel on the probe.

Diurnal pH swings and low alkalinity

Id
wq-ph-swing
Coverage
core
Mechanism
High-frequency pH profiling reveals swing amplitude, which fortnightly sampling cannot.

Thermal stress and heat waves

Id
wq-temperature-stress
Description

Extreme heat raises metabolic oxygen demand while lowering oxygen solubility — the two curves move against each other, which is why heat events kill.

Coverage
core
Mechanism
Profiling plus thermal imaging.
Citations
khaleej-heat-fishkill-ph

Turbidity and suspended solids

Id

Coverage

Mechanism

Eutrophication of the receiving water body

Id
wq-eutrophication
Coverage
partial
Mechanism

Measures the symptoms and the loading proxies; cannot by itself change the stocking decisions that cause it.

Citations

Id

Coverage
partial
Mechanism

Interpretation

Roughly a third of the field's problems are squarely ours, a third we touch, and a third we do not. That is a normal and healthy profile for a platform product. A repository claiming 'core' on sixty out of sixty-two problems would be describing a fantasy, and any experienced reviewer would discount the whole document on sight.

How To Use This File

  • In a pitch: filter to `core` and lead with those. Then show the `out_of_scope` list unprompted. Volunteering your boundaries is the single cheapest credibility purchase available in a pitch meeting.
  • In a thesis: this is the scope delimitation chapter. The `coverage` field is your inclusion/exclusion criterion and the `boundary` and `honest_note` fields are your limitations section.
  • In engineering: `core` problems generate requirements. `partial` problems generate careful language. `out_of_scope` problems generate integration partners.
