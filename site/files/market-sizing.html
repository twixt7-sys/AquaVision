Market Sizing — TAM / SAM / SOM

market-sizing v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ↗Projection ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗
ℹREAD THIS FIRST
Statement:

This file contains NO market size number, and that is deliberate.

Why:

A defensible bottom-up market size requires the number of aquaculture operations in the target geography, their size distribution, and their existing spend on monitoring and inputs. We have none of those. The BFAR Philippine Fisheries Profile has them; we have not obtained it.

What We Could Do Instead:

We could take FAO's USD 312.8 billion global aquaculture output, apply an invented 'technology penetration rate' of 2 percent, and present a USD 6.3 billion TAM. It would look professional. It would be meaningless, because the 2 percent would be pulled from nothing and the whole number would inherit its arbitrariness. Every experienced investor has seen that exact slide a hundred times and discounts it to zero on sight.

What This File Does Instead:

It gives the METHOD, the inputs required, and the sensitivity structure — so that the moment real data arrives, the number can be computed honestly and defended under questioning. A founder who says 'here is my method and here is the one input I am missing' is more credible than one who presents a confident fabrication.

When To Fill This In:

Phase 0, after obtaining BFAR statistics and completing farmer discovery. Not before.

Sizing Methodology

Chosen Approach
Bottom-up, from operator counts and per-operator willingness to pay.
Why Not Top Down

Top-down sizing from industry output value requires a penetration assumption that carries the entire result. The number becomes a restatement of the assumption dressed as research. Bottom-up sizing can be wrong, but it can be checked — and a number that can be checked is worth more than a bigger number that cannot.

Formula
TAM

SUM over all aquaculture operations globally of (annual revenue per operation from monitoring + dispensing services)

SAM

SUM over operations in reachable geographies, in culture systems the platform physically suits (open-water pond, lake cage, coastal pen — NOT RAS, NOT deep offshore), that are above the minimum scale threshold

SOM

SAM x realistic share within the planning horizon, constrained by dock network coverage, service capacity, and regulatory permission

Market Definition

What Is In
SegmentRationale
Lake and reservoir cage cultureThe core case. Stratification-driven risk, spatially distributed units, a shared water body, and no practical alternative monitoring method.
Open brackish and freshwater pond cultureLarge area, distributed units, real monitoring need. The largest segment by count.
Coastal pen and near-shore marine cagePhysically viable for the platform; harder environment; later phase.
What Is Out
SegmentRationale
Recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS)Indoor, already instrumented, and fixed sensors are strictly better than an aerial platform in a tank. We would lose, correctly.
Deep offshore cageBeyond the endurance and sea-state envelope of a small multirotor. This is a vessel or fixed-platform problem.
Single small ponds below the scale thresholdA fixed buoy is cheaper and better. Selling into this segment would be extracting money for a worse solution, which is both wrong and unsustainable.
Capture fisheriesDifferent problem, different customer, and — for the surveillance applications — a different company than the one we want to be.
Why the Exclusions Matter

Roughly half of what a lazy sizing would count is genuinely not our market. Stating the exclusions up front is the difference between a credible SAM and an inflated one. It also protects the sales team from wasting a year on prospects that will never close.

Scale Threshold Analysis

Provenance
?Assumption
The Question
Below what operation size does a fixed sensor buoy beat the OmniDrone?
Why It Decides Everything

This threshold sets the SAM. Set it too low and the sizing is fantasy; set it correctly and it is defensible.

The Logic
Buoy Economics

A fixed buoy covers one point at one or few depths, at a low capital cost, forever, with no labour. For a single small pond, this is close to unbeatable and we should say so.

Omnidrone Economics

One drone covers N stations x D depths x F frequency, but carries a much higher capital and operating cost. Its cost per observation falls as N, D, and F rise.

The Crossover

Cost_per_observation_buoy x (N x D) vs Cost_per_observation_drone. The drone wins when the number of distinct observation points needed is large — which is precisely the lake cage array case and precisely not the single-pond case.

The Asymmetry That Favours Us

A buoy cannot profile a water column without a vertical string of sensors, which multiplies its cost by D. The drone gets depth almost for free — the winch is already there, and paying out another two metres costs seconds. Depth is where our economics break in our favour, and it is not a coincidence that depth is also where the killing happens.

Estimated Threshold
unknown — must be computed once real costs and real farm layouts are known
Honest Note
This analysis is the most useful thing in this file and it is currently qualitative. Making it quantitative should be a Phase 0 deliverable. It determines the SAM, the pricing, and the sales qualification criteria all at once.

Beachhead Definition

Segment

Philippine lake cage culture operators, tilapia and milkfish, at cooperative or commercial scale

Why This One
  • The failure mode the platform is built to see (stratification-driven kills) is documented, recurring, and expensive there.
  • Operators are geographically concentrated around a small number of lakes, which makes a dock-based service network viable — a decisive operational advantage over dispersed pond geographies.
  • Cage arrays produce exactly the high N x D x F profile where our economics beat a buoy.
  • Existing cooperatives provide an aggregation layer that solves the smallholder affordability problem without requiring us to solve it.
  • Local knowledge of Taal's volcanic sulfur chemistry is defensible against imported generic products.

Market Timing

Why Now
  • Aquaculture passed capture fisheries in 2022 — the instrumentable share of the sector is now the majority.
  • UAV inland-water retrieval literature matured through 2024-2025, so the science is borrowable rather than inventable.
  • Edge inference at a few watts became practical.
  • Digital twin aquaculture moved from proposal to demonstrated case study.

Why Not Now
Note

The honest counter-case, included because a founder who cannot argue the opposite side has not thought about it.

Arguments
  • BVLOS is not authorised, and the unit economics depend on it. We may be two years early to our own business model.
  • Smallholder willingness to pay may simply not exist at any price we can offer, in which case the market waits for a public programme rather than a startup.
  • Battery energy density remains the binding constraint on multirotor endurance and is improving slowly. The mission profile may be marginal today and comfortable in three years.
