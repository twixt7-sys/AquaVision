Market Sizing — TAM / SAM / SOM (Phased: Software Platform + Hardware Upgrade Layer)

market-sizing v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ↗Projection ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗
ℹREAD THIS FIRST
Statement:

This file contains NO market size number, and that is deliberate. It now sizes TWO layered opportunities instead of one, because the company itself now has two layers.

Why:

A defensible bottom-up market size requires the number of aquaculture operations in the target geography, their size distribution, and their existing spend (on inputs, on monitoring, and now on farm-management software or its substitutes — notebooks, memory, Facebook groups). We have none of those counts. The BFAR Philippine Fisheries Profile has them; we have not obtained it.

What Changed Since V1:

The company used to be a hardware/monitoring-service business, and this file sized only that. It is now a phased platform: a free-or-cheap software layer (Phase 1) that most fish farmers of any size can adopt with a smartphone and no capital, a premium subscription/marketplace layer (Phase 2) monetising the farms that outgrow manual tracking, and a hardware upgrade layer (Phase 3 — the original OmniDrone/IoT/PondTwin business) sold to commercial and enterprise operators who need automated monitoring. Each layer has a different addressable population, a different sales motion, and a different set of missing inputs. Collapsing them into one TAM would hide the fact that the software layer is much larger and much lower-friction than the hardware layer — that asymmetry is the whole reason for the pivot, and this file should make it legible rather than smoothing it over.

What We Could Do Instead:

We could take FAO's USD 312.8 billion global aquaculture output, apply an invented 'technology penetration rate,' and present a confident TAM for either layer. It would look professional. It would be meaningless, because the penetration rate would be pulled from nothing and the whole number would inherit its arbitrariness. This applies just as much to a 'percent of farmers who will use a free app' assumption as it did to the old 'percent who will buy a drone service' assumption — a fabricated conversion funnel is a fabricated market size wearing a SaaS costume.

What This File Does Instead:

It gives the METHOD, the inputs required, and the sensitivity structure for both layers — so that the moment real data arrives (farmer discovery interviews, a BFAR operator count, or an actual Phase 1 cohort with measured activation and retention), the numbers can be computed honestly and defended under questioning.

When To Fill This In:

The software layer (Phase 1/2) can be partially estimated sooner than the hardware layer, because it does not require BFAR data to launch a pilot — a founder can start collecting real activation, retention, and conversion numbers from an actual free-tier cohort well before BVLOS or lake-cage counts are resolved. That is one of the structural advantages of leading with software: the sizing inputs become obtainable through the product itself, not only through external records requests. The hardware layer (Phase 3) remains blocked on BFAR/LLDA data and on farmer discovery exactly as before.

The Layering Logic

?Assumption
Statement

The three phases are not three unrelated markets. They are three progressively narrower filters over the same underlying population of aquaculture operators, and the whole point of sequencing them this way is that each filter is easier to pass than the one before it.

Who Qualifies

Anyone farming fish or shrimp who owns or can access a smartphone. No capital threshold, no minimum scale, no regulatory gate.

Friction

Lowest. This is the layer the old hardware-first plan could never reach on its own economics.

Layer
Phase 3 — IoT sensors / OmniDrone / PondTwin / enterprise dashboard
Who Qualifies

Commercial or enterprise operators at the scale, capital, and response-capacity threshold analysed in the hardware section below (unchanged from the original sizing logic).

Friction

Highest — exactly as demanding as the original hardware-first plan, because this is that plan, now arriving third instead of first.

Why This Matters For Sizing

The Phase 1 population is necessarily a superset of the Phase 3 population — every hardware customer was, or could have been, a free-tier app user first. This means the software TAM is not a separate business from the hardware TAM; it is the outer ring of the same target, and the hardware SAM/SOM computed in the section below should in the mature state be read as a sub-segment of the software SAM, not as an independent figure. We keep them as separate sections in this file because the missing inputs, the unit economics, and the sales motion differ enough that merging them prematurely would produce a single number nobody could defend.

The Honest Caveat

This funnel logic is a plausible structure, not a measured one. No data yet exists on what fraction of Phase 1 free-tier users would ever reach the Phase 2 threshold, let alone the Phase 3 one. Treat the funnel as a hypothesis to falsify with real cohort data, the same way the personas in customer-personas.json are hypotheses rather than research.

Layer 1 Software Market

Label

Phase 1 / Phase 2 — Aquaculture Farm-Management Software (App, Premium Analytics, Marketplace, Consultations)

Why Size This Separately

This is now the primary go-to-market layer and, on structural grounds, the larger and lower-friction of the two markets: it requires no capital outlay from the farmer, no airspace regulator, no dock network, and no minimum operation size. A farmer with one backyard pond and a smartphone is inside this market on day one; the same farmer is outside the hardware market until they can afford aeration and outgrow manual logging.

Sizing Methodology
Chosen Approach

Bottom-up, from the count of fish/shrimp farming individuals and operations with smartphone or basic internet access, filtered by realistic activation and monetisation rates benchmarked against comparable agtech and farm-management software — once those benchmarks are actually gathered.

Why Not Top Down

The same objection as for the hardware layer applies here, and arguably applies harder, because 'percent of farmers who would install a free app' is an even easier number to fabricate persuasively than 'percent who would buy a drone service.' A believable-sounding conversion funnel is still a fabrication if none of its rates have been measured.

Formula
TAM

Global count of individuals and enterprises engaged in aquaculture farming x share reachable with a smartphone/mobile-internet-based product

SAM

TAM filtered to geographies AquaVision can realistically reach with a Filipino/Southeast-Asian-first product (language, species relevance, regulatory simplicity for software) x share who are actively farming (not capture fisheries, not purely academic/hobbyist)

SOM Phase1

SAM x realistic free-tier activation rate within the planning horizon, constrained by community/organic distribution capacity and content localisation effort

SOM Phase2

SOM_phase1 x realistic free-to-paid conversion rate, constrained by the premium value proposition actually landing (predictive health, marketplace, consultations) and by the operator having a budget line to convert

Why the Exclusions Matter

The connectivity ceiling is the software layer's equivalent of the hardware layer's scale threshold: it is the honest boundary that keeps the SAM credible rather than inflated to 'everyone who farms fish.'

Beachhead Definition
Segment

Filipino smallholder and first-time fish/shrimp farmers active in existing online farming communities (Facebook groups, cooperative messaging channels), plus commercial operators willing to pilot the premium tier.

Why This One
  • Matches the existing hardware beachhead geography (Philippines), so learnings, language, and species content are reused rather than duplicated.
  • Organic/community-led distribution through existing farmer groups is plausibly low-CAC — though this is unmeasured and listed above as a blocking required input.
  • First-time farmers are, by definition, actively searching for guidance right now, which is the moment a free educational/AI-assistant product has the most obvious value — an easier activation moment than trying to displace an established workflow.
Beachhead Sizing Status

NOT COMPUTED — blocked on the same BFAR operator counts as the hardware beachhead, plus the smartphone-penetration and comparable-app-benchmark inputs above. Unlike the hardware beachhead, a rough order-of-magnitude estimate could be attempted sooner using publicly available Philippine smartphone penetration statistics and a conservative activation assumption — but doing so before that data is gathered would repeat exactly the fabrication this file exists to avoid.

Market Timing
Why Now
  • Aquaculture passed capture fisheries in 2022, and inland/smallholder-dense systems are the majority — the underlying population of potential app users grows structurally.
  • Smartphone and mobile-data costs have fallen enough across Southeast Asia to make a mobile-first product plausible for many smallholders, though the exact penetration rate for this specific population is unverified (see required_inputs above).
  • Freemium and community-led distribution playbooks are well established in adjacent developing-market agtech, which is borrowable methodology even before we have our own comparable benchmarks.

Scale Threshold Analysis
Provenance
?Assumption
The Question

Below what operation size does a fixed sensor buoy beat the OmniDrone — and, one level up, below what operation size does a Phase 2 software subscription remain the ceiling rather than a stepping stone to Phase 3 hardware?

Why It Decides Everything

This threshold sets the hardware SAM, and it now also sets the boundary between 'permanent Phase 2 customer' and 'Phase 3 upsell candidate.'

The Logic
Buoy Economics

A fixed buoy covers one point at one or few depths, at a low capital cost, forever, with no labour. For a single small pond, this is close to unbeatable and we should say so.

Omnidrone Economics

One drone covers N stations x D depths x F frequency, but carries a much higher capital and operating cost. Its cost per observation falls as N, D, and F rise.

The Crossover

Cost_per_observation_buoy x (N x D) vs Cost_per_observation_drone. The drone wins when the number of distinct observation points needed is large — which is precisely the lake cage array case and precisely not the single-pond case.

The Asymmetry That Favours Us

A buoy cannot profile a water column without a vertical string of sensors, which multiplies its cost by D. The drone gets depth almost for free — the winch is already there, and paying out another two metres costs seconds. Depth is where our economics break in our favour, and it is not a coincidence that depth is also where the killing happens.

Estimated Threshold
unknown — must be computed once real costs and real farm layouts are known
Honest Note
This analysis is the most useful thing in this section and it is currently qualitative. Making it quantitative should be a Phase 0 deliverable. It determines the hardware SAM, the pricing, and the sales qualification criteria all at once — and now it also determines which Phase 2 subscribers are worth a Phase 3 sales conversation and which are not.
Beachhead Definition
Segment

Philippine lake cage culture operators, tilapia and milkfish, at cooperative or commercial scale

Why This One
  • The failure mode the platform is built to see (stratification-driven kills) is documented, recurring, and expensive there.
  • Operators are geographically concentrated around a small number of lakes, which makes a dock-based service network viable — a decisive operational advantage over dispersed pond geographies.
  • Cage arrays produce exactly the high N x D x F profile where our economics beat a buoy.
  • Existing cooperatives provide an aggregation layer that solves the smallholder affordability problem without requiring us to solve it.
  • Local knowledge of Taal's volcanic sulfur chemistry is defensible against imported generic products.
  • Under the phased model, this beachhead is now reached warm rather than cold — ideally via operators who are already Phase 1/2 software users, which should lower acquisition cost and de-risk the sale relative to a pure cold hardware pitch. This is a hoped-for benefit of sequencing, not yet a demonstrated one.

Arguments
Arguments (expand)
