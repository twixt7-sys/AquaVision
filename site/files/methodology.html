Research Methodology

methodology v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

Purpose

How the study's claims will be tested. Written before the data exists, so that the evaluation criteria are fixed before anyone has an incentive to move them.

Why This File Is Written Now

Pre-registering the method is the difference between a study and a rationalisation. Once profiles start arriving, there will be enormous pressure to find the analysis that makes the product look good. This file exists to make that harder.

Research Questions

rq1

Question

Can an aerial platform produce depth-resolved water quality profiles that agree with a boat-deployed reference sonde within a defined tolerance?

Type
feasibility
Why It Is First

If the answer is no, nothing else matters. This is the moment the core claim is either true or it is not.

Hypothesis

H1: OmniDrone profiles will agree with a reference sonde within the stated per-channel accuracy across the full operating range, including high-turbidity and low-DO conditions.

Null

H0: No agreement within tolerance, or agreement that degrades systematically in the conditions of interest.

The Trap To Avoid

Testing only in clean, calm water. A sensor that is accurate in a clear tank and wrong in a eutrophic pond has failed, because the eutrophic pond is the entire use case. The ugly end of the range is the test that matters.

rq2

Question

Can in-situ chlorophyll measurements taken during the same sortie calibrate a UAV multispectral retrieval into concentration estimates with usable uncertainty?

Type
core_contribution
Why It Matters
This is the study's novel claim — the platform carrying its own ground truth.
Hypothesis

H2: A site-and-day-local regression of NDCI against same-sortie in-situ chlorophyll will produce concentration estimates whose uncertainty is small enough to support an operational decision.

The Honest Expectation

With 3-4 probe stations per sortie, the regression will often be poorly constrained. The interesting question is not whether it works sometimes; it is HOW MANY stations are needed before the uncertainty becomes useful. That is the publishable finding.

Secondary Question

Does a global (multi-site, multi-day) regression perform materially worse than the local one? The literature says it should. Demonstrating it on our own data would be a genuine contribution and would justify the architecture.

rq3

Question

Does depth-resolved, high-cadence observation reveal precursor conditions to mass mortality that surface sampling does not?

Type
core_contribution
Hypothesis

H3: Stratification strength and hypolimnetic oxygen debt will show measurable divergence from baseline in the days preceding mortality events, and this divergence will not be detectable in contemporaneous surface measurements.

The Design Requirement

This REQUIRES a paired surface-only measurement series to compare against. Otherwise we can show that we saw something, but not that the incumbent method would have missed it — and the second claim is the one that matters. Collect both from day one.

The Rare Event Problem

Mass kills happen a few times per year per lake. A single season may yield zero events, in which case this question is unanswerable and the study must say so rather than manufacture a positive finding from near-misses.

rq4

Question

Is chronic sub-lethal water quality variation associated with realised FCR and survival, on farms with no mortality events?

Type
the_sleeper
Why It May Be the Most Important Question

If the answer is yes and the effect is large, the entire value proposition is misstated. We are pitching catastrophe insurance when the product may be continuous yield optimisation — a better business and a far more tractable analysis, because every cycle is a sample rather than every kill.

Hypothesis

H4: Cumulative exposure to sub-optimal DO correlates with degraded FCR and survival across a production cycle, independent of mortality events.

Why It Is Methodologically Easier

Continuous outcome, abundant data, no rare-event problem. It is the one question in this list that a single season can actually answer with statistical power.

The Recommendation

This question is currently ranked fourth and is probably the one to run hardest. It is the cheapest to answer and the most likely to change the company.

rq5

Question

Do operators given this information change their behaviour, and does the change improve outcomes?

Type
the_load_bearing_question
Why It Is the Real One

Everything above is instrumentation. This is the question of whether the venture has a reason to exist.

Hypothesis

H5: Operators receiving depth-resolved alerts will take earlier intervention actions (aeration, feeding deferral, destocking) than a comparison group, and will achieve better survival and FCR.

The Brutal Methodological Problem

This needs a control group, and a control group means deliberately withholding a warning from a farm that might lose its stock. That is ethically unacceptable and it is not negotiable. See the ethics section below.

Study Design

Overall

Mixed-methods, sequential. Engineering validation first (RQ1-2), then observational field study (RQ3-4), then quasi-experimental adoption study (RQ5).

Phases

Instrument validation

Maps To
  • rq1
Design

Paired comparison. Every drone profile is matched with a boat-deployed reference sonde profile at the same station within a short time window.

Analysis
Bland-Altman agreement analysis per channel, not correlation.
Why Bland Altman Not Correlation

A high correlation coefficient can coexist with a large systematic bias — two instruments can track each other perfectly while one reads consistently 1.5 mg/L high. Correlation answers 'do they move together'; agreement answers 'can I substitute one for the other', which is the actual question. Reporting r² here would be a methodological error that a reviewer will catch.

Sample Size

To be determined by a power calculation against the stated tolerance. Do the calculation before collecting, not after.

Stratification

MUST include high-turbidity, low-DO, and high-chlorophyll conditions. A validation dataset drawn only from good days validates nothing.

Calibration study

Maps To
  • rq2
Design

Repeated sorties across varying illumination, season, and water conditions. Each sortie: imaging grid plus N probe stations, N varied deliberately from 2 to 8.

Analysis

Regression uncertainty as a function of N. Local versus global model comparison via cross-validation.

The Key Output

A curve of retrieval uncertainty against number of ground-truth points. That curve is directly actionable — it tells the mission planner how many stations to fly — and it is a genuine contribution to the UAV water quality literature, which currently just says 'ground truth is needed' without quantifying how much.

Held Out Validation

Fit on one site, test on another. A random split of one lake's data measures memorisation, not generalisation.

Longitudinal observation

Maps To
  • rq3
  • rq4
Design
Instrumented deployment at the pilot site for a minimum of one full annual cycle.
Why A Full Year Is Irreducible

The dry-season thermal stress window and the monsoon overturn window are different mechanisms in different seasons. A six-month study sees one and concludes it understands both. You cannot compress a year of weather, and any funder who asks for results in six months is asking for a study that will mislead them.

Paired Measurement

Surface-only series collected alongside, to establish what the incumbent method would have seen.

Analysis

Time-series analysis of stratification and oxygen debt. For RQ4: mixed-effects model of cycle-level FCR and survival against cumulative sub-optimal DO exposure, with cage as a random effect.

Adoption study

Maps To
  • rq5
Design
See the ethics section. This is where the methodology gets genuinely hard.
Status
·DESIGN UNRESOLVED

The Control Group Problem

Statement

The study's most important question requires a comparison group, and the obvious design is unethical.

The Unethical Design

Randomise farms to receive alerts or not. Withhold warnings from the control group. Compare mortality.

Why It Is Unacceptable

It means watching a farmer lose their stock — possibly their livelihood and the debt behind it — while holding information that might have saved it. No plausible scientific value justifies that. An ethics board should reject it and if one approved it, that would be a failure of the board rather than a licence to proceed.

The Alternatives and Their Flaws
Design
Stepped-wedge — all farms eventually receive the system, order randomised.
Pro

Everyone gets it. Ethically defensible. Standard in health services research for exactly this reason.

Con

Confounded by season, since farms onboarded in the dry season experience a different risk regime than those onboarded in the monsoon. Requires careful modelling and a longer study.

Verdict
The most defensible design available. Probably the answer.
Design
Historical comparison — same farms, before and after.
Pro
No withholding.
Con

Heavily confounded by year-to-year weather variation, which is the dominant driver of the outcome. A good year after installation proves nothing.

Verdict
Weak alone. Useful as supporting evidence.
Design
Observe-only period — collect data but do not alert, then switch on alerts.
Pro
Clean baseline.
Con

If we detect a developing kill during the observe-only period and stay silent, we are back to the unethical design with extra steps. There is no version of this where a genuine threat is observed and withheld.

Verdict

Only acceptable with an explicit, pre-registered break-glass rule: any detection above a defined severity triggers immediate disclosure and the farm exits the observation arm. State the rule in advance and honour it even when it costs the study.

Design
Comparison against neighbouring un-instrumented farms on the same lake.
Pro

No withholding — we simply are not working with them. Shared weather controls the biggest confounder.

Con

Not randomised. Farms that adopt differ systematically from those that do not, and the difference is probably competence — which is also what drives outcomes. Selection bias points the wrong way and would flatter us.

Verdict
Useful, honestly caveated. Never presented as causal.
Our Position

Stepped-wedge as the primary design, with neighbouring-farm comparison as supporting evidence, and an explicit break-glass disclosure rule during any baseline period. If a funder or a reviewer pushes for the randomised-withholding design because it is cleaner, the answer is no, and this paragraph is why.

The Honest Consequence

This means our causal evidence will always be weaker than a clean RCT would give. We accept that. A slightly weaker study is the correct price for not gambling with someone's livelihood, and a thesis that explains this trade explicitly is doing better science than one that quietly took the easy design.

Data Quality Protocol

Principle

The QC battery is not a data-cleaning chore. It is the thing that makes the dataset defensible.

Citations
qartod-manual
Elements
  • QARTOD-style flagging at source. See data-dictionary.json.
  • Scheduled reference-check of the sonde against a laboratory standard. This is the ONLY control that catches biofouling drift, which does not fail loudly — it produces plausible, confidently wrong numbers.
  • Descent-versus-ascent hysteresis check on every profile. If they disagree, the descent rate exceeded the sensor's response time and the profile is smeared.
  • Partial profiles flagged and excluded from aggregates by default.
  • Raw data immutable. Corrections are new records referencing the original.
The Pre Registration Commitment

Exclusion criteria are defined BEFORE data collection. Deciding after the fact which profiles to exclude is how a null result becomes a positive one without anyone intending fraud.

Ethics

Human Subjects
  • Farmer interviews require informed consent and ethics approval. Participants are often in precarious financial positions, which raises the bar on voluntariness — a farmer who believes participation might bring a subsidy is not freely consenting.
  • Farm production data is commercially sensitive. Anonymisation in publication is not sufficient when there are only forty cage operators on a lake and the paper names the lake.
  • Compensate participants for their time. Extractive research on smallholders is a real and common pattern and we should not add to it.
Imagery
  • Aerial imagery over a lake captures houses, boats, and people who did not consent and are not our customers.
  • Retention limits apply regardless of scientific value — and the frames most useful for training are the ones over the busiest, most populated shorelines. That tension is real.
  • No person-detection models. Not because it is hard, but because building it changes what this company is.
The Withholding Question

Addressed above. It is the study's central ethical issue and it should have its own section in the dissertation rather than a line in a form.

Dual Use

The platform's surveillance and IUU-enforcement applications are deliberately out of scope. A researcher should note that the capability exists and that the choice not to pursue it is a choice.

Conflict of Interest

The study evaluates a product the researchers have a commercial interest in. This must be declared prominently. It is also why pre-registering the analysis matters more here than in a disinterested study — the incentive to find a positive result is structural, not hypothetical.

Validity Threats

ThreatDetailMitigation
Single-site generalisationOne lake's dynamics do not transfer. Taal's volcanic chemistry is not Laguna de Bay's eutrophication.Multi-site from Phase 3 where affordable. Otherwise state the limit plainly rather than generalising.
Selection bias in adopting farmsFarms that adopt are probably better-managed, and better management drives outcomes. The bias flatters us.Stepped-wedge; measure baseline management practice; state it.
Hawthorne effectFarms in a study behave differently because they are being watched. Some of the improvement will be the observation, not the instrument.Long duration; measure the comparison group's practices too.
Rare-event statisticsA season with zero kills cannot answer RQ3. A naive classifier on this data will predict 'no kill' always and score 97 percent accurate.Report the confusion matrix, never accuracy. Be willing to conclude that the question is unanswered.
Researcher degrees of freedomMany analytic choices, a commercial interest in the outcome, and no pre-registration equals a positive result regardless of the truth.This file. Pre-register the analysis and the exclusion criteria.
Survivorship in the incident literatureOur whole problem framing rests on reported kills. Unreported smallholder losses are invisible, and chronic loss is invisible entirely.RQ4 looks where the light is not.

What Would Falsify the Whole Thesis

Note

A study that cannot state this is not a study.

Conditions
  • If drone profiles do not agree with reference sondes, the instrument does not work.
  • If same-sortie calibration does not meaningfully reduce retrieval uncertainty versus an uncalibrated index, the core architectural claim is empty.
  • If stratification and oxygen debt show no divergence before mortality events, the mechanism is not observable at our cadence and the platform cannot do the job.
  • If chronic water quality variation shows no association with FCR and survival, the sleeper hypothesis is dead and the product is catastrophe-only — which our own weather limitation makes a weak proposition.
  • If operators do not act on information, the venture has no reason to exist regardless of every other result.
The Commitment

Any of these coming back negative should be published, not buried. A negative result on the observability thesis would be genuinely valuable to the field — it would tell every other team not to build this. That is worth more than a quiet pivot.
