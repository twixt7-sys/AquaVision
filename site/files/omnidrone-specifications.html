OmniDrone — System Specification

omnidrone-specifications v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗
ℹSTATUS BANNER

NO HARDWARE EXISTS. Every value in this file is a design target or a requirement, not a measurement. Nothing has been built, weighed, or flown. The render in the repository root is an industrial design study and is not dimensionally authoritative. Where the render and this specification disagree, BOTH need reconciling — neither is validated.

Platform Context

OmniDrone is the flagship hardware component of AquaVision Smart (Phase 3) — the premium, hardware-enabled upgrade layer of the AquaVision platform, sold to commercial and enterprise farms that have outgrown the app-based Phase 1 (free) and Phase 2 (subscription) tiers. It is not AquaVision's core product; the company's foundation is the software platform, and this specification describes an optional, higher-tier extension of it.

Design Philosophy

Principles
PrincipleImplication
The probe is the productEvery trade-off resolves in favour of getting a good sonde to depth reliably. The camera is valuable; the winch is existential.
Mass is the enemyMultirotor payload fraction is brutal and unforgiving. Every gram of feature costs endurance, and endurance is already our weakest axis against every competitor.
Configure per missionDo not carry the hopper on a profiling sortie. Modular payload is not a nice-to-have; it is how the energy budget closes at all.
Assume it goes in the waterFloat, beacon, contained lithium. Design for the ditching rather than pretending it will not happen.
Fixable by a technician, not an engineerField-replaceable modules, no special tools, no calibration that needs a laptop.
Legible and benignShrouded rotors and a marine-instrument aesthetic. This machine works near people on narrow dikes and small boats.

Top Level Targets

Note

TARGETS. Derived from mission requirements, not from a built aircraft. The MTOM target is the single most consequential number here because it determines the regulatory bracket.

Configuration
Quadrotor, ducted/shrouded rotors, ventral payload bay with centre-line winch
Mtom Target Kg
6.8
Mtom Rationale
Statement
Targeting under 7 kg is a deliberate REGULATORY decision, not an engineering one.
Reasoning

CAAP brackets obligations at 7 kg gross weight. At or below 7 kg the regulatory burden is materially lighter; above it, mandatory registration and stricter compliance apply. Since commercial use requires certification regardless, the 7 kg line is less decisive than it first appears — but it still shapes the compliance path and the cost of every future variant.

Citations
caap-rpas-regulations
The Tension

6.8 kg is very tight for this payload. There is a real possibility the aircraft cannot do the mission under 7 kg, in which case the honest answer is to accept the heavier bracket and its compliance cost rather than to descope the probe. Do not let a regulatory threshold silently delete the capability that is the entire point of the product.

Verification Status

The 7 kg threshold is drawn from secondary summaries of CAAP rules. CONFIRM WITH CAAP DIRECTLY before this number drives a design decision. Building an airframe around a misremembered regulatory threshold would be an expensive and embarrassing error.

Max Wind Caveat

For the bare aircraft. The airframe does not swim, but it hovers in spray over water in the tropics and periodically brings a wet, dripping probe back into its own belly. IP54 is a floor, not an aspiration.

The Finding

With every payload fitted the aircraft is at 6.56 kg empty of consumables — which leaves roughly 0.44 kg to the 7 kg line and effectively no dispensing capacity at all. THE BUDGET DOES NOT CLOSE for a fully-configured aircraft.

Provenance
ƒCalculated
What This Finding Means
Statement
This is the most important result in the hardware section and it should not be softened.
Reading

The concept as described — probe AND imaging AND dispensing on one sub-7 kg airframe — does not appear to close on this paper model. That does not kill the venture. It kills the 'one drone does everything simultaneously' framing.

The Resolutions
  • Per-mission payload configuration. Fly the probe OR the hopper, never both. This is already in mission-profiles.json and it is the cheapest fix — but it means the marketing must stop implying one flight does all four functions.
  • Accept a heavier MTOM bracket and pay the compliance cost. Honest, and possibly correct.
  • Descope dispensing to small high-value treatments only, which mission-profiles.json already suspects is where it belongs on economic grounds anyway. Two independent lines of reasoning arriving at the same conclusion is a signal worth taking seriously.
  • Descope thermal imaging. Lowest-value sensor per gram in the set.
The Recommendation

Configure per mission, and reposition dispensing as targeted treatment rather than bulk feeding. The mass budget and the mission economics both point there independently. Say this in the pitch before someone else does the arithmetic — a founder who has found their own problem is credible; one who has it found for them is not.
