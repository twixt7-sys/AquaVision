Monitoring Approaches Compared

OmniDrone against the real alternatives — honest scoring. This comparison describes the Phase 3 (Smart Aquaculture hardware layer) monitoring approaches specifically; it does not compare Phase 1/2 software-only farm management, which competes on a different axis entirely (see competitive-landscape.json for software competitors).

chart-manual-vs-omnidrone ◎Target raw JSON ↗

A capability comparison across the sensing/monitoring approaches a Phase 3 commercial or enterprise farm actually chooses between, once it has outgrown manual logging. The OmniDrone wins on depth, coverage, and imaging; it LOSES on continuity, weather tolerance, cost, and regulatory simplicity. An honest radar shows the losses. The takeaway is not that we dominate — it is that a fixed-buoy + OmniDrone hybrid dominates either alone, which is where the Phase 3 product roadmap points. This chart is scoped to hardware monitoring methods only and says nothing about the Phase 1 free app or Phase 2 premium analytics, which are not monitoring-hardware alternatives at all.

Honest Note

Scores are the authors' qualitative judgement on a 1-5 scale, not measured benchmarks. A vendor-scored comparison always flatters the vendor; this one deliberately scores our own weaknesses low so the comparison is credible. See competitive-landscape.json.

The Honest Reading

OmniDrone and the buoy have nearly complementary shapes — we are strong exactly where the buoy is weak (depth, space, imaging) and weak where it is strong (continuity, weather, cost, regulation). That complementarity is the argument for a hybrid, not for displacement. The USV matches us on depth and beats us on weather and continuity; our answer to it is imaging and topology-independence. This chart should be shown with the conclusion stated, not left for the viewer to misread as domination.
