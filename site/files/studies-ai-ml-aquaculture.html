Machine learning, deep learning and hybrid models in aquaculture: water quality, disease detection, feed optimisation and fish behaviour (2024)

Systematic Review!Challenges★MUST FIX OR REMOVE

Review of machine learning, deep learning, attention mechanisms, and hybrid models applied to aquaculture — water quality monitoring, disease detection, feed optimisation, and fish behaviour analysis.

  • ML, DL, attention mechanisms, and hybrid models are driving gains across water quality, disease detection, feed optimisation, and behaviour analysis.
  • Model accuracy depends on acquiring substantial, high-quality training data from IoT systems.
  • High deployment costs and limited power supply for real-time data transmission pose significant challenges.
  • Federated learning, explainable AI, edge computing, and transfer learning are emerging responses to data privacy, scalability, and adaptability constraints.
Relevance To Omnidrone

The framing source for our ML approach and, more usefully, for the constraints on it. The identified barriers — deployment cost, power supply, data scarcity — map exactly onto our weakest attributes.

The Challenge Stated Plainly

This review is telling us that the things preventing ML adoption in aquaculture are deployment cost and power supply. A multirotor is expensive and has a brutal energy budget. The literature is identifying our two worst attributes as the field's binding constraints.

What It Gives Us

The four emerging responses it names are all directly applicable and all already in our architecture for independent reasons: edge computing because connectivity is unreliable; explainable AI because farmers ignore alerts they cannot interrogate; federated learning because farm data is commercially sensitive; transfer learning because we are data-poor. That convergence is reassuring — we arrived at these from first principles and the field agrees.

Model cards for model reporting (2019)

Conference Paper★MEDIUM

Introduces model cards as a documentation standard for transparent model reporting, including intended use, out-of-scope use, and disaggregated performance.

Relevance To Omnidrone

The convention followed in ai-ml-models.json. The out_of_scope_use field is the one that matters here: it is what forces us to write down, before the commercial pressure arrives, that the chlorophyll model is not a toxin detector and the anomaly model is not a staff performance monitor.

Why It Is In This Collection

Because the most important ML decision in this project is not architectural. It is the decision not to ship the kill-risk model until it is validated. A documentation standard that forces that decision to be written down and defended is doing real work.

Relevance To Omnidrone

The adaptive sampling problem formalised — where should the aircraft go next to maximise information gain, subject to safety and energy constraints? This is directly the mp-02 adaptive station selection problem, and someone has already done the theory.

The Practical Note

Version one should let the operator pick probe stations from the NDCI map. This literature is the path to automating that later, and it is worth reading before building the naive version.

The Data Problem

Why This Section Exists

The most important thing about ML in this project is not which architecture we pick. It is that we do not have data, and the model that matters most needs the rarest events.

The Arithmetic
Statement

Mass kills happen a few times per year per lake. A supervised model needs many examples. Even a multi-year, multi-site deployment yields tens of positive examples at best.

The Consequence

A naive classifier trained on this will learn to always predict 'no kill' and score 97 percent accurate. This is the classic rare-event trap and it WILL happen if anyone evaluates on accuracy rather than on the confusion matrix.

The Implications
Implication
Physics-informed beats pure data-driven for the rare-event problem.
Reasoning

We KNOW the mechanism — stratification, oxygen debt, weather trigger. Encoding known physics is strictly better than hoping to learn it from a handful of examples. This is why stratification-state estimation in ai-ml-models.json is deterministic computation rather than ML, and that choice should be defended rather than apologised for.

Implication

The chronic sub-lethal hypothesis is a far more tractable ML problem than catastrophe prediction.

Reasoning

Continuous outcomes, abundant data, every cycle a sample. If the hypothesis in cost-of-inaction.json is right — that chronic loss exceeds catastrophic loss — then it is simultaneously the better business AND the better machine learning problem. That coincidence is worth noticing and it should probably reorder the research priorities.

Reasoning

A random split of one lake's imagery measures memorisation, not generalisation, and it will flatter every model we build. The Phase 3 gate requires site-level holdout specifically to prevent this self-deception.

Synthesis

What the Field Establishes

ML works in aquaculture across water quality, disease, feeding, and behaviour. The methods are mature enough to borrow.

What the Field Warns Us About

Deployment cost, power supply, and training data scarcity are the binding constraints — and they are our three weakest attributes.

Our Position
The Discipline

The models in this project are ordered by how dangerous it is to be wrong, not by how well they demo. Water masking and chlorophyll retrieval ship first because a mistake costs a bad map. The kill-risk score ships last, or never, because a false negative reads as 'you are safe' and can cost a harvest.

The Thing To Hold Onto

Without a model, a farmer is uncertain and cautious. With a wrong model, a farmer is confident and wrong. We can make things worse than doing nothing — which is not true of any other component in this system. That asymmetry should govern every ML decision in the project.

The Interim Product

'The bottom three metres are anoxic and rain is forecast tonight' requires no model, carries no false certainty, is derived entirely from measurement, and is available on day one. Given the farmer's twenty years of experience, it may also be a better product than a score. Ship that first.
