Telemetry & Data Schema

telemetry-schema v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

The wire and storage format for everything the platform produces, across every phase: app-layer entities a farmer creates directly (accounts, farm profiles, manual water-quality entries, feeding logs, community posts, marketplace listings) and, for Phase 3 upgrade customers, everything the OmniDrone and its sensors produce. This is the contract between client app, backend, aircraft, dock, cloud, twin, and dashboard. Field definitions resolve against data-dictionary.json; do not duplicate semantics here.

Design Rules

  • UTC always, RFC 3339, millisecond precision. Local time is a presentation concern. Storing local time would destroy the diel analysis the first time a site crosses a DST or timezone boundary — and the diel analysis is the product.
  • Every measurement carries a QC flag at source; every manually-logged entry carries a `source_type` and, where relevant, an `entry_method` instead — a farmer's test strip does not get a QARTOD code, and pretending it does would manufacture false rigor.
  • Self-reported and sensor-measured records of the same conceptual quantity (e.g. dissolved oxygen) are NEVER merged into one array or table without an explicit, mandatory `source_type` discriminator on every record. A chart that silently blends a test-strip reading with a calibrated sonde reading has corrupted both.
  • Every position carries a horizontal accuracy estimate where a position is captured by a device. A position without an error bar cannot answer whether two profiles are at the same station.
  • Model outputs live in a separate object from measurements, and separate again from self-reports.
  • Records are append-only. A correction is a new record referencing the original.
  • A TAN reading without its pH and temperature partners at the same depth and instant is rejected at ingestion, not stored and patched later. Manual entries are exempt from this specific partner-presence rule — a farmer logging pH alone is normal and useful and must not be rejected for lacking a lab panel.

The Rule That Matters

Flag 3 (suspect) data is shown to humans and excluded from automated triggers. This split is deliberate: a human can weigh a suspect reading in context; an alert rule cannot, and a false alarm from a fouled sensor at 04:30 destroys trust in the whole system.

Ingestion Validation

Reject Rather Than Repair

Malformed records are rejected at the boundary and returned to an error queue (the dock's for Phase 3, the client app's sync queue for Phase 1/2). They are NOT partially stored and patched later. A dataset containing quietly repaired records is a dataset nobody can defend in an audit, a dispute, or a support conversation.

Mandatory Checks
  • Schema conformance.
  • Every record resolves to a real, active farm_id (and pond_id where applicable) — no orphaned telemetry or logs.
  • Partner presence for sensor samples — TAN without pH and temp at the same depth and instant is rejected. This check does NOT apply to manual_water_quality_entry records.
  • Temporal monotonicity within a profile.
  • Position accuracy present where a device captures a position.
  • QC flags present on every sensor channel; `confidence: self_reported` present on every manual entry.
  • Model records carry model_version and, for Phase 1/2 outputs, a linked_record_id.
  • Imagery concentration products carry an uncertainty field reference.
  • Dispensing records carry a human authoriser.
  • Marketplace orders and consultation bookings resolve to an active listing or expert record before being marked completed.
