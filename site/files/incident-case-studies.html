Documented Incident Case Studies — Evidence Base

incident-case-studies v1.1.0 updated 2026-07-18 ✓Sourced raw JSON ↗
ℹVerification Notice

Details below are drawn from search-result summaries of news reports and published studies. Every entry carries a `verification_status`. Before any of these appear in a thesis or a deck, open the primary source and confirm the figure. News reporting of fish kill tonnages is frequently revised upward or downward in later reports, and citing a superseded figure is an easy and avoidable error.

Purpose

Real, reported fish kill and water quality incidents used as the evidence base for the Phase 3 tier of the problem statement — the catastrophic, coverage-gap end of the pain hierarchy. These are other people's losses, documented by journalists and scientists. Treat them with the seriousness they deserve — each figure below is somebody's harvest.

Positioning In the Phased Narrative

Note

This file was originally the entire evidence base for the company. It is now the evidence base for one tier of three. Nothing below has been altered or reweighted — the Taal Lake fishkill and the other incidents here remain the strongest, most citable argument for Phase 3's automated water monitoring hardware, and they should still be led with whenever the audience is a Phase 3 prospect, a regulator, or a technical reviewer.

Why the Other Two Tiers Have No Equivalent File

Phase 1's guidance gap and Phase 2's scaling gap produce chronic, quiet, individually small losses — a beginner who gives up after a bad first cycle, a growing farm that plateaus without ever having a dramatic event to report. Nobody writes a news story about a farmer who never learned to identify a disease, or a mid-scale farm whose survival rate crept down over three cycles with no single day anyone could point to. That absence of documentation is not evidence the problem is smaller; it is a property of what gets reported, and it is exactly the same observation this file's own patterns section makes about chronic sub-lethal loss below. See cost-of-inaction.json's beginner-attrition-and-preventable-loss and scaling-blindness-and-missed-optimization categories, both tagged assumption, for the honest, unevidenced version of the Phase 1/2 case.

Why This Matters To Omnidrone

Taal is the archetype of the target environment: dense cage aquaculture in a stratifying volcanic lake shared by multiple municipalities, with a documented history of recurrence. The concentration of loss in one municipality (300 of 343 tonnes in Talisay) is itself the argument for spatial monitoring — the kill was not uniform across the lake, which means a single monitoring point anywhere would have given the wrong answer for most operators.

What the Omnidrone Would Have Seen
Honest Assessment

Probably: a shoaling thermocline and rising hypolimnetic oxygen debt over the preceding days, and a spatial DO gradient concentrating in the Talisay area. Possibly: nothing useful, if the volcanic sulfur input was fast and chemical rather than slow and physical. We should not claim we would have prevented this. We should claim we would have seen the water column changing, which nobody did.

Caveat
This is a retrospective counterfactual with no data behind it. It is a hypothesis to test in Phase 3, not a marketing claim. Presenting a counterfactual as a capability is exactly the overclaim the brand voice guidance prohibits.

A climate-triggered event rather than a stocking-triggered one, and therefore evidence for the 'this is getting worse' argument. A twenty-year national aggregate is the number that establishes this as a chronic structural problem rather than a run of bad luck.

Critical Caveat

PHP 322.1 M over 21 years averages roughly PHP 15 M per year nationally, which is SMALL — it is a fraction of a single large farm's annual revenue. Do NOT present this figure as the market opportunity. It almost certainly reflects only officially recorded incidents and excludes unreported smallholder losses, chronic sub-lethal production losses, and downstream economic effects. Used carelessly, this number argues AGAINST the venture. Used honestly, it argues that recorded losses are the visible tip of an unmeasured problem — which is, conveniently and genuinely, the thesis.

Why This Matters To Omnidrone

This is the most scientifically valuable entry in the file, because it identifies the causal triad — DO depletion, ammonia-N, and hydrogen sulfide — rather than just reporting a tonnage. That triad is precisely the signature of a post-overturn event: bottom water arriving at the surface. It is direct published support for the vertical profiling architecture, and it is the citation to lead with in the thesis.

Design Implication

Three of the probe's channels exist because of this finding. DO, ammonia, and the ORP-based sulfide inference are not a generic water quality panel — they are the specific diagnostic set for this documented mechanism.

The study's framing — 'occurrence AND recurrence' — is the important part. These are not accidents. Findings
Pattern

Dissolved oxygen depletion is the proximate cause in essentially every Philippine inland incident in this set.

Implication

DO is not one parameter among many. It is THE parameter. Any product that treats it as one tile among twenty on a dashboard has misread the problem.

Confidence
high
Pattern

Over-stocking appears as the underlying cause wherever investigators looked past the proximate one.

Implication

The industry's structural problem is density, and our product does not reduce density. We must be clear-eyed that we sell a mitigation for a problem whose cure is regulatory and behavioural.

Confidence
high
Uncomfortable Corollary

There is a version of this product that makes over-stocking MORE attractive, by letting operators push density closer to the edge with better instruments. That is a real risk of the technology and it deserves a paragraph in the thesis's ethics section rather than silence.

What This Evidence Base Lacks

  • Sub-lethal chronic losses. Every incident here is a mass mortality event, because those are what get reported. The slow bleed — poor FCR, stunted growth, and low survival from chronic marginal water quality — is almost certainly larger in aggregate and is entirely invisible in this dataset. If the OmniDrone's real value is in the chronic case rather than the catastrophic one, this evidence base is looking in the wrong place, and that possibility should be actively investigated rather than assumed away.
  • Denominators. We have losses without knowing total production at risk, so no incident rate can be computed. A tonnage with no denominator cannot support a probability claim.
  • Counterfactuals. No incident here has a documented case of a farm that had monitoring and avoided the kill. That comparison is the actual evidence a rational buyer wants, and nobody has produced it. Producing it would be a genuine contribution and is a strong thesis objective in its own right.
  • Primary-source verification. Everything here is second-hand as of this revision.
  • Phase 1 and Phase 2 pain. There is no journalism about a beginner farmer who quietly gave up after an undiagnosed disease outbreak, or a growing farm that plateaued for three cycles for lack of analytics. Those failures do not make the news, so this file — built entirely from news and published incident reports — structurally cannot see them. That is a limitation of this evidence base, not proof those tiers are smaller problems, and it is the reason the Phase 1/2 framing elsewhere in this repository is built on stated assumptions rather than on incidents like these.
