Deployment Playbook

deployment-playbook v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

Purpose

The operational runbook for taking a site from first contact to steady-state monitoring. Written as a checklist because in the field, prose is not read and steps get skipped — and a skipped commissioning step becomes an incident.

The Operating Reality

A working fish farm is not a research site. The drone is the least important thing happening there on any given day. If an operation requires attention it will not get, it fails. Every procedure below is written for a person with other jobs to do.

The Three Phase Scope

Statement

This file is the Phase 3 deployment playbook — the hardware/IoT operations layer for commercial and enterprise farms adopting OmniDrone, probes, and PondTwin. The company's actual first 'deployment' happens much earlier and much more lightly, in Phase 1.

Phase 1 2 Deployment App Onboarding
What Deployment Means Here

There is no site visit, no airspace check, no commissioning gate. 'Deployment' is a farmer downloading the app, completing the guided farm-setup wizard, and logging their first water reading or feeding event.

The Equivalent Checklist
  • Account creation and farm profile (species, pond count, rough size).
  • Guided setup assistant — the software equivalent of site commissioning, but self-serve and instant rather than a technician visit.
  • First manual water-quality log entry (photo or typed) — the equivalent of a baseline profile, except unvalidated and self-reported (see risk-register-fmea.json R-18).
  • Prompted enrolment in a learning module and the community forum — the Phase 1 substitute for onboarding/training (see training-certification.json).
The Honest Difference

None of the qualification, permission-chain, or baseline-before-any-claim discipline that governs Phase 3 applies here. That is the point — Phase 1 deployment is deliberately frictionless because it is the acquisition engine, not a safety-critical rollout. The trade-off is that Phase 1 data quality has no equivalent of the reference-check gate below; it is trusted less for exactly that reason.

Phase 2 Note

Phase 2 'deployment' is a conversion event, not a new install: an existing free-tier farm upgrades to premium analytics, predictive health monitoring, marketplace access, or paid expert consultation. No commissioning gate applies there either — it is a billing and feature-entitlement change.

Phases

Prospecting and qualification

Goal
Do not deploy where we will lose.
Qualification Criteria
CriterionTestWhy
Response capacityDoes the operator have aeration or other means to act on a warning?A warning to a farm that cannot respond is a countdown clock, not a product. This is the first filter, not the last.
Station densityEnough cages/stations clustered within one dock's operating radius to beat a fixed buoy?Below the scale threshold, a buoy wins and we should say so rather than sell a worse solution.
Site geometryClear launch/recovery approach, shore access, an alternate landing option over water?No alternate landing site over a large lake is a drowning waiting to happen.
Permission chainCan we actually get LGU, water-body authority, and airspace clearance here?The best site technically is worthless if the coalition cannot be assembled. See stakeholder-pain-points.json#/the_alignment_problem.
The Discipline

Disqualifying a bad-fit prospect is a win, not a loss. A deployment that fails visibly on a lake costs more in reputation than the sale was worth, because lake communities talk.

Site commissioning

Goal

Establish that we can fly here safely and that our measurements will be comparable over time.

Checklist
  • Airspace check; CAAP coordination where required.
  • LGU and water-body authority written permission.
  • Community notification — a social-licence requirement even where not a legal one. A drone appearing over a lake unannounced earns a reputation before it earns a customer.
  • Dock siting: shore access, power/solar, connectivity, security, typhoon exposure.
  • Obstacle survey: power lines, masts, trees, and the cage structure itself.
  • Alternate landing site identification and survey.
  • Bathymetry survey — one-time, rent the sensor.
  • Fixed station definition with RTK positions. Profiles are only comparable if the station is genuinely the same place each time.
  • Baseline profiles at every station across at least one full diel cycle before ANY claim is made about the site.
  • Ingest available Copernicus history for the water body — free, and it gives the site a past on day one.
  • Assign a dedicated sonde to this site and log the assignment. Biosecurity starts here.
Gate

No routine operation until baseline diel profiles exist. A site with no baseline cannot distinguish 'abnormal' from 'we have never looked before'.

Onboarding and training

Goal
The farm's people can run the routine and recognise the abnormal without calling us.
Detail File
data/08_operations/training-certification.json
The Test

Can the technician clean the probe correctly, swap a battery, recognise a fouled-sensor reading, and interpret an alert? If not, the deployment degrades quietly into confidently wrong data — worse than no data, because someone acts on it.

Steady-state operation

Goal
Reliable daily sorties against the diel and glint schedule.
Daily Rhythm

See concept-of-operations.json#/a_day_in_the_life and mission-profiles.json#/mission_scheduling_logic.

The Two Sorties That Matter

Dawn (DO minimum) and afternoon (DO maximum). The amplitude between them is a leading indicator that a single daily reading cannot produce. Two well-timed sorties beat four badly-timed ones.

Multi-site scaling

Goal
Dock network density in a lake, then adjacent lakes.
Biosecurity Gate

A drone moving between sites is a pathogen vector. Single-site sonde assignment plus full decontamination between sites is mandatory. See biosecurity-protocol.json.

Economics Note

Dense clusters and cooperative-served bays are the good unit economics. Dispersed single farms are the bad ones. Scale toward density.

Pre Flight Checklist

Note

Run before every sortie. Automated where possible; human-confirmed where consequential.

Items
  • Weather within envelope — and remember the tethered-probe wind limit is BELOW the bare-aircraft limit, and unknown until flight-tested.
  • Battery charge and health sufficient for the mission plus reserve.
  • Airspace clear; no NOTAM conflict.
  • Assigned sonde present, clean, and within calibration.
  • Calibration panel imaged (imaging sorties).
  • Payload configured for THIS mission — the mass budget forces per-mission configuration, so the wrong payload is a real failure.
  • Failsafe home point and alternate landing confirmed as reachable over water.
  • For dispensing sorties: human authorisation obtained, substance and dose confirmed, dose ceiling set.
  • Tether cutter armed and functional.

Post Flight Checklist

  • Sonde and full tether length rinsed and, for inter-site movement, decontaminated.
  • Data uploaded or buffered.
  • Battery on charge.
  • Airframe inspected for damage, corrosion, and fouling.
  • Any anomaly logged — including near-misses. Blameless reporting; a hidden near-miss is a future crash.
  • Calibration panel imaged (imaging sorties).

The Things That Will Actually Go Wrong

Note

Written from the failure modes catalogued across the hardware files, so field staff are not surprised.

Items
ProblemResponse
Probe snags on cage nettingTension anomaly detection; slack-and-retry; escalate to pilot; cutter as last resort. Log the station — a station that snags repeatedly must be moved even at the cost of coverage.
Sensor reads plausible but wrong (fouling drift)Caught only by the scheduled reference check, not by any onboard test. This is why the reference check is non-negotiable.
Dock fails to open at 04:30Dock self-reports the fault. Silent failure to open is the worst case — a missing dawn profile nobody notices until it matters.
Wind picks up mid-sortieAbort and return with reserve intact. Never finish 'one more station'.
Link dropsComplete current mission if within envelope, then return. Do not hover awaiting the link — that burns the reserve.
Battery lower than expectedThe reserve is not available to finish the mission. Every ditched aircraft was one more station from the dock.
