Pitch Deck Outline

pitch-deck-outline v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

Note

A slide-by-slide outline for the investor deck, pulling from across the repository. Rebuilt around the three-phase spine: free app (acquisition) → premium subscription (monetization) → smart hardware (upgrade layer). Built on the wager stated throughout: rigorous honesty is more fundable than polished optimism, for the right investor. Each slide names its source file and the ONE thing it must accomplish.

Deck Philosophy

Principle

Lead with who's underserved at every stage, sell the phased economics as land-and-expand, volunteer the risks at every phase, and never overclaim a conversion rate, a hardware capability, or a user's trust before it's earned.

The Arc

Aquaculture just became the majority of the world's aquatic animal production, and almost none of it is digitally managed at any price point → selling expensive, unproven hardware to that market first means paying full acquisition cost for every customer with no funnel underneath it, which is a go-to-market problem, not a technology problem → the fix is to meet farmers where they are: a free app that any beginner can use today, which is also the cheapest possible way to build trust and real farm data → as a farm grows past manual tracking, a premium tier turns that logged history into revenue → the operations that outgrow the software entirely are the ones who buy the hardware, so it sells as an upgrade into a customer we already know, not a cold sale to a stranger → here is the one thing we must prove at each phase and how we'll prove it cheaply, starting with the cheapest.

What This Deck Refuses To Do
  • Show a hockey-stick revenue forecast
  • Claim a free-to-paid conversion rate before Phase 1 usage data exists
  • Claim we prevent fish kills before Phase 3 validation
  • Hide the BVLOS dependency inside the Phase 3 story
  • Invent app downloads, active users, subscribers, or any other traction
  • Imply TRL beyond 3 for the hardware layer
  • Treat the free tier as bait we're impatient with — it is a real product for a real audience, evaluated on its own honest terms

The Insight That Makes It Defensible

N
8
Must Accomplish

Two insights, stacked. Engineering: the probe and the camera are one instrument — the drone carries its own ground truth and calibrates its own imagery in a single sortie, which nobody else does. Business: by the time a farm needs that hardware, we already hold years of its own water and production history from Phases 1-2, so the digital twin starts calibrated, not cold. The data moat is built before the hardware ships.

How We Sell — The Checkable Economics At Every Phase

N
10
Must Accomplish

Phase 1 costs the farmer nothing, so there's no ROI pitch — only an activation one. Phase 2 sells against verifiable savings (feed cost via FCR, time saved over manual records). Phase 3 leads with aeration-electricity savings: metered, budgeted, verifiable in one cycle without believing our modelling. At no phase do we lead with the least verifiable claim.

Competition — Honestly

N
12
Must Accomplish

Two competitive sets now, not one. Farm-management and record-keeping apps: we win on aquaculture-specific AI and an upgrade path they don't have, we lose on brand recognition and multi-crop breadth. Drone/monitoring hardware vendors: same honest matrix as before — we win on depth+space+imaging, lose on continuity+weather+cost, and the hybrid with a fixed buoy is our own roadmap. Volunteering both wins trust.
