Fit Assessment

Reasoning

The relievers map onto the pains coherently. But every reliever is a design target, not a measured capability, and the customer whose pains these are has not been interviewed. A value proposition canvas built without customer contact demonstrates internal consistency, not fit.

The Strategic Conclusion

Sell tier 1 and 2. Earn tier 3. Prove tier 4. Never promise tier 5 before the data exists. This inverts the instinct of every founder in this category — everyone leads with the dead fish — and it is why most of them do not survive diligence.

Differentiation

Against Doing Nothing
A metered reduction in an existing electricity bill. Not a story about fish kills.
Against A Fixed Buoy

Depth and coverage. And the honest admission that below a scale threshold, the buoy wins and we will say so.

Against A USV

Transit speed across many stations, simultaneous aerial imaging, and the ability to ignore dikes, nets, and cage structure entirely. Their endurance beats us and always will.

Against Satellite

Resolution, revisit rate, cloud immunity, and depth. And we should ingest their data rather than compete with it.

Against Salmon Platforms
Cost structure and tropical design case. Not technology — we would lose that fight.
Against A Generic Drone Service

The probe. Anyone can fly a camera over a lake; calibrated depth-resolved chemistry from a hover is genuinely hard.

The Honest Summary

What We Can Defensibly Say Today

A platform designed to profile water at depth across many stations at low marginal labour cost, with its own calibration ground truth on board, in an industry where nobody currently measures anything and fish die because of it.

What We Cannot Say
That it works. Nothing has flown.
The Pitch That Survives Diligence

Not 'we prevent fish kills'. Rather: 'here is a documented, recurring, expensive failure mode caused by a monitoring gap that is economically rather than technically insurmountable; here is why an aerial platform inverts that economics; here is the one thing we have to prove and how we will prove it; and here is the boring, checkable ROI that pays for the deployment while we prove it.'
