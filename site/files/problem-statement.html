Problem Statement

problem-statement v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 raw JSON ↗

The Core Problem

One Sentence

Aquaculture operators make decisions worth their entire annual income about a three-dimensional, fast-changing chemical environment they cannot see, using a handful of surface observations taken at the safest time of day.

Expanded

A fish farmer's stock lives in a water column that stratifies, deoxygenates, blooms, and overturns on timescales of hours. The farmer's instruments — where they exist at all — measure one point, at the surface, when someone happens to be there. The gap between the resolution at which the water actually behaves and the resolution at which it is observed is where mass mortality events live. Every documented fish kill is, at bottom, a monitoring failure as much as a water quality failure: the conditions developed over days, and nobody saw them.

Why It Persists

Not because the science is missing. Limnologists have understood stratification and overturn for a century, and the sensors exist and are affordable. It persists because the observation is expensive in labour, not in capital: profiling a lake cage array properly means a boat, a person, a sonde, and two hours, repeated several times a day including before dawn, at every cage, forever. Nobody does that. So the data that would prevent the loss is technically available and practically unobtainable, and the industry has quietly settled into managing by intuition and absorbing the losses.

The Reframe

Note

This is the intellectual centre of the pitch. It is worth getting right.

Conventional Framing
Fish farms need better sensors.
Why That Framing Is Wrong

Sensors are not the bottleneck. A multiparameter sonde is a commodity. Fixed sensor buoys already exist and are already sold into aquaculture. The bottleneck is that a fixed sensor measures one point in a system whose defining property is spatial and vertical heterogeneity — and covering an array of thirty cages with fixed sondes at every relevant depth is an absurd capital and maintenance proposition.

Our Framing

The problem is not sensing. It is COVERAGE — moving a good sensor to many places, at many depths, many times a day, at near-zero marginal labour cost. That is a logistics problem with an aerial answer.

The Consequence

This reframe is what makes an aerial platform the right shape of solution rather than an expensive novelty. If the problem were 'we need a DO reading', a $200 buoy wins and the drone is theatre. Because the problem is 'we need forty DO profiles a day across a lake without putting a person in a boat before dawn', the economics invert.

Problem Decomposition

The Observability Gap

Id
prob-observability
Statement

Water quality is monitored at a spatial and temporal resolution orders of magnitude coarser than the processes that kill fish.

Evidence Summary

Fish kill investigations in Philippine lakes repeatedly attribute mortality to dissolved oxygen depletion and to ammonia and hydrogen sulfide following water column mixing — processes that develop over hours to days and are invisible to periodic surface sampling.

Citations
seafdec-laguna-fishkill bioflux-lake-buhi-2020 inquirer-taal-fishkill-2023
Severity
critical
Addressed By
  • reeled probe profiling
  • multi-station coverage
  • diel-aware scheduling

Vertical Blindness

Id
prob-blindness-at-depth
Statement

Surface measurements cannot detect stratification, and stratification is the precondition for the most catastrophic failure mode.

Expanded

A stratified lake in the hours before an overturn can present a perfectly reassuring surface: warm, oxygenated, clear. The killing agent is sitting three metres down, anoxic and sulfide-laden, waiting for a cold rain to bring it up. A surface sensor will report 'nominal' right up to the moment of the kill, and then report the kill.

Severity
critical
Addressed By
  • reeled probe vertical profiling
  • stratification index
  • oxygen debt index
Note

This is the strongest single technical argument for the reeled probe over every fixed-surface or imagery-only competitor. Lead with it.

Diel Sampling Bias

Id
prob-diel-sampling-bias
Statement

Manual sampling happens during working hours, which systematically measures the daily oxygen maximum and misses the pre-dawn minimum where mortality occurs.

Expanded

This is not a small error. It is a structural bias that makes the measurement anti-correlated with the risk it is supposed to detect. A farm sampling at 09:00 and finding 7 mg/L may be hours away from a 2 mg/L pre-dawn crash — and the data says everything is fine.

Severity
high
Addressed By
  • autonomous dock-based scheduling
  • pre-dawn sortie capability
Citations
boyd-water-quality-2020

Monitoring Labour Cost

Id
prob-labour
Statement
Adequate monitoring requires labour that no commercially rational operator will spend.
Expanded

The economics are the whole story. Proper coverage is not withheld from farmers by ignorance; it is withheld by arithmetic. Any solution that does not attack the marginal labour cost of an observation has not addressed the problem.

Severity
high
Addressed By
  • autonomy
  • dock-based operation
  • multi-unit coverage per sortie

Reaction Instead of Anticipation

Id
prob-reactive-response
Statement

Interventions are triggered by visible symptoms — fish gasping at the surface — by which point the intervention window has largely closed.

Expanded

By the time fish pipe at the surface, DO has already fallen through the stress threshold and the physiological damage is done; survivors carry stress-induced immunosuppression into a disease window that follows weeks later. The mortality invoice arrives twice.

Severity
high
Addressed By
  • trend detection
  • risk forecasting (post-validation only)
Caveat
The forecasting claim is NOT yet supported. Until the model is validated, this problem is addressed by earlier detection, not by prediction. Do not blur the two in a pitch.

Feed Applied Without Feedback

Id
prob-feed-waste
Statement

Feed is the dominant operating cost and is dispensed on schedule rather than on evidence, so overfeeding both wastes money and degrades the water that the feeding is meant to grow fish in.

Evidence Summary

Feed commonly represents around 60 percent of aquaculture operating cost, and uneaten feed is a direct driver of the organic loading that depletes oxygen.

Citations
innovasea-fcr-guide ussec-fcr-power globalseafood-fcr-primary-indicator
Severity
high
Addressed By
  • carriage and ejection system
  • condition-aware feeding logic
Note

The reflexivity here is the interesting part and a strong thesis theme: overfeeding is simultaneously the largest cost line AND a cause of the water quality problem. One intervention attacks both. That is a rare structure and it should be made explicit.

Treatment Delivery Latency and Precision

Id
prob-treatment-delivery
Statement

When a response is needed — aeration, lime, conditioner, treatment — delivery is manual, slow, spatially imprecise, and dangerous in the exact conditions that require it.

Expanded

The moment a farm most needs someone to distribute conditioner across an array is a pre-dawn emergency in bad weather. That is also the moment it is most dangerous to put a person in a small boat. Drowning is a real occupational hazard in this sector and it is not hypothetical.

Severity
high
Addressed By
  • carriage and ejection system
  • targeted dosing to twin-identified hotspots

Late Disease Detection

Id
prob-disease-detection
Statement

Disease outbreaks are detected by mortality rather than by behavioural or environmental precursors.

Evidence Summary

Aquaculture disease losses are estimated by FAO at roughly USD 6 billion annually, and single pathogens such as white spot syndrome virus impose losses on the scale of billions of dollars a year in shrimp alone.

Citations
worldbank-disease-risk-2014 asian-fisheries-shrimp-disease
Severity
high
Addressed By
  • surface anomaly detection
  • environmental stress-window flagging
Caveat
The OmniDrone does not diagnose disease. It detects surface anomalies and adverse environmental conditions that warrant human investigation. Claiming diagnosis would be both false and, given that treatment decisions follow, harmful.

Harmful Algal Bloom Blindness

Id
prob-hab
Statement

Blooms are detected when they are visible or when fish die, and their spatial structure — which determines where the danger actually is — is essentially never mapped.

Evidence Summary

Chlorophyll-a is the dominant remote-sensing proxy for HAB detection, used in about 80 percent of studies, and satellite-based systems have demonstrated operational early warning for aquaculture mortality events.

Citations
mdpi-hab-meta-2021 pmc-sentinel-hab-chile-2020
Severity
high
Addressed By
  • multispectral NDCI mapping
  • in-situ fluorometric ground truth
  • phycocyanin discrimination
Caveat
Detects bloom biomass, not toxicity.

Data Fragmentation and Loss

Id
prob-data-fragmentation
Statement

Where data is collected, it lives in paper logbooks and personal memory, is not analysed, does not accumulate, and dies with the notebook.

Expanded

A farm that has operated for fifteen years should have fifteen years of priors on its own water. Almost none do. Every season starts epistemically from zero, and the sector-level consequence is that nobody can build the models that would help, because the training data was never retained.

Severity
medium
Addressed By
  • automated logging
  • digital twin
  • longitudinal analytics

Unmeasured Environmental Externality

Id
prob-environmental-externality
Statement

Aquaculture effluent drives eutrophication of the shared water body, which harms the next farm and eventually the farm that caused it — but nobody measures it, so nobody manages it.

Expanded

This is a commons problem with an information vacuum at its centre. In a shared lake, each operator's stocking decision imposes oxygen demand on everyone. Without measurement there is no basis for allocating carrying capacity, so regulation is either absent or arbitrary, and the lake degrades until it kills everyone's fish at once — which is, empirically, what happens.

Citations
fao-nutrient-dynamics-eutrophic ija-environmental-impacts-ph
Severity
medium
Addressed By
  • multi-farm data
  • carrying capacity analytics
Note

This is the strongest public-good argument for the platform and therefore the strongest argument for government and development-finance interest. It is also the one that requires multi-farm data, which collides directly with farmer data sensitivity. That tension is real and unresolved — see ethics-data-privacy.json.

Who Bears the Cost

Party
Smallholder cage operators
Burden

Most exposed, least equipped. A single kill event can wipe out a year of income and the debt taken to finance the cycle. They are also the least able to buy any solution — which is the central commercial tension of this venture and must not be papered over.

Party
Commercial farms
Burden

Larger absolute losses, more capacity to absorb, more capacity to pay. The realistic first customer.

Party
Feed and fingerling suppliers
Burden
Demand collapses after a regional kill event; carry credit exposure to farms that fail.
Party
Local economies
Burden
Fish kills in lake economies are employment shocks, not just business losses.
Party
Consumers
Burden
Supply shocks and price volatility.
Party
The water body itself
Burden
Bears the cumulative nutrient load; has no representation in any operator's decision.

Counterarguments We Take Seriously

Objection
Farmers already know when their water is bad. They have decades of experience.
Our Response

Largely true, and the product must respect it. Experienced operators read weather, wind, and fish behaviour well. But intuition cannot see a thermocline shoaling three metres down, and it cannot be awake at every cage at 04:00. The claim is not that instruments beat experience — it is that instruments extend it into the depths and hours where experience has no access.

Residual Risk

If experienced operators judge the information redundant, willingness to pay collapses. This is a genuine risk and it is what farmer discovery in Phase 0 must test honestly, including by actively seeking farmers who say no.

Objection

Information is useless without the capacity to respond. A warning at 3 a.m. to a farmer with no emergency aeration just tells them what they are about to lose.

Our Response

This is the most serious objection to the entire venture and we do not have a complete answer. It is why the dispensing subsystem matters: the platform must be able to deliver a response, not only a warning. It is also why the value proposition must be tested against farms that HAVE response capacity first.

Residual Risk
High. Listed as a kill criterion in the Phase 0 gate for exactly this reason.
Objection
A fixed sensor buoy is a tenth of the price.
Our Response

For one point, yes, and for a single small pond a buoy may genuinely be the right answer — we should say so rather than sell a drone. The economics favour the aerial platform when the number of stations times the number of depths times the sampling frequency is large, which is the lake cage array case. Below that threshold we are the wrong product, and pretending otherwise poisons the well.

Residual Risk
Medium. Requires disciplined qualification of prospects.
Objection
The drone will spread disease between farms.
Our Response

Correct, if designed carelessly. This is why decontamination is a hardware requirement rather than a procedure, and why single-site probe assignment is the default operating posture. See biosecurity-protocol.json.

Residual Risk
Medium and manageable, but it must be engineered, and it costs mass and cycle time.

Problem Validation Status

Status
·Literature Validated Field Unvalidated
What Is Established

That fish kills occur, that they cost real money, that the mechanisms are DO depletion and post-overturn toxicity, and that monitoring is inadequate — all supported by published sources.

What Is Not Established

That the specific operators we intend to sell to experience this as their top problem, that they would pay our price, and that they have the response capacity to act on our output. None of these are library questions. They require Phase 0 field discovery.

Intellectual Honesty Note

A problem statement built only from literature is a hypothesis about a market, not evidence of one. This one is currently a hypothesis.
