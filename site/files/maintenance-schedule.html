Maintenance Schedule

maintenance-schedule v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

Note

Intervals are design targets, not field-validated. A tropical, brackish, biologically active environment is harsh on hardware, and real intervals will likely be shorter than these until proven otherwise. Corrosion and fouling do not respect the maintenance manual.

The Three Phase Scope

Statement

This entire schedule is Phase 3 — the hardware/IoT maintenance burden that arrives once a farm adopts OmniDrone, docks, and probes. Phase 1 and Phase 2 carry a real but structurally different maintenance burden: no field technician, no corrosion, no lithium logistics.

Phase 1 2 Software Maintenance
Tasks
  • App release cadence and bug fixes
  • Server/uptime and database maintenance
  • Photo-based disease-ID model retraining as new labelled data accumulates
  • Community forum moderation queue (see ethics-data-privacy.json)
  • Marketplace listing and payment-integration upkeep (Phase 2)
The Honest Comparison

This is engineering and ops overhead, not field-labour overhead — cheaper per user, but it does not go to zero, and moderation/model-retraining in particular scale with user count the way sensor calibration scales with site count in Phase 3.

The Maintenance Philosophy

Principle
Maintainable by a technician with hand tools, or it will not be maintained.
Reasoning

A distributed network of docks on remote lakeshores cannot each have an engineer. If a task needs a laptop, a calibration rig, or a specialist, it will be deferred until it becomes a failure. Design maintenance for the person who is actually there.

The Hidden Failure

The most dangerous maintenance lapse is the one that produces no visible symptom: a fouled sensor drifting, a dock battery cooking in the sun, a corroding connector. These degrade the DATA, not the flight, and nobody notices until a decision is made on bad numbers.

Spares Strategy

Principle

A distributed lake network cannot wait for a part to ship. Downtime during the risk season is when the product most needs to work.

Critical Spares
  • Assigned sonde per site (also a biosecurity requirement)
  • Battery packs
  • Tether and cutter assembly
  • Propellers
  • pH/ISE electrodes
Lithium Logistics Note

Lithium shipping restrictions within the Philippines are real and affect how fast a dead pack can be replaced at a remote dock. Understand this before promising a service level.

The Maintenance Burden As A Business Risk

Statement

Maintenance is where distributed hardware businesses quietly bleed margin, and this one has an unusually heavy burden.

Why Heavy

Weekly sensor calibration and reference checks, corrosion in a brackish tropical environment, a novel high-cycle winch, and a solar dock that cooks its own batteries — each is a recurring field-labour cost. The unit economics in unit-economics.json must carry a realistic maintenance labour line, and the honest version of that line is probably larger than a hardware founder's first instinct.

The Implication

Field operations becomes the biggest function at scale, not engineering. This surprises hardware founders every time, and the ones who do not plan for it discover it in the margin.
