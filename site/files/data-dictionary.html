Data Dictionary — Canonical Field and Variable Definitions

data-dictionary v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 raw JSON ↗

Defines every measured variable the OmniDrone produces, its units, its valid range, its storage type, and its interpretation rules. This is the contract between the flight hardware, the ingestion pipeline, the digital twin, and the dashboard. If a variable is not in this file, it does not exist.

Formula

Interpretation

Supersaturation above ~120% indicates intense photosynthesis and therefore a large algal standing crop — which is a warning of the night-time crash to come, not good news. This inversion catches inexperienced operators out constantly.

Null when the water column is mixed. A rapidly shoaling thermocline across successive profiles is the precursor signature of an overturn event.

Stratification Strength Index

Key
stratification_index
Unit
dimensionless
Formula
Schmidt stability, normalised by water column depth
Interpretation

High stability plus a hypoxic hypolimnion plus an incoming cold front equals the classic Philippine lake fish kill setup. This composite is the OmniDrone's flagship predictive feature.

Hypolimnetic Oxygen Debt

Key
oxygen_debt_index
Unit
mg/L * m
Formula
integral over depth of max(0, DO_threshold - do_mgl) below thermocline
Interpretation

Quantifies the size of the oxygen-poor reservoir waiting to be delivered to the surface by mixing. This converts 'the bottom is anoxic' from a qualitative worry into a magnitude.

Data Quality Flags

Description

Every measurement record carries a QC flag. Adapted from the QARTOD convention used in operational oceanography, which is the mature prior art here.

Citations
qartod-manual
Flags
CodeLabelMeaning
1goodPassed all automated tests.
2not_evaluatedNo QC applied. Must not be used in analysis without review.
3suspectFailed a soft test — e.g. spike test, or rate-of-change test. Show to humans, exclude from automated triggers.
4failFailed a hard test — out of physical range, or sensor fault reported.
9missingExpected but absent.
Mandatory Tests
  • gross_range_test — is the value physically possible at all?
  • climatology_test — is the value plausible for this site, this season?
  • spike_test — is this point wildly inconsistent with its neighbours?
  • rate_of_change_test — did it move faster than physics allows?
  • flat_line_test — has the sensor stopped responding and started repeating? (The failure mode that most resembles good data.)
  • partner_presence_test — for TAN, are pH and temperature present at the same depth and instant?

Integrity Rules

  • A computed field must never be stored without its inputs. If nh3_un_ionised_mgl exists, tan_mgl, ph, and temp_c must exist in the same record.
  • Never overwrite a raw measurement with a corrected one. Corrections are new records referencing the original.
  • A profile with fewer than the configured minimum depth bins is a partial profile and must be flagged as such, not silently averaged into a site statistic.
  • Model outputs and measurements live in separate namespaces. The moment a prediction can be mistaken for an observation, the dataset is compromised.
