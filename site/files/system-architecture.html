System Architecture

system-architecture v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

The Phased Platform

Statement

AquaVision is one platform built in three phases, not three products. The app is the foundation; the hardware is an upgrade layer on top of it, not a separate system.

Why This Ordering

The old architecture started at the drone and worked outward. That was backwards for a company whose primary asset is data about ponds over time. Most farms in the addressable market cannot afford a drone on day one, and most never will need one. Every farm can use a phone. The platform is therefore designed app-first: an account, a farm profile, and a place to log what a farmer already knows how to observe. The IoT/drone stack described later in this file is a Phase 3 extension that feeds richer, higher-frequency, instrument-grade data into the exact same ingestion, storage, and presentation layers a Phase 1 farmer is already using with a pencil-and-paper replacement.

Phases

Phase 1 — Foundation

Product
AquaVision App
What It Is

Free/low-cost mobile and web client: guided farm setup, AI farming assistant, manual/photo water-quality logging, feeding schedules and reminders, photo-based disease identification, community forum, learning modules, record keeping.

Role In Architecture

The acquisition engine and the primary data-entry surface. Every entity in this platform's data model (farm, pond, user) is created here, even for farms that later add sensors.

Phase 2 — Monetization

Product
AquaVision Premium
What It Is

A subscription layer over the same account: analytics engine over logged data, predictive health monitoring, a recommendation engine, a marketplace, and paid expert consultations.

Role In Architecture

Adds compute-heavy and human-in-the-loop services on top of the Phase 1 data model. It does not require new hardware — it requires enough logged history to be useful, which is why Phase 1 must ship first and accumulate data before Phase 2 can be honestly valuable.

Phase 3 — Smart Aquaculture

Product
AquaVision Smart (OmniDrone + IoT probes + PondTwin + Enterprise Dashboard)
What It Is

The full engineering content of this repository's hardware sections: autonomous drone, reeled sonde, docking station, calibrated multispectral imaging, digital twin. Sold as a hardware-as-upgrade add-on to commercial and enterprise farms.

Role In Architecture

Replaces manual log entries with continuous instrument telemetry for the same farm and pond records, and unlocks the twin's simulated layer. Nothing about the underlying platform changes shape — a sensor reading and a manual reading describe the same conceptual entity (a water-quality observation at a pond, at a time) but are never confused for each other in the data model. See telemetry-schema.json.

The Test For Every New Feature

Does this feature need to exist for a farmer with no sensors and no drone? If yes, it belongs in the app/backend layer below, not the IoT layer, even if the eventual enterprise customer will also use it.

Architectural Principles

Principle
The app is the platform; the hardware is an upgrade to it
Implication

Farm, pond, and user records are created and owned in the Phase 1 data model. Phase 3 sensors and drones attach evidence to those same records; they do not spawn a separate system that has to be reconciled later.

Principle
Offline is the normal case, not the exception
Implication

Rural aquaculture sites lose connectivity routinely, for both a farmer's phone and a Phase 3 dock. Every layer must function with the link down and reconcile when it returns. A system that assumes the cloud is a system that fails on the day it matters.

Citations
ai-aqua-review-2024
Principle
Measurements, self-reports, and predictions live in separate namespaces
Implication

A farmer's typed-in pH reading, a calibrated sonde's pH reading, and a model's predicted pH trend are three different kinds of fact with three different confidence levels. The moment any of them can be mistaken for another, the dataset is permanently compromised and every downstream analysis — including the Phase 2 analytics a farmer is paying for — is suspect.

Principle
Raw data is immutable
Implication

Corrections are new records referencing the original, whether the original is a sensor sample or a farmer's manual log entry. Never overwrite a measurement or a log.

Principle
The edge decides what matters; the cloud decides what it means
Implication

For Phase 3: inference and data reduction on board the aircraft; training, twin simulation, and cross-site analytics in the cloud. For Phase 1/2: the client app decides what to ask the farmer and when; the backend decides what the accumulated answers mean.

Principle
The farmer's data belongs to the farmer
Implication

Constrains the architecture at every phase: per-tenant isolation, exportability, and deletion must be real capabilities, not policy statements, for a free Phase 1 account exactly as much as for an enterprise Phase 3 contract. See ethics-data-privacy.json.

Responsibilities

These services read the same farm/pond/log records the Phase 1 services write. They are compute- and judgement-heavier, and several (marketplace, consultations) involve real money and a human expert, which is why they are gated behind a paid tier rather than given away for free.

The Honest Dependency

This layer is only as good as the Phase 1 logging behind it. An analytics engine over a farmer who logged three readings in six months will honestly say so, not paper over the gap with an interpolated-looking chart. See dashboard-spec.json for how sparse-data states are shown.

Raw multispectral imagery is far too large to upload over an intermittent cellular link, and a bloom detection that requires backhaul fails exactly when the network is congested or down.

The Fusion Core

Status
·Phase 3 Subsystem
Why This Is the Architecture and Not A Feature

The probe-calibrates-the-camera insight from solution-overview.json is not a processing step bolted onto a pipeline. It is the reason the Phase 3 hardware upgrade exists, and its architecture should be shaped around it. It sits inside the shared cloud layer above and is invoked only for farms with drone imagery to fuse.

The Flow
  • Imagery arrives as reflectance (already calibrated at the edge against the dock panel and the irradiance log).
  • NDWI masks non-water pixels. Nothing downstream sees a dike or a boat.
  • NDCI computed per water pixel — a relative field, not a concentration.
  • Probe profiles from the same sortie arrive with in-situ chlorophyll at known positions.
  • A local regression fits index to concentration for THIS site, THIS day, THIS illumination.
  • The regression is applied across the masked field, producing a concentration map WITH a propagated uncertainty field.
  • Both the map and its uncertainty enter the twin.
The Critical Design Decision

The regression is site-and-day-local, never global. A single global index-to-concentration model across sites and conditions is the mistake that makes UAV water quality retrievals fail in production. Turbidity, CDOM, and illumination differ enough between two lakes — and between two days on one lake — that a global fit is confidently wrong.

Citations
mishra-ndci-2012 mdpi-drones-inland-review-2024 sciencedirect-uav-multispectral-ponds-2024
The Failure Mode To Guard Against

With few probe stations per sortie, the regression is fitted on three or four points. That is a fragile fit and its confidence interval will often be embarrassingly wide. The honest response is to PROPAGATE that uncertainty into the map and show it, not to hide it behind a smooth colour ramp. A map that looks precise and is not will be believed, and someone will make a decision on it.

The Latency Requirement

From dock to alert, under 5 minutes when connected (Phase 3). For a Phase 1/2 manual entry there is no equivalent hard latency requirement — the entry is a record, not a triggering event — except where a farmer-logged reading crosses a rule-based threshold, in which case the same alerting service used for sensor data evaluates it.

Storage Model

Time Series

Sensor/drone profiles and telemetry, derived indices, AND manually-logged water-quality entries and feeding logs. High write volume for the sensor case, low-and-irregular write volume for the manual case; both append-only.

Spatial

Imagery mosaics, concentration fields, uncertainty fields (Phase 3 only). Large objects, low query rate.

Relational

Users, farms, ponds/units, community posts and threads, learning modules and progress, marketplace listings and orders, consultation bookings, and — for Phase 3 — sites, stations, sondes, sorties, tenants, authorisations.

Immutability

Raw measurements and raw logs are append-only. A correction is a new record with a reference to the original and a reason, whether the original came from a sonde or from a farmer's phone. This is not a purity exercise — it is what makes the dataset defensible in an audit, a dispute, or a customer support conversation.

Retention

Raw data indefinitely, across every phase. It is the asset. For Phase 3 customers the moat is years of sensor profiles on a lake nobody else has measured; for Phase 1/2 customers the moat is the accumulated history of a farm's own logged cycles, which is exactly the dataset Phase 2's analytics and predictive models are trained to need.

The Offline Design

Why It Is A First Class Concern

Most agritech architectures assume connectivity and degrade badly without it. Ours cannot, at any phase — a smallholder's phone on a spotty rural signal has the same problem as a dock on a lakeshore.

Rules
  • The Phase 1 app must let a farmer log water quality, feed, and disease photos with no connection, and sync later without loss or duplication.
  • The dock schedules and flies with no cloud contact. Indefinitely.
  • The local dashboard (app or dock) works with no cloud contact.
  • Alerts based on threshold rules fire locally where possible. Only model-based alerts requiring cloud inference are deferred — which is a good reason to keep the safety-critical alerts rule-based rather than model-based, at every phase.
  • Sync is idempotent and resumable. A phone or a dock that reconnects after four days uploads four days of data without duplication or loss.
  • Clock discipline matters: a client that has been offline still needs accurate timestamps, or the diel and trend analyses are corrupted on reconnection.

Multi Tenancy and Data Boundaries

Requirement

Farm A must never see Farm B's data. Not by convention — by construction. This applies identically to a free Phase 1 account and an enterprise Phase 3 contract.

Why It Is Architectural

Use case UC-10 (lake-scale carrying capacity) requires cross-farm aggregation, and the operator who benefits from the lake-wide analysis is the one who least wants their own stocking visible in it. Community forum posts add a second, softer version of the same tension: farmers want peer advice without exposing their own production numbers. Neither tension is resolvable by a policy document; both must be resolved in the data model.

Approach

Strict per-tenant isolation by default. Community posts are explicitly opt-in public content, separated in the model from a farm's private operational data. Cross-farm analytics operate only on explicitly consented, aggregated, and — where possible — differentially private outputs. The default is no sharing.

The Honest Gap

We have not designed this properly. It is flagged here because it is the kind of thing that is cheap now and impossible later. See ethics-data-privacy.json.

Status
·UNRESOLVED — Architectural Decision Required Before the First Multi Farm Deployment and Before Community Features Scale Beyond A Small Closed Group

Security

Considerations
  • Individual farmer accounts hold personal data (phone numbers, location, farm production data) from the very first Phase 1 signup, not just from enterprise Phase 3 contracts. Account security is not deferred to a later phase.
  • The AI assistant and disease-identification pipeline both ingest farmer photos and messages, which may incidentally contain people, houses, or other personal data.
  • The marketplace and consultation services move money and personal contact information between farmers, vendors, and experts; payment handling should not be built in-house without a clear reason.
  • Command-and-control link integrity for Phase 3 aircraft. An aircraft that accepts a spoofed command is a weapon. Authenticated, encrypted C2 is not optional.
  • The dock is a physically accessible computer on a lakeshore. Assume it will be opened.
  • Farm production data is commercially sensitive at every phase. A breach that reveals a competitor's stocking density, or a farmer's private forum activity, is a real commercial harm to a real customer.
  • Imagery contains people, houses, and boats. It is personal data whether we intended to collect it or not.
  • The dispensing interlock must not be reachable from the network. Ever. An attacker who can dispense is an attacker who can poison a pond.
Status
·Not Designed. Named Here So It Is Not Forgotten. A Security Architecture Retrofitted After the First Deployment — Or the First Thousand Free Tier Signups — Is A Security Architecture That Does Not Exist.

Technology Choices

Status
·DELIBERATELY UNSPECIFIED
Reasoning

Naming a database, a cloud vendor, or a specific LLM provider at this stage is premature and it is the kind of decision that looks like progress while being irrelevant. The architecture above constrains what is needed: a mobile/web client with offline-first sync; conventional relational and object storage for accounts, farms, logs, forum, and marketplace data; an abstracted interface to a third-party LLM for the assistant (so the provider can change without reshaping the product); and, for Phase 3, append-only time series at moderate volume, large object storage, offline-tolerant sync, and edge inference at a few watts. Many stacks satisfy those. Choose when there is a real workload to measure, not before.

The Only Real Constraint

It must run for users in the Philippines with Philippine latency and Philippine data residency expectations, on low-end Android devices and patchy rural connectivity, and it must not assume the site — or the farmer's phone — has internet.
