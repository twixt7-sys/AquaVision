Unit Economics

unit-economics v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ↗Projection ◆Confidence: speculative raw JSON ↗
ℹREAD THIS FIRST

This file restructures unit economics around SaaS metrics — CAC, free-to-paid conversion, LTV, churn, gross margin — as the PRIMARY lens, because Phase 1/2 is now the near-term business and it is a software business with software metrics. The Phase 3 hardware unit economics from the original plan are kept in full underneath, unchanged in substance, as the layer that applies once a customer needs automated physical monitoring. Every number below is a named, tagged assumption or a modeled projection anchored to comparable businesses, not a measured value — there are zero users today, so there cannot yet be a measured conversion rate, churn rate, or CAC. Multiplying unmeasured inputs together produces a confident-looking LTV that is wrong at the root; this file shows the formula and flags every input, so a real number slots in the moment Phase 1 has usage data.

The Saas Unit

Chosen Unit

One registered farmer account (Phase 1/2). A second unit, one dock, governs Phase 3 and is described below unchanged from the original hardware model.

Why the Account

Phase 1/2 cost and revenue both scale with accounts, not with hardware. An account is what a person creates when they download the app, and it is the unit CAC, conversion, and churn are measured against in any comparable freemium business.

Why This Range

This is a widely cited rule-of-thumb range for consumer/prosumer freemium conversion in general software markets. It is NOT derived from any aquaculture- or emerging-market-specific data, and it should be treated as a placeholder to be replaced by measured data within the first few months of Phase 1/2 launch, not as an established benchmark for this product.

The Named Risk

Freemium conversion in a low-and-middle-income, price-sensitive smallholder market may be structurally lower than in software markets the 2-5% heuristic is usually drawn from. If true conversion lands well under 2%, the Phase 2 revenue case does not close on the modeled subscriber counts in financial-projections.json, and pricing, tiering, or the target segment for premium would need to change.

Phase 3 Smart Aquaculture Economics

Carried Forward From the Original Hardware Plan

The structure below is substantively unchanged from the prior single-phase version of this file. It remains the correct model for the Phase 3 hardware-as-a-service layer, and it should NOT be blended with the Phase 1/2 SaaS metrics above — a dock and a free-tier account are different units with different cost drivers.

The Unit
Chosen Unit
One dock serving its cluster of stations.
Why the Dock Not the Drone

Coverage, cost, and revenue all scale with docks, not aircraft. See docking-station.json#/dock_network_economics.

The Implication

Unit economics are DENSITY-driven. A dock over a dense cage cluster and a dock over a sparse lake have the same cost and very different revenue. Site density is a hard qualification criterion, not a preference.

The Cost To Serve Model
Structure

Per-dock, per-period cost = amortised capital + consumables + field labour + platform + regulatory allocation.

The Revenue Per Unit Model
Structure

Per-dock revenue = subscription per site (or per cage) x sites per dock, plus deployment fees and ancillary reporting.

The Density Leverage

Because cost per dock is roughly fixed but revenue scales with stations served, contribution margin per dock rises steeply with cluster density. A cooperative-served bay of many members' cages on one dock is the best economics in the plan; a single farm on one dock is the worst.

Ltv Cac Note
Statement

Phase 3 LTV/CAC cannot be computed and should not be faked, exactly as in the original plan.

Why

LTV needs a price, a margin, and a churn rate; CAC needs a sales cost per closed deal. We have none. Two structural observations still hold: (1) high-touch relationship sales into a distrustful sector means HIGH CAC — not a low-touch SaaS motion; (2) data-compounding value and hardware-in-our-docks lock-in should mean LOW churn once trust is established. High-CAC/low-churn favours a small number of deep, long relationships over broad thin adoption.

?Assumption

The Cross Phase Synergy and Its Risk

The Thesis

Phase 1/2 is meant to make Phase 3 sales cheaper and warmer: a commercial farm that has used the app for a year, trusts the brand, and has an engagement history is a fundamentally easier hardware sale than a cold enterprise lead — solving part of the trust problem before the sales conversation starts.

The Named Risk

This synergy is the single most important UNTESTED assumption added by the phased model. It is entirely plausible that Phase 1/2 users and Phase 3 hardware buyers are simply two different populations (a smallholder using the free app rarely becomes the enterprise operator who buys a dock), in which case Phase 3 must be sold and CAC'd exactly as in the original hardware-only plan, and the 'funnel' framing is more narrative than mechanism.

The Honest Bottom Line

Statement

We do not know if either layer's unit economics close, and we have said so at every layer rather than manufacturing numbers that say they do.

What We Do Know

Phase 1/2 economics hinge almost entirely on a conversion rate borrowed from generic software benchmarks, not measured for this market. Phase 3 economics are density-driven, maintenance-sensitive, and BVLOS-dependent, unchanged from the original hardware plan. The bridge between the two phases is an assumed synergy, not a demonstrated one.

The Immediate Deliverable

Ship Phase 1, measure activation and conversion for real, and replace the SaaS assumptions above with measured numbers before they flow into any funding narrative. This is weeks of product usage, not a research programme — the cheapest and fastest de-risking step available in the whole plan.
