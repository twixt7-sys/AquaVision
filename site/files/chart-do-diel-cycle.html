Why Sampling Time Decides What You See

The diel dissolved oxygen cycle over 24 hours

chart-do-diel-cycle ⚠SAMPLE DATA — NOT REAL raw JSON ↗
boyd-water-quality-2020
⚠SYNTHETIC WARNING

THIS CURVE IS FABRICATED. It is physically plausible but describes no real pond. It illustrates the MECHANISM. The webpage MUST show the illustrative_synthetic badge. Replace with real telemetry at v2.0.0.

Dissolved oxygen peaks in mid-afternoon and bottoms out just before dawn. A manual sample at 9 a.m. reads a safe 6.4 mg/L; the 5 a.m. minimum on the same day is 2.8 mg/L — acute stress. This is the diel sampling bias, and it is the entire argument for autonomous pre-dawn monitoring.

Notes

Threshold lines are INDICATIVE (see water-quality-standards.json) and must be replaced with sourced species-specific values. The teaching value survives even with placeholder thresholds because the point is the SHAPE and the sampling-time bias, not the exact numbers.
