Solution Overview — The AquaVision Platform

solution-overview v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

The Platform In One Paragraph

AquaVision is an AI-powered aquaculture management platform built to serve fish farmers at every stage of the journey, not just the ones who can already afford sensors. Phase 1 is a free-to-low-cost app — an AI farming guide, a farm setup assistant, water quality logging, feeding schedules, photo-based disease identification, a farmer community, learning modules, and record keeping — the on-ramp that brings thousands of beginner and smallholder farmers onto the platform before they can pay for anything. Phase 2 turns that base into a business: a premium subscription layer adds analytics, predictive health monitoring, smart recommendations, a marketplace for feed and equipment and buyers, and paid expert consultations, monetising the farms that have outgrown a notebook. Phase 3 is where the platform grows a body: IoT sensors, in-situ water probes, and the OmniDrone/OmniDock system feed a digital twin (PondTwin) and an enterprise dashboard, giving commercial and enterprise operators automated monitoring and response at a scale no manual round can reach. These are not three products. They are one platform's growth path — from a record-keeping app to an autonomous sensing system — and each phase is only as credible as the data it has honestly earned so far.

Why Phased Rather Than Hardware First

Statement

The old pitch was 'we build a drone.' The new one is 'we build the aquaculture management platform; the drone and IoT sensors are how the platform sees and acts for farms that have outgrown manual logging.'

Reasoning

A hardware-first company has to defer smallholders on affordability grounds, because a drone-and-dock deployment only pencils out at commercial scale. A phased platform does not have that problem: smallholders are the acquisition engine in Phase 1, not a segment deferred until later. Revenue and engineering rigor still ramp with farm size and willingness to pay — they simply ramp inside one continuous product instead of gatekeeping the whole company behind hardware that has not flown.

The Omnidrone System
Note

Everything below this point is the deep engineering content for the Phase 3 hardware layer specifically. It is unchanged from the original hardware-first pitch because it was already rigorous and honest — it is simply now scoped to Phase 3 rather than presented as the whole product.

The System In One Paragraph

The OmniDrone is an autonomous multirotor that launches from a shore-side dock, flies a planned route across a fish farm, and at each station lowers a multiparameter sonde on a powered winch to profile the water column from surface to bottom. While it hovers, a calibrated multispectral and thermal payload images the surrounding water, and the probe's in-situ chemistry calibrates that imagery into real concentrations rather than relative indices. A carriage and ejection system dispenses feed, lime, conditioners, or treatments to specific coordinates. Everything streams to the AquaVision platform, which fuses point profiles with area imagery, drives a digital twin of the water body, and surfaces the result as an answer rather than a dataset.

The Four Functions

Profile — the reeled probe

Function Id
f1-probe
What It Does

Lowers a sonde to measure dissolved oxygen, temperature, pH, ammonia, ORP, turbidity, chlorophyll-a, and conductivity at multiple depths at each station.

Why It Is the Core

Surface measurements cannot see stratification, and stratification is the precondition for the catastrophic failure mode. Every other function on this vehicle could be replicated by a competitor with an off-the-shelf drone. This one could not. If the winch fails, the product is a camera.

Scan — surface anomaly detection

Function Id
f2-scan
What It Does

Images the water surface in RGB, multispectral, and thermal, detecting floating mortalities, fish piping at the surface, oil sheen, foam, algal mats, debris, and net damage above the waterline.

Boundary

Sees the surface. Sunk mortalities and submerged structure are invisible to it, which caps the mortality-detection claim in species whose dead fish sink.

The Architectural Insight
Statement
The probe and the camera are not two features. They are one instrument.
Explanation

A multispectral camera over inland water produces indices — NDCI, NDWI — which are relative numbers, not concentrations. Turning an index into a chlorophyll concentration requires a local regression against real measurements, and that regression is site-specific and drifts with conditions. Almost every UAV water quality study in the literature handles this by sending a boat out with a sonde. That is why almost none of them became products: the boat is the whole cost.

What We Do Differently

The vehicle carries its own ground truth. Every sortie that computes an index also drops the probe, so the calibration is generated in the same place, at the same time, under the same illumination, by the same machine. The point measurement extrapolates across the image; the image tells the point measurement where to look next.

Why This Is the Thesis

This is the defensible scientific contribution and it should be the centre of both the pitch and the dissertation for the Phase 3 offering. Not 'a drone with sensors' — a self-calibrating spatial sensing system where the two halves make each other work. State it in those terms.

Honest Caveat

Unproven. The calibration workflow must survive two different days and two different illumination conditions before this paragraph is anything more than a good idea. That is the Phase 3 hardware's own exit gate, distinct from the Phase 1/2 software gates.

What the System Is Not
Purpose

Volunteering boundaries is the cheapest credibility available. Every item here is something a reader might otherwise assume about the Phase 3 hardware specifically.

Items
Items (expand)
  • Not a fish kill predictor. Not yet. The risk model is unvalidated and will be for at least a full season. Until then the claim is earlier detection, not prediction.
  • Not a toxin detector. It measures pigments correlated with taxa that sometimes produce toxins. A high chlorophyll reading is a reason to sample, not a diagnosis — and people eat the shellfish.
  • Not a disease diagnostic. It detects mortality and stress windows. A vet diagnoses.
  • Not a biomass estimator. No underwater vision. This is a real gap and it will eventually mean integrating with sonar vendors rather than competing with them.
  • Not autonomous in the decisions that matter. It does not medicate a pond on its own authority. Consequential actions stay with the person who owns the consequence.
  • Not a replacement for the farmer's experience. It extends that experience into the depths and hours where it has no access.
  • Not weatherproof. It cannot fly in the storms that trigger overturn events — which is a genuine limitation of the catastrophe-prevention story and the strongest argument for a fixed-buoy hybrid.
  • Not a surveillance platform. The IUU-enforcement application is deliberately not pursued; it would make adversaries of the communities we need as customers.

Why the Dock Matters As Much As the Drone
Note

Under-appreciated in most drone pitches, including, so far, ours.

Reasoning
Reasoning (expand)
  • Autonomy is what makes the pre-dawn sortie possible, and the pre-dawn sortie is where the entire diel-bias argument lives. A drone that needs a human to launch it will never fly at 04:30, which means it will only ever measure the safest hour of the day — exactly the failure it was built to fix.
  • The dock is what lets the probe be dry between sorties, which is why we foul less than a moored sonde.
  • The dock holds the calibrated reflectance panel, without which the multispectral time series is not comparable across days.
  • The dock is the decontamination station, which is what makes multi-site operation biosecure rather than a pathogen distribution service.
  • The dock is the unit of network economics. Coverage is measured in docks, not drones.
Conclusion

Four of the system's five distinguishing claims depend on the dock, not the aircraft. The dock should be treated as a co-equal product, not an accessory.

Operating Concept Summary

Sketch

Dock on shore, solar or grid powered, connected. Scheduled sorties several times daily including pre-dawn. Each sortie: launch, transit, station-keep, profile, image, optionally dispense, next station, return, dock, rinse, charge, upload. Human oversight from the dashboard; human decision on every consequential action; certificated remote pilot in the loop until BVLOS authorisation permits otherwise.
