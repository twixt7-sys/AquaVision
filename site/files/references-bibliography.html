⚠CRITICAL WARNING READ BEFORE CITING
Statement:

Most entries below carry verification_status: cited_from_search_result. That means the bibliographic details came from a search engine's summary and NOBODY HAS OPENED THE SOURCE.

What Could Be Wrong:
  • Author lists may be incomplete or wrong.
  • Years may be the publication year, the online-first year, or the accessed year — these differ and reviewers notice.
  • Volume, issue, and page numbers are frequently absent or wrong.
  • The finding attributed to the source may be a paraphrase of a paraphrase.
  • Some URLs may have rotted since indexing.
  • A few entries below are placeholders for well-known works whose exact details have NOT been checked at all — they are marked needs_verification.
The Rule:

Open every source before it appears in a submitted thesis or an investor document. A reference list you have not read is an academic integrity problem and a diligence liability. This is not pedantry: a committee member who finds one fabricated citation will disbelieve your entire bibliography, and they will be right to.

How To Verify:

Retrieve the DOI, confirm the authors and year against the publisher's page, confirm the finding is actually in the text, and update verification_status to url_checked with an accessed date.

Every citation_id used anywhere in this repository resolves here. Entries carry a verification_status that MUST be read before use.

Citation Style

APA 7th edition. Pre-rendered strings in the `apa7` field are BEST-EFFORT and inherit the verification problem above.
