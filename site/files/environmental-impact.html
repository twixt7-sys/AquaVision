Environmental Impact Assessment

environmental-impact v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ?Assumption ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗

The Principle

A product sold on environmental stewardship must account for its OWN footprint, honestly, including the harms. An environmental impact section that lists only benefits is not an assessment; it is an advertisement.

Phase Framing

Statement

AquaVision is now three layers, and the ENVIRONMENTAL footprint and impact story below belongs almost entirely to Phase 3 (the OmniDrone/IoT/PondTwin hardware layer) — it is unchanged from the original hardware-first assessment and is reproduced as-is under that label.

Phase 1 2 Environmental Footprint
Statement

The Phase 1/2 software layer (free app, premium analytics, marketplace) has a materially different and much smaller direct footprint: mobile/web compute and data storage, not batteries, airframes, or sondes deployed on the water. Its environmental relevance is indirect — better feeding decisions and disease-prevention guidance logged and surfaced to farmers who previously kept no records at all could reduce feed waste and antimicrobial overuse at the behavioural level, before any sensor is involved.

The Honest Caveat

This is a hypothesis about behaviour change, not a measured effect, and it is weaker and slower than the sensor-driven Phase 3 mechanism below because it depends entirely on farmers acting on free-tier guidance with no verification loop. It also inherits the same over-stocking-style tension: better production guidance could as easily support intensification as restraint. No lifecycle assessment of the compute/hosting footprint has been done and is not a near-term priority relative to the Phase 3 hardware assessment below.

Status
·Not Measured, Not Modeled — Noted For Completeness Only

Phase 3 Hardware Environmental Impact

Note

Everything below this point is the ORIGINAL hardware-layer environmental assessment, unchanged in substance, now explicitly scoped to Phase 3 (OmniDrone, docks, IoT sondes, PondTwin) rather than to the company as a whole.

Positive Impacts
Impact
Reduced eutrophication of shared water bodies
Mechanism

Condition-aware feeding reduces uneaten feed, the primary driver of the nutrient loading that degrades the water. Carrying-capacity analytics support collective management.

Citations
fao-nutrient-dynamics-eutrophic ija-environmental-impacts-ph
Magnitude

Potentially significant, UNPROVEN. Depends on farms actually feeding on evidence and on carrying-capacity analytics being adopted.

The Caveat

Undercut by the over-stocking paradox — the same instruments could enable intensification that increases loading. Net effect is genuinely uncertain and must not be claimed as unambiguously positive.

Impact
Reduced chemical and antimicrobial use
Mechanism
Precision placement delivers a given effect with less total substance.
The Caveat

Cuts both ways — easier dosing can increase total use. See ethics-data-privacy.json#/the_antimicrobial_stewardship_tension.

Impact
Earlier pollution detection
Mechanism

Sheen, effluent, and debris detection supports faster response to pollution events in shared waters.

Magnitude
Real but incremental.
Impact
Data for ecosystem-based management
Mechanism

Continuous multi-farm environmental data is exactly what an ecosystem approach to aquaculture (EAA) requires and currently lacks.

Potentially the largest positive impact — but it requires the cross-farm data governance problem to be solved, which is unresolved.

The Net Assessment
Statement

The net environmental impact is genuinely uncertain and depends on adoption patterns we cannot yet predict.

The Honest Reasoning

If the product reduces feed waste and supports carrying-capacity management, the benefit likely exceeds the hardware footprint. If it primarily enables intensification and easier chemical dosing, the net could be negative. The outcome depends on HOW it is adopted and marketed, not just on what it does — which places a real responsibility on the company's own choices.

Why State It This Way

Because claiming a confident net-positive impact at this stage would be exactly the sustainability-washing the field is full of. The intellectually honest position is that the sign of the net impact is a function of the company's own discipline, and that is both true and a stronger statement than a fabricated positive number.

Lifecycle Thinking
Commitment

Before scale, conduct a real lifecycle assessment of the hardware, not a hand-wave. A distributed network of hundreds of drones, docks, and sondes has a material footprint that a single-unit intuition misses.
