Mark Only
Asset
photo_2026-07-17_13-52-22.jpg
Description

The 'A' monogram formed from a triangular peak, with a stylised fish nested inside and a wave sweeping beneath.

Usage

Favicon, app icon, drone fuselage badge, and any context under ~48 px where the wordmark would be illegible.

Symbolism
Triangle

The 'A' of AquaVision, read simultaneously as an aerial vantage point — the drone's-eye view looking down.

Fish In Eye Form

The fish outline doubles as an eye. Vision and the subject of vision are the same shape: the point of the product is seeing the fish.

Wave

The water surface — the boundary the whole product is about crossing, since everything that matters happens beneath it.

Note For Pitch

This is a genuinely good mark for the story at every phase, not just Phase 3: it encodes 'seeing beneath the water' whether that seeing comes from a farmer's own logged photo in the free app or from an OmniDrone sortie later. Use it as the visual spine of the deck.

Clear Space
Minimum clear space on all sides equal to the height of the wave element.
Misuse
  • Do not recolour the mark outside the palette.
  • Do not place the mark on a mid-tone blue background where the gradient loses contrast.
  • Do not stretch, rotate, or add effects.
  • Do not separate the fish from the triangle.

Wordmark

The logo wordmark is a wide-tracked geometric sans in light weight. Match its spirit, do not attempt to recreate it in body copy.

'Inter Tight', 'Inter', system-ui, sans-serif — tracked wide at large sizes to echo the wordmark.

Product Design Language

Scope Note

This section describes the Phase 3 OmniDrone hardware specifically — the smart-aquaculture upgrade layer, not the company's core product. The Phase 1 app and Phase 2 premium tier are screens, not hardware, and should be designed against the same palette and typography below rather than against this section.

Source
Derived from the supplied render (photo_2026-07-17_13-52-27.jpg).
Observed Cues
  • Ducted rotors in full circular shrouds — reads as safe-to-approach, which matters for a machine that will operate near people on narrow dikes and small boats.
  • Soft white shell with pale cyan accents and integrated light strips — marine instrument, not military hardware. Deliberate distance from the black-quadcopter aesthetic.
  • Twin ventral pods flanking a centre-line probe — the payload architecture is legible from the outside.
  • Sprawled quadrupod landing gear with wide feet — designed for soft mud dikes and boat decks.
  • Gimballed sensor head forward of centre.
  • Reeled probe deployed on a visible tether beneath the belly — the signature silhouette of the product.
Design Principles
  • Legibility over stealth. A farmer, a neighbour, and a regulator should all be able to see what this machine is doing and infer that it is benign.
  • The shrouds are a functional safety choice first and a brand cue second. Do not let a future weight-saving exercise quietly delete them without re-running the safety case.
  • White shell is a thermal decision in a tropical deployment as much as an aesthetic one.
  • The tether should read as intentional. It is the product's most distinctive feature and its hardest engineering problem.
Honest Note
The render is an industrial design study, not an engineering drawing. Rotor sizing, pod volume, and gear geometry in the image have not been validated against the mass and power budgets in data/05_hardware/. Where the render and the specification disagree, the specification is not automatically right either — both need reconciling before tooling.

Voice and Tone

Principles
  • Concrete over aspirational. 'Profiles dissolved oxygen every two hours at four depths' beats 'revolutionises aquaculture'.
  • Respect the operator's expertise. The farmer has known this water for twenty years. The product supplies instruments, not wisdom.
  • Never dramatise a fish kill. Real people lose real money and sometimes their livelihood. Describe it accurately and let the facts carry the weight.
  • State uncertainty out loud. The brand's differentiator is trustworthiness of measurement; hedging language used honestly is an asset, not a weakness.
Avoid
  • 'Revolutionary', 'game-changing', 'disruptive' — say what it does instead.
  • Implying the system predicts fish kills, until a validated model exists. This is the single most tempting and most dangerous overclaim available to this company.
  • Implying the system detects toxins. It detects pigments correlated with taxa that sometimes produce toxins. The gap between those two statements is where lawsuits live.
  • Military or surveillance framing. It is corrosive to farmer trust and it invites the IUU-enforcement association the company should keep at arm's length.
  • Claiming the free app already has farmers, downloads, or traction before that data exists. See traction.json — the same honesty discipline that applies to the OmniDrone's TRL applies to the app's user count.

Ui Guidance For the Webpage

Layout

Data-dense but calm. This is an instrument, not a landing page. Whitespace between sections, tight within tables.

Dark Mode

Required. Operators check the dashboard before dawn — that is when the DO minimum occurs — and a white screen at 04:30 is genuinely hostile.

The Water Metaphor

Depth is the organising spatial idea. Use vertical position to mean depth wherever a profile is shown, and never invert the axis to make a chart look conventional.

Chart Defaults
Series Colors
  • --av-ocean
  • --av-current
  • --av-shallow
  • --av-deep
  • --av-slate
Threshold Lines

Always drawn from --av-warning or --av-critical, dashed, labelled with the threshold value and its citation.

Missing Data

Render gaps as gaps. Never interpolate across a sensor outage — a smooth line through missing data is a lie the eye believes.
