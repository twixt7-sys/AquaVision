ℹREAD THIS FIRST

NO COMPONENT HAS BEEN SOURCED. NO SUPPLIER HAS BEEN CONTACTED. NO PRICE HAS BEEN QUOTED. Every figure below is an order-of-magnitude placeholder based on general knowledge of component categories. The correct use of this file is as a STRUCTURE to fill in during Phase 0 vendor engagement, and as an honest statement of what we do not know. Any BOM cost that reaches an investor deck must come from real quotes. A fabricated BOM is the fastest way to build a financial model that is wrong at the foundation, and every number downstream inherits the error.

Platform Context

This BOM covers OmniDrone/OmniDock, the hardware components of AquaVision Smart (Phase 3) — the premium hardware upgrade layer of the AquaVision platform sold to commercial/enterprise farms that have outgrown the app-based Phase 1/2 tiers. It is a cost basis for an optional add-on layer, not for AquaVision's core (software) product.

Why the Range Is So Wide

The sonde alone could be USD 3,000 or USD 12,000, and might have to be developed rather than bought. Until that one line is resolved by an actual vendor conversation, the total is not meaningful to within a factor of two.

What Is NOT In This Number
  • The dock. A separate BOM that does not exist yet and is a co-equal product.
  • Assembly labour.
  • Non-recurring engineering — the winch development alone is substantial.
  • Certification and testing.
  • Tooling.
  • Spares and consumables.
  • The per-site sonde required by single-site biosecurity assignment. Every site needs its own probe, and at the sonde's price that is a significant and easily-missed capital line.
The Honest Statement

The aircraft component cost is plausibly USD 15-40k at prototype quantity. Fully-loaded system cost including the dock, NRE, and per-site sondes is unknown and is probably several times this. Any unit economics built on the number above is built on sand — see unit-economics.json, which says the same thing.

Supply Chain Considerations

Consideration
Sensor vendor lock-in
Risk

A probe architecture locked to one vendor's sonde hands them our margin permanently. They will notice.

Mitigation

Design for sensor interchangeability from day one, even at a mass and complexity cost. This is a strategic decision, not an engineering preference.

Consideration
Chinese component dependency
Risk

Airframe components, motors, and flight controllers are cheap and abundant from Chinese suppliers, with geopolitical and export-control exposure attached. Some customers — particularly government ones — may have procurement restrictions.

Mitigation
Know the origin of every part before a government customer asks.
Consideration
Lead times
Risk

Specialist sensors have long lead times that will govern the prototype schedule regardless of how fast the team works.

Mitigation
Order the sonde first. It is the long pole and it is also the biggest unknown.
Consideration
Battery logistics
Risk

Lithium shipping restrictions to and within the Philippines are real and they affect spares logistics for a distributed dock network.

Mitigation
Understand this before promising a service level.

The Phase 0 Action

Task
Replace every line in this file with a real quote from a real supplier.
Effort
Days of phone calls and emails. Not research.
Why It Matters Disproportionately

The BOM feeds the unit economics, which feeds the pricing, which feeds the financial model, which feeds the funding ask. An error here propagates through every commercial document the company produces. It is also the single cheapest way to convert this repository's largest guess into a fact.

The First Call

A sonde vendor. Ask what their lightest unit with optical DO and fluorometric chlorophyll weighs and costs. That one answer resolves the mass budget, the BOM, and possibly the viability of the whole concept.
