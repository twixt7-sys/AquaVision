AI / ML Model Cards

ai-ml-models v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ◎Target raw JSON ↗
mitchell-model-cards-2019
ℹSTATUS BANNER

NO MODEL HAS BEEN TRAINED. NO MODEL HAS BEEN EVALUATED. This is as true of the Phase 1 photo-based disease model and the Phase 2 predictive-health model as it is of the Phase 3 chlorophyll retrieval — AquaVision has no real users yet (see traction.json), so none of these models has real usage data to learn from. Every `performance` field below is null and every `target_performance` field is an aspiration. These are specifications for models we intend to build, written before the data exists, so that the evaluation criteria are fixed before anyone has an incentive to move them.

Convention

Follows the Model Cards convention (Mitchell et al., 2019). Every card carries an out_of_scope_use field, because a model card without one is marketing.

Citation Ids

  • mitchell-model-cards-2019

The Governing Principle

Statement

The order in which these models ship is a safety decision, not a product decision — and that is true within each phase, not just once.

Reasoning

The models are ordered below from 'safe to be wrong' to 'dangerous to be wrong', separately for the Phase 1/2 app-layer models and the Phase 3 sensor/drone models. A misclassified piece of debris costs a farmer thirty seconds of annoyance. A confidently wrong 'your fish are fine' from a photo, or a false negative from the kill-risk model, reads as permission and could cost a harvest. The temptation will be to ship the exciting one first because it demos well. Resist it.

The Rule
No model ships until its failure mode has been costed in the customer's currency, not ours.

Phase Note

Statement

Model cards below are tagged with the product phase whose feature they support. Phase 1/2 models run on data a farmer typed in or photographed with a phone; Phase 3 models run on calibrated sensor and drone telemetry. Where a Phase 1/2 model and a Phase 3 model address a similar question (feeding, health risk), the cards say explicitly how they differ and why both are worth building rather than one replacing the other.

Phase 1
AquaVision App — free/low-cost tier.
Phase 2
AquaVision Premium — paid subscription tier, built on Phase 1 data.
Phase 3
AquaVision Smart — OmniDrone/IoT hardware upgrade tier.

Models

Photo-Based Disease & Stress Identification

Id
disease-identification-photo
Task

From one or more farmer-submitted photos of fish or shrimp, suggest candidate visible conditions (lesions, fin/tail rot, parasite load, discoloration, abnormal behaviour captured in a short clip) with a confidence per candidate.

Phase
1
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate

Image classification model, likely a fine-tuned convolutional network or a smaller vision-language model, transfer-learned from public aquaculture/veterinary imagery datasets where they exist. Exact architecture deliberately unspecified until a labelled dataset plan is real.

Why It Is First In the App Tier

It is the single feature most likely to convert a skeptical smallholder into a daily user, and it is also the app-tier model with the most direct path to real harm if it is confidently wrong, so it gets the same ordering discipline as the Phase 3 models below.

Intended Use

Give a smallholder a fast, non-diagnostic first read on a visibly abnormal fish or shrimp, with a suggested next step: keep monitoring, post to the community for peer input, or (Premium) book a paid expert consultation.

Out of Scope Use
  • NOT a veterinary diagnosis. A model output is a triage suggestion, not a clinical finding.
  • NOT a basis for medication dosing or treatment decisions on its own.
  • NOT validated across species, regions, or the full range of conditions a farmer may encounter — coverage starts narrow (a handful of common, visually distinct presentations) and is stated as narrow.
  • NOT a substitute for mandatory disease reporting obligations where a suspected condition is notifiable to authorities — the product must route those cases to a human, not resolve them silently.
Inputs
  • Farmer-submitted photo(s) or short video clip
  • Self-reported species
  • Optional symptom checklist
Outputs
  • Ranked candidate conditions with calibrated confidence
  • Suggested next action
  • Automatic flag to expert consultation when confidence is low or the condition is potentially reportable
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

No labelled photo dataset exists yet. Setting an accuracy target now would be inventing a number. The near-term goal is narrow, honest coverage of the most common presentations, not comprehensive diagnosis, and the model should say 'unclear, consult a human' far more often than a flashy demo would prefer.

Known Failure Modes
  • Poor photo quality — blur, bad lighting, wrong angle — is the normal case on a low-end phone camera, not an edge case
  • Visually similar presentations with different underlying causes
  • Severe class imbalance: common, mild conditions will vastly outnumber rare, serious ones in any early dataset
  • Species and regional variation in how a condition presents
  • A confidently wrong 'looks healthy' verdict is more dangerous than an honest 'unclear' — it can delay treatment or a report a farmer would otherwise have filed
Fairness and Harm Considerations

Photos submitted by farmers may incidentally include people, hands, or property. Training data handling must follow the same imagery privacy discipline as the Phase 3 drone footage. See ethics-data-privacy.json.

Human Oversight

Always advisory. Every result carries a route to a human — a community post at minimum, a paid expert at the Premium tier — and the model never issues a treatment instruction on its own.

Citations
asian-fisheries-shrimp-disease worldbank-disease-risk-2014

AI Farming Assistant (Conversational)

Id
ai-farming-assistant
Task

Answer a farmer's natural-language questions about fish farming — species selection, pond setup, water quality basics, feeding, common problems — and help them navigate the app.

Phase
1
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate

Orchestration layer over a third-party large language model, grounded with retrieval over AquaVision's own learning-module content and, where relevant, the farmer's own logged farm data. Provider deliberately unspecified — see system-architecture.json#/technology_choices.

Intended Use

A conversational front door for the Phase 1 app: answering general aquaculture questions, guiding a new user through farm setup, and pointing an existing user to the right feature (log water quality, check a learning module, post to the forum) rather than trying to be the authority on everything.

Out of Scope Use
  • NOT a source of specific medication dosing, chemical treatment instructions, or veterinary diagnosis — those questions must be redirected to the disease-identification flow, the community, or a paid expert, not answered directly by the assistant.
  • NOT a substitute for the water-quality logging or disease-ID features. If a farmer describes a dying pond in chat, the assistant's job is to route them to the right tool and, where warranted, to an urgent human channel — not to reason it out unaided.
  • NOT authoritative on anything the assistant cannot ground in AquaVision's own content or the farmer's own data. An LLM that free-associates plausible-sounding aquaculture advice is a liability, not a feature.
Inputs
  • Farmer's chat messages
  • Retrieved learning-module content
  • The farmer's own farm/pond records where the farmer has granted access
Outputs
  • Conversational responses
  • Suggested in-app actions
  • Escalation prompts to community, disease-ID, or expert consultation
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

No usage data exists. The near-term target is behavioural, not accuracy-based: the assistant should decline or redirect out-of-scope questions (dosing, diagnosis) at a very high rate, even at the cost of feeling less 'smart' in a demo.

Known Failure Modes
  • Confident hallucination on aquaculture facts outside its grounded content
  • Users treating conversational fluency as domain authority ('it talks like an expert, so it must be one')
  • Prompt injection or manipulation attempts via chat
  • Silent drift in answer quality when the underlying third-party model is updated by its provider
Human Oversight

Every response involving health, dosing, or treatment carries a visible disclaimer and a link to a human channel. Conversation logs are reviewable so that systematically wrong answers can be caught and the grounding content corrected.

Feeding Schedule & Ration Recommendation (Logged-Data)

Id
feeding-schedule-recommendation
Task

Suggest a feeding schedule and ration size from a farmer's self-reported species, stocking density, growth stage, and logged feeding history — without any water-quality sensor input.

Phase
1
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate

Rule-based recommendation from published species/stage feeding tables initially, refined by the farmer's own logged feed-to-growth outcomes over time where those outcomes are recorded.

How This Differs From the Phase 3 Feeding Advisor

This model has no water-condition telemetry at all — it works from what a Phase 1 farmer has actually recorded: species, stocking, and feed logs. The Phase 3 'feeding-advisor' card below answers a different, narrower question (defer or reduce a specific feeding given today's measured oxygen) for farms with sensors. Both can coexist for a farm that upgrades: the schedule recommendation sets the plan, the condition-aware advisor overrides it on a bad-water day.

Why Rules First

Species feeding tables are published and interpretable. A learned model needs outcome data (growth, FCR) tied to feeding history, which does not exist yet for any Phase 1 farm.

Citations
innovasea-fcr-guide boyd-water-quality-2020
Intended Use

Populate sensible default feeding schedules and reminders during farm setup, and suggest adjustments as a farmer logs feed and, optionally, growth or mortality observations.

Out of Scope Use
  • NOT a growth or FCR guarantee.
  • NOT a substitute for species-specific expert guidance in unusual conditions.
  • NOT aware of water quality unless the farmer also logs it manually — a recommendation made without any water-quality context should say so.
Inputs
  • Self-reported species and stocking data
  • Feeding logs
  • Optional manual water-quality entries
  • Optional growth/mortality observations
Outputs
  • Suggested feeding schedule (times, ration)
  • Suggested adjustment with plain-language reasoning
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

Cannot be set responsibly before real logged outcome data exists across at least one growth cycle.

Known Failure Modes
  • Self-reported stocking counts and species are often estimates, and the recommendation is only as good as those inputs
  • No signal at all when a farmer stops logging — the model must say 'insufficient recent data' rather than keep repeating a stale schedule with unchanged confidence
  • Regional and strain variation in feeding response not captured by generic published tables
Human Oversight

Advisory only. Never assumes silent compliance — the farmer confirms or logs an actual feeding, and a missed confirmation is itself a signal shown back to the farmer, not hidden.

Predictive Health / Anomaly Monitoring (Logged-Data)

Id
predictive-health-anomaly
Task

Estimate whether a farm's recent pattern of manually logged water-quality readings, feeding, and mortality looks anomalous relative to that farm's own history, as an early-warning signal for the Premium tier.

Phase
2
Status
·Concept — NOT IMPLEMENTED, NOT VALIDATED, MUST NOT SHIP
The Warning
Statement

This is the Phase 2 sibling of the Phase 3 kill-risk-forecast model below, and it inherits the same danger, in a harder form, because its inputs are sparser and noisier.

Why

A Phase 1/2 farm has no continuous sensor stream — it has whatever the farmer remembered to type in, whenever they remembered to type it. A false 'no anomaly detected' on that thin a data diet reads as reassurance the model has no real basis for. A farmer who skipped logging for two weeks and sees a calm status tile has been given false comfort by an absence of data, not a finding.

The Asymmetry

Without this model, a Premium farmer knows their own records are incomplete and stays appropriately uncertain. With a poorly-caveated model, they may substitute the tile's calm colour for their own judgement. The model must make data sparsity itself a visible, first-class output — not a caveat buried in a tooltip.

Architecture Candidate

Anomaly detection over the farm's own logged time series (statistical baseline per farm initially; a learned model only once enough farms have enough logged history to train and, critically, to validate on held-out farms rather than held-out time windows of the same farm).

Relationship To Phase 3 Kill Risk Forecast

If a farm upgrades to Phase 3 sensors, this model is superseded by the richer, continuously-observed kill-risk-forecast model, which has an actual physical basis (stratification, oxygen debt) that this logged-data model cannot see. This model exists specifically for farms that have not upgraded, and its outputs should be visibly less confident than the Phase 3 model's for that reason.

Inputs
  • Manually logged water-quality entries
  • Feeding logs
  • Mortality/observation logs
  • Logging frequency and recency itself, as a feature
Outputs
  • Anomaly flag relative to the farm's own history
  • Data-completeness score alongside the flag
  • Plain-language description of what changed, not just a score
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

Cannot be set responsibly before real logged data and real outcomes exist across multiple farms and at least one full cycle.

The Real Bar

It must not be shown at all for a farm whose logging is too sparse to support any signal. A model that fills that gap with an interpolated-looking 'all normal' tile is worse than showing nothing.

Required Evidence

An honest evaluation against farms' real outcomes, including the anomalies it missed, before this graduates past 'concept'.

Known Failure Modes
  • Sparse, irregular logging is the default case for a free-to-premium-converting farmer, not an edge case
  • Rare events (real health crises) are rare in any farmer's own history, so a per-farm baseline has very little positive signal to learn from
  • A farmer who logs more when things are going badly and less when things are fine will bias the model toward mistaking 'no recent logs' for 'no problem'
  • No physical mechanism behind the signal, unlike the Phase 3 model — it is pattern-matching on sparse self-report, and it should never be presented with a false sense of the same rigor
Human Oversight

Until validated: show the underlying logged trend in plain language and let the farmer, or a consulted expert, draw the conclusion — exactly the interim posture the kill-risk-forecast model below takes, and for the same reason.

The Interim Product

'You have not logged dissolved oxygen in 11 days, and your last two entries were trending down' is a true, useful statement that requires no model and carries no false certainty. Ship that before any anomaly score.

Water Masking (NDWI threshold)

Id
water-masking
Task
Separate water pixels from land, vegetation, structure, and boats.
Phase
3
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate
Threshold on NDWI, with a learned refinement only if thresholding proves inadequate.
Why It Is First

Everything downstream depends on it. A dike pixel entering a chlorophyll statistic corrupts the statistic silently.

Intended Use
Preprocessing for every imagery product.
Out of Scope Use
Not a shoreline mapping tool. Not for land use classification.
Inputs
  • Green reflectance
  • NIR reflectance
Outputs
  • Binary water mask
  • Mask confidence
Citations
mcfeeters-ndwi-1996
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

High recall on water, high precision on exclusion. Prefer excluding an ambiguous pixel over including it — a smaller, cleaner mask beats a larger, contaminated one.

Known Failure Modes
  • Cage netting and floats within a water body
  • Shadow
  • Dense floating algal mat, which may read as vegetation and be masked out — precisely the thing we most want to measure
  • Turbid shallow margins
The Interesting Failure

A thick surface algal mat can be misclassified as vegetation and masked out of the analysis. The bloom disappears from the chlorophyll map exactly when it is worst. This is a known NDWI pathology and it must be explicitly tested for, not discovered in production.

Human Oversight
Mask visible and overridable in the UI.

Chlorophyll-a Retrieval (NDCI + local regression)

Id
chla-retrieval
Task
Convert per-pixel NDCI into estimated chlorophyll-a concentration with uncertainty.
Phase
3
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate

Site-and-day-local regression of NDCI against in-situ fluorometric chlorophyll from the same sortie. Start with linear or simple nonlinear; do not reach for a neural network to fit four points.

Why Not A Global Model

Turbidity, CDOM, and illumination differ enough between lakes — and between two days on the same lake — that a global fit is confidently wrong. The literature is consistent on this and it is the main reason UAV retrievals fail in production.

Citations
mishra-ndci-2012 mdpi-drones-inland-review-2024 sciencedirect-uav-multispectral-ponds-2024
Intended Use

Spatial mapping of algal biomass across a water body, calibrated by same-sortie ground truth.

Out of Scope Use
  • NOT a toxicity measurement. Chlorophyll indicates algal biomass, not toxin presence. A high reading is a reason to sample, not a diagnosis.
  • NOT valid outside the range of the in-situ points it was fitted on. Extrapolating a three-point regression beyond its calibration range is fabrication.
  • NOT comparable across sites without refitting.
Inputs
  • NDCI field
  • Water mask
  • In-situ chlorophyll at known stations
  • Turbidity as covariate
Outputs
  • Concentration field
  • Per-pixel uncertainty field
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

To be set against the reference sonde in Phase 3. Setting a target accuracy now, before any data, would be inventing a number.

Known Failure Modes
  • Sun glint contamination saturating the signal
  • Fitting on 3-4 points produces a fragile regression with wide confidence intervals — this is the normal case, not an edge case
  • High turbidity decoupling the red-edge signal from chlorophyll
  • Extrapolation beyond the calibration range
The Honest Problem

With only three or four probe stations per sortie, the regression will often be poorly constrained. The correct response is to show the wide uncertainty, not to hide it behind a smooth colour ramp. A precise-looking map that is not precise will be believed, and a farmer will act on it.

Human Oversight

Uncertainty field always rendered. Maps below a confidence threshold are shown as 'insufficient calibration' rather than as a map.

Surface Anomaly Detection

Id
surface-anomaly
Task

Detect and classify floating mortalities, surface-piping fish, oil sheen, foam, algal mats, debris, and above-water net damage.

Phase
3
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate
Small-object detection CNN, quantised for edge inference at a few watts.
Intended Use
Flag surface anomalies for human review, geolocated to a specific cage.
Out of Scope Use
  • NOT a mortality count. It detects floating fish; it does not count stock.
  • NOT a disease diagnosis.
  • NOT for assessing personnel performance. This is a hard rule — see the technician persona in customer-personas.json.
Inputs
  • RGB imagery at <=15 mm GSD
Outputs
  • Detections with class, calibrated confidence, and geolocation
  • Image crop as evidence
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

Precision matters far more than recall here, and this is counterintuitive.

Reasoning

A missed dead fish is found on the next sortie two hours later. A false alarm at 04:30 that drags a manager out of bed for nothing destroys trust in the whole system, and the third false alarm means the tenth alert — the real one — is ignored. Alert fatigue is not a UX problem; it is a safety failure. Tune for precision and accept lower recall.

Target Precision
>= 0.90
Target Recall
>= 0.60
Status
·Targets Only — No Data Exists
Known Failure Modes
  • Sun glint mimicking sheen
  • Floating debris versus fish
  • Wave texture as false positives
  • Birds
  • Species and lighting variation between sites
The Generalisation Problem

A model trained on one lake will likely fail on another — different water colour, different light, different debris, different species. The Phase 3 gate deliberately requires evaluation on a HELD-OUT SITE, not a held-out split of the same site. A random split of one lake's imagery proves nothing about generalisation and would flatter the model badly.

Fairness and Harm Considerations

The imagery contains people, boats, and houses. The model must not be extended to person detection, and the training data pipeline must not retain identifiable imagery of individuals. See ethics-data-privacy.json.

Human Oversight

Every detection routes to human confirmation with the image crop attached. A bare classification with no evidence is unactionable and will be ignored. Rejections become training data.

Stratification State Estimation

Id
stratification-state
Task
Compute thermocline depth, Schmidt stability, and hypolimnetic oxygen debt from a profile.
Phase
3
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate
Deterministic computation, not machine learning.
Why Not Ml

This is established limnological physics with a century of literature behind it. Using a learned model where a physical formula exists is worse in every dimension: less accurate, less interpretable, needs training data we do not have, and fails unpredictably outside its training distribution. The instinct to apply ML to everything should be resisted precisely here, where the physics is known.

Citations
idso-schmidt-stability-1973
Intended Use
Quantify the water column's structure and the size of the oxygen-poor reservoir below.
Out of Scope Use
Not a prediction. It describes the current state.
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

Correctness is verifiable against the reference sonde. This is a computation, not an estimate.

Known Failure Modes
  • Partial profiles — a profile that stopped at 5 m in a 12 m column cannot compute stability and must be flagged, not silently averaged
  • Weak gradients where the thermocline is genuinely ambiguous
Human Oversight
Profile chart always available. The number never appears without the shape it came from.

Composite Fish Kill Risk Forecast

Id
kill-risk-forecast
Task
Estimate the probability of a mass mortality event in a defined forward window.
Phase
3
Status
·Concept — NOT IMPLEMENTED, NOT VALIDATED, MUST NOT SHIP
The Warning
Statement

This is the most dangerous artefact in the entire product, and it is the one everyone will ask for first.

Why

A false negative from this model does not read as 'no prediction available'. It reads as 'you are safe'. A farmer who defers running aerators because our score said 12/100 and then loses a harvest has been harmed BY US. We would have converted their uncertainty — which they knew how to live with — into a false certainty that they acted on.

The Asymmetry

Without the model, the farmer is uncertain and cautious. With a wrong model, the farmer is confident and wrong. We can make things worse than doing nothing, which is not true of any other model in this file.

The Commercial Pressure

This score is the single most compelling thing that could appear in a demo. Investors will ask for it. Customers will ask for it. The pressure to ship a plausible-looking version trained on thin data will be intense and continuous. This paragraph exists to be read on the day that pressure arrives.

Architecture Candidate

Ensemble over stratification index, oxygen debt, chlorophyll trend, diel amplitude, weather forecast, and stocking density.

Inputs
  • Stratification state time series
  • Oxygen debt
  • Chlorophyll trend
  • Diel DO amplitude
  • Weather forecast
  • Stocking density (operator-supplied)
Outputs
  • Risk score
  • Contributing factor attribution
  • Uncertainty
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

Cannot be set responsibly before seeing real events.

The Real Bar

It must beat the experienced farmer's own judgement. If it cannot, it should not ship at any accuracy — an instrument that is worse than the expert it advises is a liability with a subscription fee.

Required Evidence

An honest confusion matrix against real events across at least one full seasonal cycle, including the events it missed.

Known Failure Modes
  • Trained on too few events — mass kills are rare, so the positive class is tiny and the model will learn to always say 'no' and score 97 percent accurate. This is the classic rare-event trap and it will happen.
  • Site-specific dynamics that do not transfer between lakes
  • The trigger is usually a storm, and we cannot fly in storms, so our observations stop precisely when the event fires
Human Oversight

Until validated: the system shows the MECHANISM — the stratification, the oxygen debt, the forecast — in plain language, and lets the farmer draw the conclusion. Given their expertise, that is probably better than a score anyway, and it is certainly more honest.

The Interim Product

'The bottom three metres are anoxic and rain is forecast tonight' is a true statement, derived from measurement, that an experienced operator can act on. It requires no model, carries no false certainty, and is available on day one. Ship that.

Condition-Aware Feeding Advisor

Id
feeding-advisor
Task
Recommend whether to feed, defer, or reduce ration based on current water conditions.
Phase
3
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate

Rule-based initially, from published species thresholds. Learned refinement only once real FCR outcome data exists.

How This Differs From the Phase 1 Schedule Recommendation

This model reacts to measured water conditions (dissolved oxygen, temperature) on a sensor-equipped farm. The Phase 1 'feeding-schedule-recommendation' card above has no water-condition input at all and instead works from logged history. A farm that upgrades keeps both: the Phase 1 model sets the baseline plan, this model overrides it on a bad-water day.

Why Rules First

The physiology is known: fish in low DO do not convert feed, and feeding into low oxygen adds organic load to water that is already short. A rule encoding that is interpretable, debuggable, and correct on day one. A learned model would need outcome data we will not have for years and would be less explainable to the person deciding.

Citations
innovasea-fcr-guide boyd-water-quality-2020
Out of Scope Use
Not a feed formulation tool. Not a growth model.
Human Oversight
Advisory only. It never withholds feed autonomously.
Known Limitation

We infer appetite from water condition. Incumbent systems watch the fish eat with an underwater camera, which is a direct observation and strictly better feedback. Our advantage is coverage across forty cages, not fidelity in one.

Cross Cutting Commitments

Commitment
Calibrated confidence, not raw softmax
Why

An uncalibrated confidence shown as a percentage is decoration that looks like information. If we cannot calibrate it, we do not show a number. This applies to a disease-ID photo result exactly as much as to a chlorophyll map.

Commitment
Explainability is load-bearing, not a nice-to-have
Why

An alert that tells a farmer to spend money on emergency aeration will be ignored unless it says why. XAI here is a commercial requirement before it is an ethical one.

Citations
ai-aqua-review-2024
Commitment
Evaluate on held-out FARMS or SITES, not held-out splits of the same one
Why

Random splits of one lake's data, or one farm's logging history, measure memorisation, not generalisation, and they will flatter every model in this file.

Commitment
Publish the failures
Why

Commitment
Data sparsity is itself a first-class output
Why

A Phase 1/2 model built on whatever a farmer remembered to log must say when it does not have enough recent data to say anything, rather than silently reusing a stale, falsely confident state.

The Data Problem

Statement

We have no training data at any phase, and the most valuable models in each phase need the data that is hardest to get.

The Arithmetic

For Phase 3, mass kills happen a few times a year per lake — a supervised model needs many examples and even a multi-year, multi-site deployment yields tens of positive examples at best. For Phase 1/2, the constraint is different but just as real: there are zero real users today, so the disease-ID, assistant, and predictive-health models have no labelled photos, no conversation logs, and no logged farm histories to learn from until the app actually ships and people actually use it.

Implications
  • Physics-informed approaches beat pure data-driven ones for the Phase 3 rare-event problem. We know the mechanism; we should encode it rather than hope to learn it from a handful of examples.
  • Rule-based, published-threshold approaches beat premature learned models for the Phase 1 feeding and predictive-health problems too, for the same reason: the physiology and the arithmetic of sparse self-reported data are both known, and pretending a model has learned something from a dozen logged entries is the same overclaiming mistake in a different phase.
  • The chronic sub-lethal hypothesis from cost-of-inaction.json is far more tractable ML than a rare-event forecast — continuous outcomes, abundant data, every cycle a sample. If that hypothesis is right, it is both the better business AND the better machine learning problem, for Phase 2's analytics as much as for Phase 3's. That coincidence is worth noticing.
  • Phase 1 is, among other things, the mechanism by which Phase 2 and Phase 3 models eventually get real training data. A free tier that nobody uses produces no data flywheel; the acquisition goal and the data goal are the same goal.
  • Transfer from published datasets and from Copernicus/veterinary/aquaculture literature should be exploited hard everywhere. We are data-poor and the world is not.
