AI / ML Model Cards

ai-ml-models v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target raw JSON ↗
mitchell-model-cards-2019
ℹSTATUS BANNER

NO MODEL HAS BEEN TRAINED. NO MODEL HAS BEEN EVALUATED. Every `performance` field below is null and every `target_performance` field is an aspiration. These are specifications for models we intend to build, written before the data exists, so that the evaluation criteria are fixed before anyone has an incentive to move them.

Convention

Follows the Model Cards convention (Mitchell et al., 2019). Every card carries an out_of_scope_use field, because a model card without one is marketing.

Citation Ids

  • mitchell-model-cards-2019

The Governing Principle

Statement
The order in which these models ship is a safety decision, not a product decision.
Reasoning

The models are ordered below from 'safe to be wrong' to 'dangerous to be wrong'. A misclassified piece of debris costs a farmer thirty seconds of annoyance. A false negative from the kill-risk model reads as 'you are safe' and could cost a harvest. The temptation will be to ship the exciting one first because it demos well. Resist it.

The Rule
No model ships until its failure mode has been costed in the customer's currency, not ours.

Models

Water Masking (NDWI threshold)

Id
water-masking
Task
Separate water pixels from land, vegetation, structure, and boats.
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate
Threshold on NDWI, with a learned refinement only if thresholding proves inadequate.
Why It Is First

Everything downstream depends on it. A dike pixel entering a chlorophyll statistic corrupts the statistic silently.

Intended Use
Preprocessing for every imagery product.
Out of Scope Use
Not a shoreline mapping tool. Not for land use classification.
Inputs
  • Green reflectance
  • NIR reflectance
Outputs
  • Binary water mask
  • Mask confidence
Citations
mcfeeters-ndwi-1996
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

High recall on water, high precision on exclusion. Prefer excluding an ambiguous pixel over including it — a smaller, cleaner mask beats a larger, contaminated one.

Known Failure Modes
  • Cage netting and floats within a water body
  • Shadow
  • Dense floating algal mat, which may read as vegetation and be masked out — precisely the thing we most want to measure
  • Turbid shallow margins
The Interesting Failure

A thick surface algal mat can be misclassified as vegetation and masked out of the analysis. The bloom disappears from the chlorophyll map exactly when it is worst. This is a known NDWI pathology and it must be explicitly tested for, not discovered in production.

Human Oversight
Mask visible and overridable in the UI.

Chlorophyll-a Retrieval (NDCI + local regression)

Id
chla-retrieval
Task
Convert per-pixel NDCI into estimated chlorophyll-a concentration with uncertainty.
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate

Site-and-day-local regression of NDCI against in-situ fluorometric chlorophyll from the same sortie. Start with linear or simple nonlinear; do not reach for a neural network to fit four points.

Why Not A Global Model

Turbidity, CDOM, and illumination differ enough between lakes — and between two days on the same lake — that a global fit is confidently wrong. The literature is consistent on this and it is the main reason UAV retrievals fail in production.

Citations
mishra-ndci-2012 mdpi-drones-inland-review-2024 sciencedirect-uav-multispectral-ponds-2024
Intended Use

Spatial mapping of algal biomass across a water body, calibrated by same-sortie ground truth.

Out of Scope Use
  • NOT a toxicity measurement. Chlorophyll indicates algal biomass, not toxin presence. A high reading is a reason to sample, not a diagnosis.
  • NOT valid outside the range of the in-situ points it was fitted on. Extrapolating a three-point regression beyond its calibration range is fabrication.
  • NOT comparable across sites without refitting.
Inputs
  • NDCI field
  • Water mask
  • In-situ chlorophyll at known stations
  • Turbidity as covariate
Outputs
  • Concentration field
  • Per-pixel uncertainty field
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

To be set against the reference sonde in Phase 3. Setting a target accuracy now, before any data, would be inventing a number.

Known Failure Modes
  • Sun glint contamination saturating the signal
  • Fitting on 3-4 points produces a fragile regression with wide confidence intervals — this is the normal case, not an edge case
  • High turbidity decoupling the red-edge signal from chlorophyll
  • Extrapolation beyond the calibration range
The Honest Problem

With only three or four probe stations per sortie, the regression will often be poorly constrained. The correct response is to show the wide uncertainty, not to hide it behind a smooth colour ramp. A precise-looking map that is not precise will be believed, and a farmer will act on it.

Human Oversight

Uncertainty field always rendered. Maps below a confidence threshold are shown as 'insufficient calibration' rather than as a map.

Surface Anomaly Detection

Id
surface-anomaly
Task

Detect and classify floating mortalities, surface-piping fish, oil sheen, foam, algal mats, debris, and above-water net damage.

Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate
Small-object detection CNN, quantised for edge inference at a few watts.
Intended Use
Flag surface anomalies for human review, geolocated to a specific cage.
Out of Scope Use
  • NOT a mortality count. It detects floating fish; it does not count stock.
  • NOT a disease diagnosis.
  • NOT for assessing personnel performance. This is a hard rule — see the technician persona in customer-personas.json.
Inputs
  • RGB imagery at <=15 mm GSD
Outputs
  • Detections with class, calibrated confidence, and geolocation
  • Image crop as evidence
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

Precision matters far more than recall here, and this is counterintuitive.

Reasoning

A missed dead fish is found on the next sortie two hours later. A false alarm at 04:30 that drags a manager out of bed for nothing destroys trust in the whole system, and the third false alarm means the tenth alert — the real one — is ignored. Alert fatigue is not a UX problem; it is a safety failure. Tune for precision and accept lower recall.

Target Precision
>= 0.90
Target Recall
>= 0.60
Status
·Targets Only — No Data Exists
Known Failure Modes
  • Sun glint mimicking sheen
  • Floating debris versus fish
  • Wave texture as false positives
  • Birds
  • Species and lighting variation between sites
The Generalisation Problem

A model trained on one lake will likely fail on another — different water colour, different light, different debris, different species. The Phase 3 gate deliberately requires evaluation on a HELD-OUT SITE, not a held-out split of the same site. A random split of one lake's imagery proves nothing about generalisation and would flatter the model badly.

Fairness and Harm Considerations

The imagery contains people, boats, and houses. The model must not be extended to person detection, and the training data pipeline must not retain identifiable imagery of individuals. See ethics-data-privacy.json.

Human Oversight

Every detection routes to human confirmation with the image crop attached. A bare classification with no evidence is unactionable and will be ignored. Rejections become training data.

Stratification State Estimation

Id
stratification-state
Task
Compute thermocline depth, Schmidt stability, and hypolimnetic oxygen debt from a profile.
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate
Deterministic computation, not machine learning.
Why Not Ml

This is established limnological physics with a century of literature behind it. Using a learned model where a physical formula exists is worse in every dimension: less accurate, less interpretable, needs training data we do not have, and fails unpredictably outside its training distribution. The instinct to apply ML to everything should be resisted precisely here, where the physics is known.

Citations
idso-schmidt-stability-1973
Intended Use
Quantify the water column's structure and the size of the oxygen-poor reservoir below.
Out of Scope Use
Not a prediction. It describes the current state.
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

Correctness is verifiable against the reference sonde. This is a computation, not an estimate.

Known Failure Modes
  • Partial profiles — a profile that stopped at 5 m in a 12 m column cannot compute stability and must be flagged, not silently averaged
  • Weak gradients where the thermocline is genuinely ambiguous
Human Oversight
Profile chart always available. The number never appears without the shape it came from.

Composite Fish Kill Risk Forecast

Id
kill-risk-forecast
Task
Estimate the probability of a mass mortality event in a defined forward window.
Status
·Concept — NOT IMPLEMENTED, NOT VALIDATED, MUST NOT SHIP
The Warning
Statement

This is the most dangerous artefact in the entire product, and it is the one everyone will ask for first.

Why

A false negative from this model does not read as 'no prediction available'. It reads as 'you are safe'. A farmer who defers running aerators because our score said 12/100 and then loses a harvest has been harmed BY US. We would have converted their uncertainty — which they knew how to live with — into a false certainty that they acted on.

The Asymmetry

Without the model, the farmer is uncertain and cautious. With a wrong model, the farmer is confident and wrong. We can make things worse than doing nothing, which is not true of any other model in this file.

The Commercial Pressure

This score is the single most compelling thing that could appear in a demo. Investors will ask for it. Customers will ask for it. The pressure to ship a plausible-looking version trained on thin data will be intense and continuous. This paragraph exists to be read on the day that pressure arrives.

Architecture Candidate

Ensemble over stratification index, oxygen debt, chlorophyll trend, diel amplitude, weather forecast, and stocking density.

Inputs
  • Stratification state time series
  • Oxygen debt
  • Chlorophyll trend
  • Diel DO amplitude
  • Weather forecast
  • Stocking density (operator-supplied)
Outputs
  • Risk score
  • Contributing factor attribution
  • Uncertainty
Performance
—
Target Performance
Note

Cannot be set responsibly before seeing real events.

The Real Bar

It must beat the experienced farmer's own judgement. If it cannot, it should not ship at any accuracy — an instrument that is worse than the expert it advises is a liability with a subscription fee.

Required Evidence

An honest confusion matrix against real events across at least one full seasonal cycle, including the events it missed.

Known Failure Modes
  • Trained on too few events — mass kills are rare, so the positive class is tiny and the model will learn to always say 'no' and score 97 percent accurate. This is the classic rare-event trap and it will happen.
  • Site-specific dynamics that do not transfer between lakes
  • The trigger is usually a storm, and we cannot fly in storms, so our observations stop precisely when the event fires
Human Oversight

Until validated: the system shows the MECHANISM — the stratification, the oxygen debt, the forecast — in plain language, and lets the farmer draw the conclusion. Given their expertise, that is probably better than a score anyway, and it is certainly more honest.

The Interim Product

'The bottom three metres are anoxic and rain is forecast tonight' is a true statement, derived from measurement, that an experienced operator can act on. It requires no model, carries no false certainty, and is available on day one. Ship that.

Condition-Aware Feeding Advisor

Id
feeding-advisor
Task
Recommend whether to feed, defer, or reduce ration based on current water conditions.
Status
·Concept
Architecture Candidate

Rule-based initially, from published species thresholds. Learned refinement only once real FCR outcome data exists.

Why Rules First

The physiology is known: fish in low DO do not convert feed, and feeding into low oxygen adds organic load to water that is already short. A rule encoding that is interpretable, debuggable, and correct on day one. A learned model would need outcome data we will not have for years and would be less explainable to the person deciding.

Citations
innovasea-fcr-guide boyd-water-quality-2020
Out of Scope Use
Not a feed formulation tool. Not a growth model.
Human Oversight
Advisory only. It never withholds feed autonomously.
Known Limitation

We infer appetite from water condition. Incumbent systems watch the fish eat with an underwater camera, which is a direct observation and strictly better feedback. Our advantage is coverage across forty cages, not fidelity in one.

Cross Cutting Commitments

Commitment
Calibrated confidence, not raw softmax
Why

An uncalibrated confidence shown as a percentage is decoration that looks like information. If we cannot calibrate it, we do not show a number.

Commitment
Explainability is load-bearing, not a nice-to-have
Why

An alert that tells a farmer to spend money on emergency aeration will be ignored unless it says why. XAI here is a commercial requirement before it is an ethical one.

Citations
ai-aqua-review-2024
Commitment
Evaluate on held-out SITES, not held-out splits
Why

Random splits of one lake's data measure memorisation, not generalisation, and they will flatter every model in this file.

Commitment
Publish the failures
Why

The confusion matrix including the misses is the only honest evidence. A model card with only the good numbers is a brochure.

Commitment
Federated or on-premise training where farmers require it
Why

Farm production data is commercially sensitive and farmers have sound reasons to refuse to hand it over. Assuming we get to centralise it is an assumption, not a plan.

Citations
ai-aqua-review-2024

The Data Problem

Statement
We have no training data, and the most valuable model needs the rarest events.
The Arithmetic

Mass kills happen a few times a year per lake. A supervised model needs many examples. Even a multi-year, multi-site deployment yields tens of positive examples at best. This is a fundamental constraint, not an engineering inconvenience.

Implications
  • Physics-informed approaches beat pure data-driven ones for the rare-event problem. We know the mechanism; we should encode it rather than hope to learn it from a handful of examples.
  • The chronic sub-lethal hypothesis from cost-of-inaction.json is far more tractable ML — continuous outcomes, abundant data, every cycle a sample. If that hypothesis is right, it is both the better business AND the better machine learning problem. That coincidence is worth noticing.
  • Transfer from published datasets and from Copernicus history should be exploited hard. We are data-poor and the world is not.
