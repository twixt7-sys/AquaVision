Citation Ids

Currency Note

Values in USD as published by FAO. No inflation adjustment applied.

Vintage Warning

SOFIA 2024 reports 2022 data — a two-year lag is normal for this dataset. As of this repository's date (July 2026), SOFIA 2026 may be available and would supersede these figures. Check before using in a submission. Citing a superseded flagship report is a small error that signals a large carelessness.

Repositioning Note

The underlying FAO figures in this file are unchanged from the prior edition — they are real, external, verified statistics and this pivot gives no license to touch them. What has changed is the analysis layered on top: AquaVision is now a phased software-first platform (free/low-cost app -> premium subscription -> hardware upgrade), not a hardware-first drone company, so the 'so what' attached to each statistic now speaks to both the software addressable population (Phase 1/2) and the hardware addressable population (Phase 3) rather than to hardware alone. See phased_strategy_relevance below for the consolidated version of that reasoning.

Why This Is the Slide

This is the single most important statistic available to this company, and it should be slide two. It marks a structural inflection: humanity now gets most of its aquatic animal protein from systems that someone owns, manages, and could in principle log, analyse, and eventually instrument. Capture fisheries cannot be managed or sensor-optimised in any meaningful way — you cannot put a farm-management app, let alone a dashboard, on the open ocean. Farmed systems can. The addressable surface for aquaculture SOFTWARE crossed 50 percent of the sector in 2022, and every one of those managed operations is a potential app user regardless of whether it ever becomes a hardware customer. The addressable surface for aquaculture HARDWARE is a narrower subset of that same 51 percent, gated by scale and capital. Every argument this company makes, at every phase, rests on this number.

Why This Matters To Aquavision

Nearly two-thirds of farmed aquatic animals come from inland systems — ponds, lakes, reservoirs. Inland pond and cage farming is disproportionately smallholder and family-operated relative to marine cage culture, which is precisely the population the Phase 1 free app is built to reach first. It is also, unchanged from the prior analysis, the OmniDrone's Phase 3 environment: inland water is where stratification, eutrophication, and overturn dominate, and where a small aerial platform is operationally viable. The same statistic justifies both layers of the strategy — start inland because that is where the software's target users and the hardware's technical fit both point.

Why This Matters To Aquavision

Seventy percent of the world's aquatic animal production is on our doorstep. A Philippine company building for Southeast Asian conditions is building for the centre of the industry, not the periphery — which is an unusual and genuine structural advantage at every phase. Most aquaculture technology, software and hardware alike, is built in Norway, Scotland, or North America and exported to Asia where it fits poorly: a generic farm app in English with temperate-crop assumptions, or a salmon precision-aquaculture platform, both transfer badly to tropical smallholder tilapia and milkfish. Building the free app, the premium tier, and the eventual hardware for tropical, smallholder-dense, monsoon-exposed conditions from the start is a real differentiator across all three phases, not a consolation for lacking Phase 3 capital on day one.

Philippine Context

Status
·INCOMPLETE — This Is the Most Important Gap In This File, and It Now Blocks BOTH the Software and the Hardware Market Sizing, Not Just the Hardware One
Why It Matters

Our beachhead is the Philippines. Global FAO figures establish that the sector matters; they say nothing about our actual market at either phase. National production by species, number of operators (ideally split by smallholder/first-time vs established/commercial), cage counts by lake, farm-level cost structures, and — new to this phased strategy — smartphone and mobile internet access among fisherfolk households, are all required and all currently absent.

Required Sources
  • BFAR Philippine Fisheries Profile — the annual statistical yearbook. This is the single most important document this repository is missing, for the software layer's operator counts just as much as for the hardware layer's.
  • Philippine Statistics Authority fisheries production data.
  • LLDA (Laguna Lake Development Authority) cage inventories and water quality monitoring records.
  • Taal Lake Protected Landscape and Seascape management records for cage counts.
  • SEAFDEC/AQD technical publications for species-specific culture practice and cost structures.
  • DICT/PSA household ICT-use surveys, or an equivalent source, for smartphone ownership and mobile data access among Philippine fisherfolk and small-scale aquaculture households — a new requirement introduced by the software layer that did not exist in the hardware-only version of this file.

How To Use These Statistics

  • The aquaculture-overtakes-capture figure is the opening argument for the company as a whole, not just for the hardware layer. Use it once, prominently, and let it do its work.
  • The 62.6 percent inland figure justifies both the Phase 1/2 software beachhead population (smallholder inland farmers) and the Phase 3 platform choice (aerial rather than marine). Use it when challenged on why start with inland smallholders, and again when challenged on why aerial rather than marine.
  • The 70 percent Asia figure justifies the geography for the whole company. Use it when a foreign investor asks why not start in Norway, whether the question is about the app or the drone.
  • Do NOT use the USD 312.8 billion aquaculture value as a market size for EITHER layer. It is the value of the FISH, not of the software or the hardware addressable within it. Conflating industry output with technology TAM is the most common and most transparent error in agritech pitches, and every professional investor is watching for it, for a freemium software pitch just as much as for a hardware pitch. See market-sizing-tam-sam-som.json for the defensible, two-layer construction.
