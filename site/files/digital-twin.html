PondTwin — Digital Twin Specification

digital-twin v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗

The Definition We Are Holding Ourselves To

Statement

A digital twin is a virtual representation kept synchronised with a physical system by live sensor data, running a model of that system's behaviour, capable of answering counterfactual questions.

The Distinction That Matters

A dashboard shows what happened. A twin runs a model and can answer 'what if'. If our PondTwin cannot answer 'what happens to DO at cage 4 if I run the aerators for six hours', it is a 3D dashboard and we should call it that.

Why We Are Being Strict

'Digital twin' is used loosely enough in industry to mean nothing at all — often just a rendered model with live numbers on it. A thesis that adopts the loose usage has conceded its own contribution. Defend the strict definition or drop the term.

sciencedirect-dt-aiot-2023 hamzah-dt-water-review-2024 pmc-dt-rainbow-trout-2023
Our Honest Status

What is specified below is, in v1, mostly a 3D dashboard with interpolation. The simulation core that would make it a real twin is Phase 3+ work and depends on validation data we do not have. We should say '3D visualisation with a twin roadmap' until the simulation exists, rather than claiming the term now and being caught by a knowledgeable reviewer.

Why A Twin and Not A Chart

The Real Argument

The water body is three-dimensional and the farmer's mental model of it is two-dimensional. Every existing tool — a meter reading, a logbook, a dashboard tile — presents water quality as a scalar. The actual system is a stratified volume with horizontal structure, and the whole reason fish die is that the dangerous part is in a dimension nobody looks at.

The Claim

The twin's contribution is not visual polish. It is making depth legible to a person who has never been able to see it. A farmer who can rotate a pond and SEE the anoxic layer sitting under their cages understands the risk in a way that '1.1 mg/L at 6 m' does not convey.

The Test

If the twin does not change what the farmer understands, it is decoration and the engineering effort belongs elsewhere.

Twin Layers

Layer
Geometry
Content
Bathymetry, shoreline, cage and pen positions, dock location, station positions.
Source
One-time survey at site commissioning, plus operator-supplied cage layout.
Note

Bathymetry is a one-time cost, not a recurring measurement. Rent a LiDAR or a survey boat once; do not carry the sensor.

Layer
State — measured
Content

The profiles. DO, temperature, pH, turbidity, chlorophyll, ORP, conductivity at known depths at known stations at known times.

Provenance Rule

This layer is measurement. It renders differently from every other layer and it is never blended with them.

Layer
State — interpolated
Content

The volume between stations, filled by spatial interpolation and by the calibrated imagery field at the surface.

The Honesty Problem
Statement
This is where a twin becomes a liar, and it does so beautifully.
Explanation

We measure six stations in a lake with thousands of cubic metres. The rendering fills the volume between them with a smooth, confident, gorgeous gradient. The eye reads that gradient as data. It is not data — it is an assumption about what water does between the places we looked, and it is exactly the assumption that fails when a pocket of bad water sits in the gap.

The Requirement

The twin MUST render uncertainty visually. Volume far from any station must look visibly less certain — transparency, noise, explicit confidence banding, something. A smooth interpolation with no uncertainty cue is the single most misleading artefact this company could ship, precisely because it is the most persuasive.

The Temptation

The version with the smooth gradient demos better. It will look better in the deck, in the pitch, and on the website. This paragraph exists to be read on the day someone proposes removing the uncertainty rendering because it 'looks noisy'.

Requirement Id
REQ-TWIN-003
Layer
State — simulated
Content

A physical model of the water column: heat exchange, oxygen production and consumption, mixing, and their evolution.

Status
·PHASE 3+. Does Not Exist. This Is the Layer That Would Make It An Actual Twin.
What It Would Enable
  • Counterfactual questions — 'what if I aerate'
  • Forward projection between sorties
  • Filling the gap when weather grounds the aircraft
  • Physically-constrained interpolation, which would be far better than the geometric interpolation above
The Dependency

A physical model must be calibrated against observed behaviour of THIS water body. That needs a season of data. There is no shortcut.

The Interesting Consequence

Once the simulation exists, it also fills our worst gap: we cannot fly in storms, but a calibrated model CAN project the water column through a storm from the last profile and the weather data. The twin is the answer to our own flight envelope limitation. That is a genuinely strong argument for investing in the simulation core, and it is not obvious until you notice that our observation gap and the model's purpose are the same gap.

Layer
Stock
Content
Species, stocking date, count, estimated biomass, feed history, mortality history.
Source

OPERATOR-SUPPLIED. We do not measure any of it — no underwater vision, no biomass estimation.

The Honest Note

The twin's stock layer is only as good as the farmer's records, and the farmer's records are often a guess. A twin that displays a confident biomass number sourced from a farmer's estimate has laundered a guess into an authoritative-looking figure. Label operator-supplied data distinctly from measured data, always.

Layer
Events
Content
Sorties, alerts, dispensing actions, aeration runs, mortality events, weather.
Why It Matters

The event timeline is what turns a state display into a history someone can learn from. It is also the audit record.

The Provenance Rendering Requirement

Statement

The twin must make it visually obvious which parts are measured, which are interpolated, which are simulated, and which the farmer typed in.

Why This Is the Hardest UX Problem In the Product

A twin's persuasive power comes from looking like a coherent, complete picture of reality. Its honesty requires showing that it is a sparse set of measurements with a lot of inference stretched between them. Those two goals are in direct opposition, and the resolution is a design problem nobody has solved well.

The Rule
When in doubt, look less certain. An under-confident twin costs a little persuasion. An over-confident one costs a harvest.
Rendering Guidance
Measured
Solid, sharp, at the station location. Visibly a point.
Interpolated
Progressively more transparent and more visibly uncertain with distance from any station.
Simulated
Distinct visual treatment — never blended into the measured layer.
Operator Supplied
Explicitly badged. It is testimony, not measurement.
Stale

Time-decayed. A profile from six hours ago is not the current state, and after a storm it may be worthless. Data has a half-life and the UI should show it decaying.

Interaction Model

Primary View
A rotatable, sliceable 3D volume of the water body with cages in place.
The Key Interaction

Slice vertically through a cage and see the column. This is the interaction that does the work — it makes the invisible dimension visible, and it is the whole point.

The Second Interaction

Scrub time. Watch the thermocline move over days. Watch the diel cycle breathe. This is where a farmer's intuition and the data meet, and it is where the twin earns its keep.

Restraint

Resist the urge to add every metric. The twin's job is to make depth and time legible. Everything else belongs in the dashboard.

Prior Art

Note

Digital twins for aquaculture moved from proposal to demonstrated case study in 2023-2024. We are not inventing this — we are applying it, and we should cite the people who did the work.

What Is Genuinely Novel Here

Not the twin. The data source. Existing aquaculture twins are fed by fixed sensors in one location or by manual sampling. A twin fed by depth-resolved profiles across many stations several times daily, with the imagery calibrated by the same vehicle, is a better-observed twin than the literature has had access to. The contribution is the observation architecture, not the twin architecture — and a thesis should say so plainly rather than overclaiming novelty in a well-trodden area.

The Review That Matters Most

Hamzah et al. (2024) reviewed drone-based digital twins for water quality specifically. That is the closest prior art to this exact concept and it should be read carefully before another line of twin code is written.

The Gap Analysis

What V1 Will Actually Be

A 3D visualisation of measured profiles with honest interpolation and an event timeline. Genuinely useful. Not a twin.

What Makes It A Twin
The simulation core, which needs a season of calibration data.
The Recommendation

Build v1 honestly and call it what it is. The simulation is Phase 3+ and it is worth doing properly, because it is also the answer to the storm-grounding problem. Do not claim the term early; the reviewer who catches it will discount everything else.
