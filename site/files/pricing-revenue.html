Pricing & Revenue Model

pricing-revenue v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ↗Projection ◆Confidence: speculative raw JSON ↗
ℹREAD THIS FIRST

This file gives modeled price BANDS and revenue-model LOGIC for each of the three phases, not confirmed prices. None of the numbers below are 'verified_external' — every one is tagged 'modeled_projection' or 'assumption' and is a planning anchor, not a committed price list. The Phase 3 hardware layer keeps the prior discipline exactly: no price appears there until Phase 0 discovery supplies a real cost-to-serve and a measured willingness to pay. The Phase 1/2 software layer is new, and its bands are modeled against comparable freemium/agri-SaaS pricing patterns, not against this company's own data, because none exists yet. A founder who says 'here is our pricing logic and the bands we are testing toward' is more credible than one who presents a confident price list built on zero users.

The Three Phase Pricing Structure

Phase 1
Free / near-free — the acquisition tier. No monetisation.
Phase 2

Freemium premium subscription + marketplace take-rate + consultation fees — the monetisation tier.

Phase 3

Hardware-as-a-service deployment pricing — the enterprise tier, priced against loss avoidance and aeration savings rather than hardware cost, exactly as in the original hardware-first plan.

Why Free Not Near Free

Any price, however small, reintroduces the affordability barrier this tier exists to remove, and adds payment-friction cost (processing, support tickets, churn at the paywall) that a true free tier avoids. 'Near-free' is kept in the model name only as a fallback if Phase 0-equivalent app discovery finds a reason a token price improves retention (e.g. loss-aversion effects reported in some freemium literature) — but the default plan is USD/PHP 0.

Phase 3 Smart Aquaculture Pricing

Carried Forward From the Original Hardware Plan

The logic below is unchanged from the prior single-phase version of this file — it was already disciplined and remains correct for the Phase 3 hardware-as-a-service layer.

Pricing Philosophy
Principle

Price against value delivered, not cost incurred — anchor on the value the customer can VERIFY, not the value we claim.

Why

Verifiable value (aeration savings, audit-labour savings) is a floor the customer will accept because they can check it. Claimed value (avoided mortality) is a ceiling we cannot prove. Price near the verifiable floor to win the deal, and let the unverifiable upside be the reason they renew once they have seen it.

Should We Sell the Hardware Outright
Answer
Almost certainly not.
Reasoning
  • A five-figure-USD capital ask to a fish farm is a non-starter on affordability and gadget-fatigue.
  • Selling the hardware transfers maintenance and calibration responsibility to a customer who cannot do it, which destroys the data quality the whole product depends on.
  • Service retention keeps the data relationship, which is the moat.
Model

AquaVision owns the hardware; the customer buys the monitoring service — hardware-as-a-service, not equipment sales.

Cross Phase Notes

Why No Single Price List

Three genuinely different pricing problems sit under one brand: a zero-price acquisition tier, a freemium conversion problem with an unmeasured willingness-to-pay, and a hardware-as-a-service problem that requires a real BOM before any number is honest. Collapsing them into one pricing page would hide which numbers are guessable now (Phase 1/2, against comparable software businesses) and which are genuinely unknowable before physical-world discovery (Phase 3).

When This Gets Filled In

Phase 1/2 bands tighten as soon as real conversion and usage data exist post-launch — weeks, not seasons. Phase 3 numbers fill in only after farmer discovery and a real sonde/drone BOM, exactly as before — this has not gotten easier just because the company also has a software product now.
