Carriage & Ejection System

carriage-ejection-system v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗
ℹREAD THIS FIRST
Statement:

Two independent analyses in this repository — the mass budget in omnidrone-specifications.json and the mission economics in mission-profiles.json — arrived separately at the same conclusion: this subsystem cannot viably deliver bulk grow-out feed.

The Arithmetic:

A commercial cage takes tens of kilograms per feeding. This aircraft's total useful payload target is 1.8 kg, and that budget is already spent on the probe and the cameras. Meanwhile a person in a boat moves a 25 kg sack in one trip at effectively zero marginal cost. We would need dozens of sorties and dozens of battery cycles to do what one person does before breakfast.

The Conclusion:

Reposition this subsystem as a PRECISION TREATMENT DELIVERY system, not a feeding system. Small masses of high-value material — conditioners, lime, probiotics, treatments — placed precisely, at a specific coordinate, at a moment when nobody can safely put a boat out. That is a real and defensible capability. Competing with a man and a sack on bulk logistics is not.

Why This Matters More Than It Looks:

The concept description that started this project listed 'administer feeds' first among the dispensing functions. The engineering says feeds are the one thing this system should not do. Following the arithmetic rather than the original framing is what separates a specification from a wish list — and finding this yourself is far better than having an investor find it for you.

Platform Context

This is a subsystem of OmniDrone, the aerial component of AquaVision Smart (Phase 3) — the premium hardware upgrade layer of the AquaVision platform for commercial/enterprise customers who have outgrown the app-based Phase 1/2 tiers, not the company's core product.

What It Should Actually Do

Primary Role

Deliver small, precisely metered doses of high-value material to a specific location identified by the digital twin, without putting a person on the water.

Why This Is Strategically Important

It is the answer to the strongest objection against the entire venture — that information without response capacity is just a countdown clock. A platform that can act on its own finding is a fundamentally different proposition from one that can only report. That is worth carrying the mass for, even at 1.5 kg of payload.

The Scenarios Where It Wins
  • Lime or conditioner to a low-pH pocket the twin identified, at 5 a.m., in weather where a boat is dangerous.
  • Probiotic or conditioner applied evenly across a pattern that a person throwing by hand cannot achieve.
  • A starter ration spread across a nursery pond where the total mass IS small and even distribution matters more than tonnage.
  • Treatment to one cage in an array of forty, without dosing the other thirty-nine.
The Scenarios Where It Loses
  • Bulk grow-out feed. Every time. Do not pretend otherwise.

Architecture

Configuration
Twin ventral pods flanking the centre-line winch, per the supplied render.
Render Note

The render's twin-pod arrangement is a good instinct — it balances mass laterally around the winch and keeps the centre line free for the tether. Whether the pods' apparent volume matches any real dose requirement is unvalidated.

Modules
Module
Granular hopper
Payload
Pelleted feed, lime, granular conditioner
Metering
Auger or rotary valve with closed-loop mass feedback
Why Closed Loop

Open-loop metering by time or revolutions drifts with material density, humidity, and fill level. In the tropics, a pellet that has absorbed moisture has a different bulk density than the same pellet yesterday. Without a load cell measuring what actually left the hopper, the dose record is fiction — and the dose record is the thing that makes the system auditable.

The Safety Architecture

Principle
An uncommanded discharge is a poisoned pond. Design accordingly.
Requirement Id
REQ-HW-008
Controls
Control
No autonomous dispensing, ever
Detail

Every dispensing mission requires explicit human authorisation for that specific mission, that specific substance, that specific dose, and that specific location. The authorisation does not persist to the next mission.

Why

A model error that miscounts a threshold becomes a chemical event. The feature that skips this gate would demo beautifully right up until the day it destroyed a customer's harvest — and there is no version of that story where the company survives it.

Control
Mechanical interlock
Detail

The dispensing mechanism is physically incapable of discharge without an armed state, and arming is a deliberate act.

Control
Dose ceiling
Detail

A hard maximum per mission, enforced in firmware, that no software command can exceed. The ceiling is set from the substance's label rate and the water volume, and it is not overridable from the dashboard.

Control
Discharge logging
Detail

Actual delivered mass and coordinates logged from the load cell, not from the commanded value. What we intended to dispense and what we dispensed are different facts and both matter.

Control
Abort on anomaly
Detail
Any dispensing anomaly aborts the mission and returns the aircraft with the load intact.
The Dual Use Problem
Statement
A drone that makes precision dosing easier also makes over-dosing easier.
Reasoning

The subsystem's whole value is that it lowers the cost and effort of applying a substance to water. That is good when the substance is lime and the operator is careful. It is bad when the substance is an antimicrobial and the operator is anxious about an outbreak. Lowering the friction on a behaviour increases the behaviour, and prophylactic antimicrobial use in aquaculture is already a public health problem.

Our Position

The dose ceiling and the human authorisation gate are the technical controls. The honest admission is that they are weak controls against a determined operator, and that this subsystem's contribution to antimicrobial stewardship could plausibly be negative rather than positive. This deserves a paragraph in the thesis ethics chapter rather than the usual silence.

Regulatory Gate

Warning
Dispensing veterinary medicine is not the same activity as dispensing lime, legally.
Detail

Anything meeting the definition of a veterinary medicinal product brings in prescription requirements, professional oversight, record-keeping obligations, and withdrawal periods. The drone becomes an application device for a regulated substance, and the operator may need veterinary supervision.

Citations
caap-rpas-regulations
Note

CAAP has issued advisory material covering special permits for agricultural spraying drones. Whether aquaculture dispensing falls under that framing, under BFAR's, under a veterinary framework, or under all three is UNRESOLVED and needs a lawyer rather than a founder's reading.

Action

Phase 0: get a legal opinion on the substance classes before designing the module around any of them. Building a medicine dispenser and then discovering it needs a veterinarian on site would be an expensive lesson.
