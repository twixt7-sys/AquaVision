Concept of Operations (ConOps)

concept-of-operations v1.1.0 updated 2026-07-18 ◎Target raw JSON ↗
ieee-1362

Note

Follows the spirit of IEEE 1362 ConOps structure. Describes how the system is used in the field, by whom, under what authority.

Phase Scope Note

AquaVision is now a phased platform (see solution-overview.json): a free Phase 1 app, a paid Phase 2 analytics/marketplace layer, and a Phase 3 hardware upgrade (IoT sensors, water probes, OmniDrone, PondTwin) sold to commercial and enterprise operators. This entire ConOps — docks, sorties, remote pilots, BVLOS — describes the Phase 3 hardware layer specifically. Phases 1 and 2 are a mobile/web app and a subscription service; neither has a flight operations concept.

Citation Ids

  • ieee-1362

Operational Context

Environment

Tropical inland or brackish water body. Fish cages or ponds. Shore access via dike or bank. Grid power unreliable or absent. Cellular coverage intermittent. Monsoon season for roughly half the year. Ambient temperature 25-35 C, humidity high, water corrosive and biologically active.

The Design Case

Not a research site. A working farm, operated by people with other jobs to do, where the drone is the least important thing happening that day. If the system requires attention, it will not get it.

Operating Modes

Mode
Attended VLOS
Description

Certificated remote pilot present, aircraft in visual line of sight, pilot able to take control.

Regulatory Status
Permitted under standard CAAP rules, subject to registration and pilot certification.
Citations
caap-rpas-regulations
When Used
Phases 1-3. All early operations. Any first flight at a new site.
Economics

Poor. A pilot per sortie destroys the cost-per-observation advantage that is the entire premise. In this mode we are an expensive way to do what a boat does.

Honest Note
This is the mode we will actually operate in for years. The business model should be stress-tested against it rather than against the mode we hope to get.
Mode
Supervised BVLOS
Description

Aircraft beyond visual line of sight, remote pilot monitoring from the dashboard, able to command return.

Regulatory Status
Requires specific CAAP authorisation.
Citations
caap-rpas-regulations caap-types-of-operations
When Used
The target operating mode from Phase 4.
Economics
This is where the model works. One pilot supervising several docks.
Mode
Autonomous scheduled
Description

Dock-initiated sorties on schedule with no human in the launch loop; human notified of results and of exceptions.

Regulatory Status

Requires BVLOS authorisation plus a substantial safety case. Do not assume this is available.

When Used
Aspirational. The dawn sweep needs it.
The Dependency

The flagship use case — the 04:30 profile — requires either this mode or a person at the dock at 04:30. If neither is available, the product's most distinctive value is unreachable. This is the sharpest expression of why BVLOS is on the critical path, and it should be said in exactly these terms to investors rather than buried.

Mode
Manual override
Description
Pilot takes direct control.
When Used
Any anomaly. Always available. Never removed.

Roles and Authority

Role
Remote Pilot in Command
Authority

Final authority on flight. Can abort any mission for any reason without justification. This authority is never delegated to software.

Requirement
CAAP RPA Controller Certificate
Citations
caap-rpas-regulations
Role
Farm Manager
Authority

Authorises dispensing actions. Sets thresholds. Receives alerts. Does NOT have flight authority.

Note

The separation matters: the person with commercial pressure to fly must not be the person who decides it is safe to fly.

Role
Farm Technician
Authority
Ground handling, probe cleaning, battery handling, fault reporting. No flight authority.
Note

The system depends on this person's goodwill more than any org chart admits.

Role
AquaVision Operations
Authority

Maintains the platform, the models, and the fleet. Cannot unilaterally change alert thresholds on a customer's farm.

Role
The software
Authority

None that matters. It schedules, measures, computes, and recommends. It does not decide to dispense, does not declare water safe, and does not fly outside the envelope a human set.

The Honest Footnote

This narrative is a design intent, not a case study. Nothing in it has happened. It is written in past tense because that is how storyboards work, and it must be labelled clearly wherever it is used so that nobody mistakes it for a customer testimonial. Note also what the story does NOT claim: the drone did not prevent the mixing, did not fly in the storm, and did not make the decision. It measured, and a person acted. That is the real product and it is enough.

Human Machine Boundary

Principle
The system observes and recommends. The human decides and is accountable.
Hard Rules
  • No autonomous dispensing of any substance. Ever. A model error becomes a poisoned pond.
  • No autonomous declaration that water is safe. The system reports what it measured and what it did not measure. Absence of a detection is not a clearance, and the UI must never let grey read as green.
  • No flight outside the human-set envelope, regardless of mission priority.
  • Every alert states its evidence and its uncertainty. An alert that cannot explain itself will be ignored the third time, and the third time may be the real one.
Why These Are Hard Rules

Each one blocks a feature that would demo well. That is precisely why they are written down before the demo pressure arrives rather than after.

Failure Handling

Link Loss

Continue the current mission if within envelope, then return to dock. Do NOT hover in place awaiting a link — that burns the reserve that gets the aircraft home.

Low Battery

Abort mission, return, and land. The reserve is not negotiable and is not available to finish 'just one more station'. Every ditched aircraft in this industry was one more station away from the dock.

Winch Jam With Probe Deployed

The nightmare case. A drone with a fouled sonde caught on a net is a tethered aircraft with a finite battery. Requires a tether cutter as a last resort — which means deliberately dropping the most expensive part of the payload into the lake to save the aircraft, and recovering it later. The cutter must exist, and the decision to use it should be a human's, not a timer's.

Probe Snag On Structure

Detect via winch tension anomaly. Attempt a controlled release, then escalate to the pilot.

Dock Unavailable

Divert to a pre-surveyed alternate landing site. Over a lake, there may not be one — which is why alternate site survey is part of site commissioning and not an afterthought.

Sensor Fault

Flag the channel, continue the mission with the remaining sensors, and mark the profile partial. Never interpolate over a dead sensor. A smooth line through missing data is a lie the eye believes.

Ditching

Assume it will happen. The aircraft should float, carry a locator beacon, and contain its lithium. A sunk drone in a lake used for food production is an environmental incident, not just a lost asset.

Site Commissioning

Note

What must happen before the first sortie at any new site. Skipping this is how sites become incidents.

Steps
  • Airspace check and, where required, CAAP coordination.
  • LGU and water body authority permission. In Philippine lakes the LGU is the practical gatekeeper.
  • Community notification. Not a legal requirement in most cases; a social licence requirement in all of them. A drone that appears over a lake without warning acquires a reputation before it acquires a customer.
  • Dock siting: shore access, power or solar, connectivity, security.
  • Obstacle survey: power lines, masts, trees, and the cage structure itself.
  • Alternate landing site identification.
  • Bathymetry and station definition — profiles are only comparable across time if the stations are fixed.
  • Baseline profiles at every station across at least one diel cycle, before any claim is made about anything.
  • Ingest available Copernicus history for the water body. Free, and it gives the site a past on day one.

Training and Handover

Principle

The farm's people must be able to run the routine and recognise the abnormal without calling us.

Detail File
data/08_operations/training-certification.json
The Test

Can the technician clean the probe correctly, swap a battery, and recognise a fouled sensor reading? If not, the deployment will degrade quietly into confidently wrong data — which is worse than no data, because someone will act on it.
