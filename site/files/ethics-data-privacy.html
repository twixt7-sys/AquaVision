Ethics, Data Privacy & Responsible Use

ethics-data-privacy v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

Why This File Matters

A platform that flies cameras over shared water, holds sensitive farm data, dispenses substances, and could be turned to surveillance carries real ethical weight. These are not compliance checkboxes; several of them are places where the company could do genuine harm, and a couple are places where the technology's second-order effects run against its stated purpose. This file names them plainly.

Data Ownership

Principle
The farmer's data belongs to the farmer. We are custodians, not owners.
Why It Binds Us

It rules out the data-monetisation business models an investor will inevitably suggest — selling aggregated farm data, or building a proprietary dataset the farmer cannot take with them. Those models are available and lucrative and we are declining them, and the declining has to be real, in the architecture, not just in a values statement.

Commitments
  • Data is exportable in an open format, always.
  • Data is deletable on request.
  • Per-tenant isolation by construction, not by policy.
  • If we leave, the farmer keeps their history. See training-certification.json#/knowledge_transfer_and_exit.

The Phase 1 Scale Shift

Statement

The phased platform story changes the shape of this problem, not just its scale. Phase 1 collects farmer-entered personal and farm data from thousands of beginner/smallholder users, from day one, as the deliberate acquisition and data-accumulation engine — years before any drone ever flies. The custodian-not-owner commitment above now has to hold at that scale and that early, not just for a handful of Phase 3 enterprise pilot sites.

What Is New
  • Volume and timing: far more individuals' personal and farm data, collected far earlier in the company's life, before the governance and support processes have been proven at scale.
  • Sensitivity mix: account/identity data, farm location and production data, and self-reported livelihood-relevant information, all from users with less bargaining power and less digital-privacy literacy than an enterprise Phase 3 customer.
  • The custodian principle is unchanged, but it is now load-bearing for a much larger and more vulnerable population from the very first release, not phased in gradually.
The Honest Admission

A data-custody promise made to ten enterprise pilot farms is easy to keep by hand. The same promise made to thousands of smallholders through an app requires it to be true by construction — export, deletion, and per-tenant isolation have to work as shipped features, not as a support-ticket process, because there is no field team to do it manually at this scale.

Community and Marketplace Data Considerations

Statement

The Phase 1 community forum and the Phase 2 marketplace each introduce a data-governance question that the original hardware-only story never had to answer.

Community Forum Content Moderation
What It Is

Farmer-to-farmer discussion, questions, and shared experience — valuable for the community-led, low-CAC growth story, and a genuine source of the qualitative signal the product is otherwise missing at Phase 1.

The Tension

Moderation requires reading user-generated content, which sits close to the same surveillance line already drawn in the_surveillance_temptation below — the difference is the data is text a farmer chose to post publicly, not imagery captured incidentally. Still requires a stated moderation policy, a way to report harm, and a clear line against using forum content for anything beyond community health (no repurposing posts as marketing or training data without separate consent).

Status
·Policy Not Yet Drafted — Flagged Here Rather Than Assumed Benign.
Marketplace Transaction Data
What It Is

Phase 2 marketplace activity (feed/equipment purchases, buyer connections) generates commercially sensitive transaction data distinct from water-quality or farm-log data.

The Tension

Transaction data is exactly the kind of dataset that is commercially valuable to aggregate and sell (to feed suppliers, for market intelligence) — which is precisely the data-monetisation model the custodian principle above already declines. The commitment has to extend explicitly to marketplace data, not just farm-log data, or it is a loophole.

Commitment

Marketplace transaction data is held under the same custodian-not-owner principle as farm data: not sold or shared with counterparties beyond what completing the transaction requires, and exportable/deletable on the same terms.

The Aerial Imagery Problem

Statement

A drone flying over a lake photographs houses, boats, shorelines, and people who are not our customers and did not consent.

Why It Is Serious

This is personal data collected incidentally, and in a lake community it is also a social-licence question. The frames most useful for training our models are the ones over the busiest, most populated shorelines — so the scientific value and the privacy risk are maximised in the same place. That tension is real and unresolved.

Controls
  • Collect the minimum imagery necessary for the water quality and anomaly tasks.
  • No person-detection models. Not because it is hard — because building the capability changes what this company is, and a capability that exists will eventually be used.
  • Retention limits on raw imagery containing identifiable people, regardless of its training value.
  • On-board or edge blurring/masking of shoreline structures where feasible.
  • Geofence surveys to the water body; do not overfly dwellings unnecessarily.
  • Community notification at site commissioning — a social requirement, not just a legal one.

The Surveillance Temptation

Statement
The platform is an excellent surveillance tool, and it must not become one.
The Capabilities We Are Declining
  • IUU-fishing enforcement — detecting unauthorised vessels. Technically easy, and it would turn a farm instrument into an enforcement tool, making adversaries of the same coastal communities we need as customers.
  • Personnel monitoring — tracking who is where, how fast a technician responded. It would destroy the trust the technician relationship depends on. See risk R-12 and the technician persona.
  • Neighbour monitoring — using imagery to document a competitor's stocking or practices.
Why Declining Matters

Each of these is a real capability the hardware already has. Choosing not to build the software for them is an active choice, and it is the choice that keeps the company's relationship with lake communities collaborative rather than adversarial. A surveillance drone and a monitoring drone can be the same aircraft; they are not the same company.

The Governance

These exclusions should be written into the company's charter or its terms, not left to case-by-case discretion, because the commercial pressure to add 'just one' enforcement contract will be real.

The Overstocking Paradox

Statement

Better instruments may let operators push stocking density closer to the edge, worsening the systemic risk the product exists to reduce.

Why It Is Uncomfortable

The root cause of the disaster we sell against is over-stocking. Our product does not reduce stocking; it gives operators more confidence to approach the limit. There is a real version of this technology that makes the commons problem worse by enabling intensification.

Our Partial Answer
  • Carrying-capacity analytics that surface the SYSTEMIC cost, aimed at cooperatives and regulators who have an interest in the whole lake.
  • Not marketing the product as a licence to intensify.
  • Positioning the shared-lake data as a governance asset, not just a per-farm optimisation tool.
The Honest Admission

This is a partial answer, not a solution. An individual operator with better instruments and a profit motive may stock harder regardless of what our lake-wide analytics say. We cannot fully resolve this, and the thesis ethics chapter should say so rather than pretend the technology is unambiguously beneficial. See risk R-15.

The Antimicrobial Stewardship Tension

Statement
A drone that makes dosing easier makes over-dosing easier.
Detail

Lowering the friction on applying a substance increases the behaviour. When the substance is an antimicrobial and the operator is frightened of an outbreak, easier dosing may mean more prophylactic antimicrobial use — which is already a public health problem. Our contribution to antimicrobial stewardship could plausibly be negative.

Controls
  • Firmware dose ceilings not overridable from the dashboard.
  • Per-mission human authorisation.
  • Substance-class gating with veterinary medicines legally separated.
  • Precision placement that reduces total dose for a given effect — the one genuinely positive lever.
Honest Note
The controls are weak against a determined operator. This deserves a paragraph in the thesis, not silence. See carriage-ejection-system.json#/the_safety_architecture.

Algorithmic Responsibility

Principles
  • No unvalidated predictive claim reaches a farmer. A false 'you are safe' can cost a livelihood. See ai-ml-models.json.
  • Every alert explains its evidence and states what it does not know.
  • The system advises; the human decides and is accountable. Consequential actions are never autonomous.
  • Confidence shown to users is calibrated, or it is not shown as a number.
  • Models are evaluated on held-out sites and their failures are published.

Equity and Extraction

Statement

Research and commerce on smallholders can be extractive, and this sector has a history of it.

Commitments
  • Compensate farmers for research participation.
  • Informed consent that accounts for the power imbalance — a farmer who thinks participation brings a subsidy is not freely consenting.
  • Do not build a product that only serves the well-capitalised farms while studying the smallholders. The impact story is hollow if the smallholder is only ever a research subject and never a beneficiary.
  • The cooperative and financing channels exist so that the people with the greatest need are reached, not just observed.
The Hard Truth
The stakeholder with the most acute need can least afford the product. Every honest document in this repository returns to this. It is an equity problem the company must actively work against, or it will default — as ventures do — to serving whoever can pay, and quietly abandon the mission it was founded on.

The Data Governance Gap

Statement

The lake-scale carrying-capacity use case needs cross-farm data, and the operator who benefits from the lake-wide picture is the one who least wants their own stocking visible in it.

Status
·UNRESOLVED — A Genuine Open Problem, Flagged Rather Than Pretended Solved.
The Tension

The public good (managing shared carrying capacity) requires aggregating private, sensitive, commercially damaging data. The individual incentive is to withhold. Standard aggregation and anonymisation are weak when there are only forty operators on a named lake.

Candidate Directions
  • Explicit, revocable, per-purpose consent.
  • Differentially private aggregate outputs.
  • Cooperative-level governance where members collectively consent to lake-wide analysis.
  • Federated approaches that never centralise raw farm data.
Why It Is Here and Not Solved

Because it is cheap to design for now and impossible to retrofit, and because pretending it is solved would be worse than admitting it is open. See system-architecture.json and api-specification.json, both of which default to no sharing precisely because this is unresolved.

Responsible Use Summary

The Commitments That Cost Us Something
  • No data monetisation — declines a lucrative model.
  • No surveillance capabilities — declines enforcement contracts.
  • No unvalidated predictions — declines the best demo feature.
  • No autonomous dispensing — declines a capability that would impress.
  • Compensated, consenting research — costs money and slows the work.
  • Serve the smallholder, not just the payer — costs margin.
Why List Them This Way

A responsible-use section that costs the company nothing is a marketing document. These commitments each forgo something real. That is how you tell whether an ethics policy is load-bearing: look for what it declines, not what it professes.
