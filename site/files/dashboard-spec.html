Dashboard Specification

dashboard-spec v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

One Shell Three Tiers

Statement

There is one app shell, not three separate products. What a user sees is gated by tier (free / Premium / enterprise), which usually — but not always — lines up with phase (1 / 2 / 3).

Tiers
Tier
Free — Phase 1
Audience
Beginner and smallholder farmers, the acquisition base.
Views
  • AI assistant chat
  • Farm setup wizard
  • Water quality log (manual/photo entry)
  • Feeding schedule & reminders
  • Disease identification (photo)
  • Community forum
  • Learning modules
  • Records / history
Tier
Premium — Phase 2
Audience

Farms that have outgrown manual tracking and want more from the same logged data, no new hardware required.

Views
  • Everything in Free, plus:
  • Analytics
  • Predictive health (advisory)
  • Recommendations
  • Marketplace
  • Expert consultation booking & messaging
Tier
Enterprise — Phase 3
Audience
Commercial and large operators who have added OmniDrone/IoT hardware.
Views
  • Everything in Premium, plus:
  • Site overview
  • Profile view (depth-resolved)
  • PondTwin
  • Map
  • Fleet health
The Rule
A farm can be on Premium without ever touching Phase 3 hardware — the marketplace, consultations, and analytics do not require a sensor. A farm cannot reach the Enterprise views without the hardware, because those views render data that does not exist without it.

The Governing Insight

Statement

The dashboard must be an ANSWER, not an analytics tool — and 'the answer' is a different thing depending on who is asking.

Reasoning

A free-tier beginner opening the app most days wants to know what to do today: log the water, feed on schedule, ask the assistant a question. An enterprise farm manager at 05:30 wants to know whether her fish are in danger right now. Both are the same principle — minimise the distance between opening the app and knowing what to do — applied to very different stakes and very different data richness.

The Test

Can she get the answer in under five seconds, on a phone, in sunlight, half awake? If not, we have built a tool for ourselves and called it a product. This test applies to the Phase 1 home screen exactly as much as to the Phase 3 site overview.

The Second Screen

Everything else — the profiles, the maps, the history, the forum, the learning library — lives one level down, for the moments a user wants to explore or learn rather than decide. That layer is genuinely valuable and it must not be the front door.

The Phase 1 2 Home View

Content

A single home screen: the assistant entry point, today's feeding reminder, a prompt to log water quality if it has been a while, and a status strip showing the farm's own logged trend — never dressed up as more certain than it is.

The Sparse Data Problem

Most free-tier farms will log irregularly. The home view must say so plainly — 'last logged 9 days ago' reads the same way the Phase 3 staleness rule does, and for the same reason: a stale or sparse log must never quietly read as 'fine'.

Empty States Are Not Failure States

A brand-new account with nothing logged yet should feel like an invitation (finish setup, log your first reading, ask the assistant a question), not a broken dashboard. This is the single highest-traffic screen in the product and the one most likely to be seen by a skeptical first-time user.

The Top Level View

Content

One line per site or farm: is anything wrong, and how sure are we? Applies to a Phase 3 enterprise site exactly as specified below; for a Phase 1/2 farm the same five states apply to the farm's manually logged trend rather than continuous sensor telemetry.

The Critical Rule

NO DATA MUST NEVER READ AS SAFE. This is the single most important rule in the UI, at every tier. A grey tile that a tired person glances at and reads as 'not red, therefore fine' is how a monitoring system kills someone's harvest — and a free-tier farmer who has not logged in two weeks deserves the same grey-not-green honesty as an enterprise site with a dead sensor. Grey must feel like an absence, not a reassurance — and it must be visually distinct from green in a way that survives sunlight, colour blindness, and a two-second glance.

The Staleness Rule

Every state carries its age. 'Nominal, measured 14 minutes ago' and 'Nominal, logged 9 days ago' are radically different statements, and the app must never present them with the same visual confidence. Data has a half-life; the UI must show it decaying, whether the data came from a sonde or a farmer's thumb.

Accessibility As Safety

Statement

This is not a compliance checkbox. It is a safety requirement, and it applies to the smallholder's low-end Android phone as much as to the enterprise operator's tablet.

Rules
  • Status is NEVER encoded by colour alone. Every status carries an icon and a text label. Roughly 1 in 12 men has a red/green colour vision deficiency, and that demographic is heavily represented among fish farmers.
  • Dark mode is mandatory, not a preference. Operators and farmers alike check this before dawn, and a white screen at 04:30 is genuinely hostile — it destroys night vision on a person who may be about to walk onto a dike in the dark.
  • Readable in direct tropical sunlight on a phone. Test it outside, not in the office.
  • Tap targets sized for wet hands.
  • The critical path must work on a slow connection and a low-end device. A free-tier farmer on a budget Android phone with one bar of signal is the median user, not an edge case.
  • The app's core Phase 1 flows (log water quality, log feeding, view the assistant) must work fully offline and sync later — this is the same offline-first requirement as the Phase 3 dock, applied to a phone.

Alert Design

The Principle

An alert that cannot explain itself will be ignored the third time, and the third time may be the real one. This holds whether the alert came from a calibrated sensor or from a rule evaluated against a farmer's own logged numbers.

Every Alert Carries
  • What was measured or logged, where, and when — and which of the two it was.
  • What threshold it crossed, and where that threshold came from — cite the source.
  • The evidence: the profile chart, the image crop, or the logged entry.
  • What we do NOT know. If the ammonia channel was QC-failed, or a Phase 1/2 farm hasn't logged ammonia at all, say so.
  • A concrete suggested action, or an honest 'we do not have a recommendation'.
The Language Rule
Bad
Risk score: 78/100
Good

Bottom 3 m at cages 3-5 is anoxic (1.1 mg/L at 6 m, measured 04:52). Heavy rain forecast tonight. If the column mixes, that water reaches your fish.

Good Phase 1 2 Example

You logged low dissolved oxygen twice this week and haven't logged since. Consider a fresh reading before your next feeding.

Why

The score is unvalidated and it hides its reasoning. The sentence is measured (or logged) fact plus a stated mechanism, and it lets a farmer — experienced or novice — apply their own judgement to it. Given that, it is not a compromise — it is a better product, at every tier.

Alert Fatigue

Track the acknowledge-to-action rate as a first-class product metric, per tier. If people stop acting on alerts, the product has failed no matter how accurate the sensors — or how well-designed the logging reminders — are. See data-pipeline.json#/the_alert_fatigue_problem.

The view must state the data-completeness basis of every chart. A farm with three logged entries in two months gets an honest 'not enough data yet' state, never a smoothed-looking trend line built from almost nothing.

The Axis Rule

Depth increases downward. Always. Never invert it to make the chart look like a conventional line graph. The whole point is to make the water column legible, and a depth axis that runs upward breaks the one intuition the user has.

Features
  • Overlay yesterday's profile
  • Overlay the dawn and afternoon profiles to show diel amplitude
  • Threshold lines drawn with their citations
  • QC flags visible per point — a suspect reading must look suspect

The Hard Requirement

Interpolated volume must look less certain than measured points. A smooth confident gradient over unmeasured water is the most persuasive lie this product could tell, and it is the version that demos best.

View
Map
Audience
Enterprise (Phase 3) manager
Tier
enterprise
Content
The calibrated concentration field over the site.
Requirement

The uncertainty field must be viewable, and the calibration quality (n points, R²) must be visible on the map itself — not buried in metadata. A map fitted on 3 points with an R² of 0.41 should look different from one fitted on 8 points with an R² of 0.92.

View
History
Audience
Manager, owner, auditor (any tier, scoped to what that tier has logged)
Tier
free/premium/enterprise
Content

Time series with events overlaid: log entries or sorties, alerts, feeding, aeration runs, dispensing, weather, mortality — whichever apply to the farm's tier.

Why It Matters

This is where the farmer's intuition and the data meet, at any tier. A manager who sees the shape from the week before her last kill, and recognises it, has learned something no alert could teach her. It is also the view that makes the case for staying subscribed — the value compounds with duration, whether that duration is measured in sensor-days or logged entries.

View
Reports
Audience
Manager, auditor
Tier
premium/enterprise
Content
Date-ranged environmental export with QC flags, source_type, and provenance intact.
The Discipline

Include the QC flags and the self-reported labels. An auditor should see which readings we do not trust, and which were never instrument-measured at all. Hiding either makes the report look better and be worth less — and an auditor who later discovers we filtered silently will disbelieve everything.

enterprise

Aircraft, dock, sonde status. Calibration due dates. Sonde site assignment.
Note

Sonde site assignment appears here because it is a biosecurity control. A sonde at the wrong site is an incident, not a logistics detail.

What the Dashboard Will Not Show

Item
A technician performance metric
Why

The moment we surface 'response time by staff member', the technician becomes the adversary of the system. They charge the batteries and clean the probe. If they perceive it as surveillance of them, it will be broken every time it matters and we will never find out why. This is a product decision derived directly from the technician persona and it must survive the owner asking for it.

Item

An unvalidated fish kill risk score, or its Phase 1/2 predictive-health equivalent, shown as a bare number

Why

A false negative reads as 'you are safe'. Until validated, show the mechanism and let the farmer conclude. See ai-ml-models.json#/models/kill-risk-forecast and #/models/predictive-health-anomaly.

Item
A toxicity indicator
Why
We measure pigments, not toxins. People eat the fish.
Item
A confident biomass or stock count
Why

We do not measure biomass at any tier. Displaying the farmer's own estimate back to them in an authoritative font launders a guess into a fact.

Item
Interpolation across a sensor outage or a logging gap
Why

A smooth line through missing data is a lie the eye believes, whether the gap is a dead sensor or two weeks a farmer forgot to log. Render gaps as gaps.

Item

A manually-logged reading styled with the same visual confidence as a calibrated sensor reading

Why

The whole point of the source_type discriminator in the data model is defeated if the UI erases the distinction it exists to preserve.

Item
Specific medication dosing from the assistant or disease-ID flow
Why
That is a veterinary decision. The product routes to a human; it does not make the call.

The Webpage Consuming This Repository

Note

This repository is designed to be read by a custom HTML page. That page is a different artefact from the operational/app dashboard above, and conflating them would produce something that serves neither.

Guidance
  • Fetch manifest.json first; lazy-load sections from index.files[] by id.
  • Render the provenance badge on EVERY quantitative claim. The badge palette is in brand-identity.json#/palette/provenance_badges. This is a load-bearing safety feature of the repository, not decoration — do not strip it for visual cleanliness.
  • The `illustrative_synthetic` badge must be impossible to miss. That data describes no real pond and no real farmer.
  • Chart payloads under data/12_datasets/charts/ conform to schema-registry.json#/definitions/chart_payload and can be rendered generically.
  • Where a record has an `honest_note`, `caveat`, or `the_hard_truth` field, that is usually the intellectually important part. Do not collapse it behind a 'read more'.
  • Cross-link glossary terms wherever they appear in prose.
