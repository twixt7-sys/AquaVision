Glossary — Aquaculture, Fisheries, Remote Sensing, and Systems Terms

glossary v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 raw JSON ↗

Controlled vocabulary for the whole repository and for the thesis. Where a term has a contested definition, both readings are given. Terms are grouped for UI filtering but the flat `terms[]` array is authoritative.

Terms

Term
Aquaculture
Abbrev
—
Group
aquaculture
Definition

The farming of aquatic organisms — fish, molluscs, crustaceans, and aquatic plants — involving intervention in the rearing process to enhance production, such as regular stocking, feeding, and protection from predators. Implies individual or corporate ownership of the stock being cultivated, which is the line that separates it from capture fisheries.

Citations
fao-sofia-2024
Note

The ownership criterion matters legally and statistically: an organism becomes 'aquaculture' the moment someone owns and intervenes in it.

Term
Capture Fisheries
Abbrev
—
Group
aquaculture
Definition

The harvesting of naturally occurring aquatic organisms from marine or inland waters, without ownership of or intervention in the stock prior to capture.

Citations
fao-sofia-2024
Term
Cage Culture
Group
aquaculture
Definition

Rearing of fish in enclosures of net or mesh suspended in an existing water body (lake, reservoir, bay), where water exchanges freely with the surrounding environment. High stocking density, no control of inflow water quality — which is precisely why cage culture is the most exposed to ambient dissolved oxygen crashes and algal blooms.

Note

The dominant mode in Philippine lake systems such as Taal and Laguna de Bay. Its vulnerability profile is the OmniDrone's core market rationale.

Term
Pond Culture
Group
aquaculture
Definition

Rearing of fish in constructed earthen or lined impoundments where the operator controls inflow, outflow, and often aeration. Lower ambient risk than cage culture but higher exposure to sediment and effluent management issues.

Term
Recirculating Aquaculture System
Abbrev
RAS
Group
aquaculture
Definition

A land-based, largely closed system in which water is mechanically and biologically filtered and reused. Highest capital cost, highest control, lowest environmental exchange.

Note

RAS is largely OUT of the OmniDrone's addressable market — an indoor tank does not need an aerial vehicle. Stated here so the market sizing does not over-claim.

Term
Polyculture
Group
aquaculture
Definition

Culturing two or more species together in the same unit, chosen so their feeding niches complement rather than compete — for example milkfish with tilapia and shrimp.

Term
Integrated Multi-Trophic Aquaculture
Abbrev
IMTA
Group
aquaculture
Definition

Deliberately co-culturing species from different trophic levels so that the waste of one becomes the input of another — fed finfish alongside filter-feeding shellfish and nutrient-extracting seaweed.

Term
Stocking Density
Group
aquaculture
Definition

Biomass or number of individuals per unit volume or area of culture water, e.g. kg/m³ or fingerlings/ha. The single most common root cause cited in Philippine fish-kill investigations, because density drives oxygen demand.

Citations
pna-tilapia-fishkill inquirer-taal-fishkill-2023
Term
Carrying Capacity
Group
aquaculture
Definition

The maximum standing biomass a water body can sustain without breaching water quality thresholds. Distinguish ecological carrying capacity (what the ecosystem tolerates) from production carrying capacity (what maximises yield) — they are not the same number and conflating them is how lakes get over-stocked.

Term
Fish Kill
Group
aquaculture
Definition

A sudden mass mortality event in a water body. In Philippine inland aquaculture the dominant proximate causes are dissolved oxygen depletion, ammonia and hydrogen sulfide toxicity following water column overturn, and harmful algal blooms — usually with over-stocking as the underlying cause and a weather trigger as the precipitant.

Citations
seafdec-laguna-fishkill bioflux-lake-buhi-2020 inquirer-taal-fishkill-2023
Term
Overturn
Aka
  • Turnover
  • Upwelling
  • Destratification event
Group
water_quality
Definition

The abrupt vertical mixing of a stratified water body, driven by surface cooling, heavy rain, or strong wind. It drags oxygen-poor, sulfide- and ammonia-rich hypolimnetic water to the surface where the fish are. This is the mechanism behind most catastrophic lake fish kills, and it can complete in hours — which is the entire argument for continuous rather than weekly monitoring.

Note

Critical concept for the OmniDrone thesis: a weekly grab sample has roughly zero probability of catching the precursor conditions.

Term
Thermal Stratification
Group
water_quality
Definition

Layering of a water body by temperature and therefore density, into a warm mixed surface epilimnion, a sharp-gradient metalimnion (thermocline), and a cold, often anoxic bottom hypolimnion.

Note

The reeled probe's core value: a surface sensor cannot see stratification at all. Only a vertical profile can.

Term
Epilimnion
Group
water_quality
Definition
The upper, warm, wind-mixed, generally oxygenated layer of a stratified water body.
Term
Hypolimnion
Group
water_quality
Definition

The lower, cold, dense, poorly mixed layer of a stratified water body. Frequently hypoxic or anoxic, and the reservoir of the ammonia and hydrogen sulfide that an overturn delivers to the fish.

Term
Thermocline
Group
water_quality
Definition

The zone of steep temperature change separating epilimnion from hypolimnion. Its depth and sharpness are the primary state variables for overturn risk.

Term
Dissolved Oxygen
Abbrev
DO
Group
water_quality
Definition

The concentration of molecular oxygen dissolved in water, in mg/L or as percent saturation. The master variable of aquaculture: everything else can be wrong slowly, but DO kills in hours.

Operational Thresholds
Note

Species- and life-stage-specific. See data/09_compliance/water-quality-standards.json for the sourced table.

Warmwater Finfish General
>= 5 mg/L desirable; < 3 mg/L acute stress; < 2 mg/L mortality risk
Citations
igs-do-aquaculture-2026 boyd-water-quality-2020
Term
Diel Cycle
Aka
  • Diurnal cycle
Group
water_quality
Definition

The 24-hour oscillation of dissolved oxygen and pH driven by photosynthesis by day and respiration by night. DO peaks in mid-afternoon and reaches its minimum just before dawn.

Note

The pre-dawn minimum is when fish die and when nobody is on the water. A monitoring regime that samples at 9 a.m. systematically measures the safest moment of the day and concludes the pond is fine.

Term
Hypoxia
Group
water_quality
Definition

Oxygen concentration low enough to impair normal biological function. In aquaculture practice conventionally below about 3 mg/L for warmwater finfish, though the biologically meaningful threshold is species-specific.

Citations
ncbi-takifugu-hypoxia-2022
Term
Anoxia
Group
water_quality
Definition

Effectively complete absence of dissolved oxygen. Drives the chemical reduction reactions that generate hydrogen sulfide in bottom sediments.

Term
Biochemical Oxygen Demand
Abbrev
BOD
Group
water_quality
Definition

The mass of oxygen consumed by microorganisms decomposing organic matter in a water sample over a fixed period, conventionally five days at 20 °C. A proxy for organic pollution load — uneaten feed and faeces in an aquaculture context.

Term
Chemical Oxygen Demand
Abbrev
COD
Group
water_quality
Definition

Oxygen equivalent of the organic matter oxidisable by a strong chemical oxidant. Faster to measure than BOD and correlated with it, but not interchangeable.

Term
Total Ammonia Nitrogen
Abbrev
TAN
Group
water_quality
Definition

The sum of ionised ammonium (NH4+) and un-ionised ammonia (NH3) expressed as nitrogen. Only the un-ionised fraction is acutely toxic, and that fraction rises sharply with pH and temperature — which is why TAN must never be interpreted without simultaneous pH and temperature.

Note

This coupling is a design driver: the probe must measure pH, temperature, and ammonia in the same instant at the same depth, or the ammonia number is uninterpretable.

Term
Un-ionised Ammonia
Abbrev
NH3
Group
water_quality
Definition

The neutral, lipid-soluble, gill-permeable and therefore toxic form of ammonia. Fraction of TAN present as NH3 increases with pH and temperature.

Term
Nitrite
Abbrev
NO2-
Group
water_quality
Definition

Intermediate in nitrification. Toxic because it oxidises haemoglobin to methaemoglobin, producing 'brown blood disease' and functional asphyxiation even in fully oxygenated water.

Term
Nitrate
Abbrev
NO3-
Group
water_quality
Definition

End product of nitrification. Comparatively low acute toxicity but a driver of eutrophication.

Term
Hydrogen Sulfide
Abbrev
H2S
Group
water_quality
Definition

Produced by sulfate-reducing bacteria in anoxic sediment. Extremely toxic to fish at low concentrations, and more toxic at low pH where the un-ionised form dominates. Delivered to the water column by overturn.

Citations
seafdec-laguna-fishkill
Term
pH
Group
water_quality
Definition

Negative log of hydrogen ion activity. In aquaculture, pH is a modulator variable more than a direct killer: it controls ammonia and sulfide toxicity, and it swings diurnally with photosynthesis.

Term
Alkalinity
Group
water_quality
Definition

The buffering capacity of water against pH change, mostly carbonate and bicarbonate, expressed as mg/L CaCO3. Low alkalinity means wild diurnal pH swings; it is the reason liming is a standard intervention.

Term
Turbidity
Group
water_quality
Definition

Optical cloudiness of water from suspended particles, in NTU or FNU. Confounds every optical sensor on the platform, which is why it must be measured alongside them rather than treated as a nuisance.

Term
Secchi Depth
Group
water_quality
Definition

The depth at which a standard black-and-white disc disappears from view. The oldest and still most widely used field measure of water transparency and, by proxy, plankton density.

Note

Worth preserving in the product: farmers trust it, and it gives the OmniDrone a free ground-truth calibration target that every extension worker already understands.

Term
Salinity
Group
water_quality
Definition

Dissolved salt content, in ppt or PSU. Critical in brackish-water culture such as Philippine milkfish and shrimp ponds; also drives oxygen solubility.

Term
Electrical Conductivity
Abbrev
EC
Group
water_quality
Definition

Ability of water to conduct current; the practical proxy from which salinity and total dissolved solids are derived.

Term
Oxidation-Reduction Potential
Abbrev
ORP
Group
water_quality
Definition

Millivolt measure of the oxidising or reducing tendency of water. Useful as an early integrative indicator of sediment and organic loading before DO itself collapses.

Term
Eutrophication
Group
water_quality
Definition

Nutrient enrichment — chiefly nitrogen and phosphorus — driving excessive primary production, whose subsequent decomposition depletes oxygen. In aquaculture the system is frequently the source of its own eutrophication via uneaten feed.

Citations
fao-nutrient-dynamics-eutrophic
Note

The reflexive quality of this problem is a strong thesis theme: the farm poisons its own water, and cannot see itself doing it.

Term
Harmful Algal Bloom
Abbrev
HAB
Group
biology_health
Definition

Proliferation of algae that harms fish or people, whether by producing toxins, by mechanically damaging gills, or by causing oxygen depletion on collapse. Not all blooms are toxic and not all toxic events are visible blooms — a distinction the marketing must not blur.

Citations
mdpi-hab-meta-2021 hab-review-2024
Term
Chlorophyll-a
Abbrev
Chl-a
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

The dominant photosynthetic pigment in algae and the most widely used optical proxy for algal biomass; used as the HAB indicator in roughly 80 percent of remote sensing studies.

Citations
mdpi-hab-meta-2021
Caveat
Chl-a indicates algal biomass, not toxicity. A high Chl-a reading is a reason to sample, not a diagnosis.
Term
Phycocyanin
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

Accessory pigment characteristic of cyanobacteria. More specific to the taxa that produce freshwater toxins than Chl-a is, and therefore a better bloom-risk discriminator where the sensor can resolve it (~620 nm).

Term
Normalized Difference Chlorophyll Index
Abbrev
NDCI
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

A spectral band ratio using red and red-edge reflectance to estimate chlorophyll-a in turbid inland water, where classic blue-green ocean algorithms fail.

Formula
NDCI = (R_rededge - R_red) / (R_rededge + R_red)
Citations
mishra-ndci-2012 mdpi-hab-meta-2021
Term
Normalized Difference Water Index
Abbrev
NDWI
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

Band ratio using green and near-infrared reflectance to delineate open water from land. The OmniDrone's first processing step: find the water before analysing it.

Formula
NDWI = (R_green - R_nir) / (R_green + R_nir)
Citations
mcfeeters-ndwi-1996
Term
Coloured Dissolved Organic Matter
Abbrev
CDOM
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

Optically active dissolved organic compounds that absorb strongly in the blue. A major confounder for chlorophyll retrieval in inland waters and a variable of interest in its own right.

Citations
mdpi-drones-inland-review-2024
Term
Total Suspended Solids
Abbrev
TSS
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

Mass concentration of particles suspended in water. Drives backscatter and therefore dominates the optical signal in shallow, wind-mixed tropical ponds.

Citations
mdpi-drones-inland-review-2024
Term
Multispectral Imaging
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

Imaging in a small number (typically 4–10) of discrete, relatively broad spectral bands. The practical trade-off point for UAV water quality work: enough spectral information for band-ratio indices, low enough data volume to process on board.

Citations
sciencedirect-uav-multispectral-ponds-2024
Term
Hyperspectral Imaging
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

Imaging in many (often hundreds of) narrow contiguous bands, permitting species-level pigment discrimination. Higher cost, mass, and data burden.

Citations
mdpi-hyperspectral-hab-2025
Term
Radiometric Calibration
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

Conversion of raw sensor digital numbers into physical reflectance, correcting for illumination, sensor response, and atmosphere. Without it, indices computed on two different days are not comparable — and a time series of uncalibrated indices is worse than no time series, because it looks like data.

Note

The reason the specification mandates a downwelling irradiance sensor and a calibrated reflectance panel in the dock.

Term
Sun Glint
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

Specular reflection of the sun off the water surface into the sensor, saturating the signal and destroying water-leaving radiance. Mitigated by flight geometry, viewing angle, and polarising filters. The dominant reason UAV water surveys are flown at constrained solar angles.

Note

A physical constraint that directly shapes the mission planner's allowable time windows. Cannot be engineered away in software.

Term
Water-Leaving Radiance
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

The portion of light that penetrates the water surface, interacts with the water column, and re-emerges — the only part of the signal carrying information about water constituents.

Term
Ground Sample Distance
Abbrev
GSD
Group
remote_sensing
Definition

Real-world distance represented by one image pixel. Determines whether a floating dead fish is a detectable object or a single ambiguous pixel.

Formula
GSD = (sensor_pixel_pitch x altitude) / focal_length
Term
Unmanned Aerial Vehicle
Abbrev
UAV
Aka
  • Drone
  • RPA
  • UAS
  • RPAS
Group
uav_systems
Definition

An aircraft operated without a pilot on board. Philippine civil aviation regulation uses 'RPAS' (Remotely Piloted Aircraft System) as the governing term, which is the vocabulary the compliance file uses.

Citations
caap-rpas-regulations
Term
Visual Line of Sight
Abbrev
VLOS
Group
uav_systems
Definition

Operation in which the remote pilot maintains direct unaided visual contact with the aircraft. The default permitted mode under CAAP rules.

Citations
caap-rpas-regulations
Term
Beyond Visual Line of Sight
Abbrev
BVLOS
Group
uav_systems
Definition

Operation outside the remote pilot's direct visual contact. Requires specific CAAP authorisation. The economic unlock for autonomous dock-based operation, and the single largest regulatory dependency in this entire business plan.

Citations
caap-rpas-regulations caap-types-of-operations
Term
Maximum Take-Off Mass
Abbrev
MTOM
Aka
  • Gross weight
  • MTOW
Group
uav_systems
Definition

Maximum permitted mass of the aircraft at take-off including payload, fuel or battery. The parameter on which Philippine regulatory obligations bracket — the 7 kg boundary is the one that matters here.

Citations
caap-rpas-regulations
Term
Return to Home
Abbrev
RTH
Group
uav_systems
Definition

Automated flight mode returning the aircraft to a stored home position, triggered by link loss, low battery, or command. Over water, a conventional RTH that assumes a land home point is a drowning waiting to happen; the failsafe logic must be water-aware.

Term
Geofence
Group
uav_systems
Definition

A software-enforced boundary constraining where the aircraft may fly. Both a safety mechanism and, over leased water lots, a property-rights mechanism.

Term
Ingress Protection Rating
Abbrev
IP
Group
uav_systems
Definition

IEC 60529 two-digit code for enclosure protection against solids and liquids. For a vehicle whose job description includes hovering over water in the tropics and dipping a wet probe into its own airframe, this is a first-order requirement rather than a nice-to-have.

Citations
iec-60529
Term
Payload Fraction
Group
uav_systems
Definition

Useful payload mass divided by maximum take-off mass. The number that decides whether a multirotor concept is viable; for electric multirotors it is characteristically unforgiving.

Term
Disc Loading
Group
uav_systems
Definition

Thrust per unit rotor disc area. Low disc loading means better hover efficiency and endurance but a physically larger aircraft — the central trade in this airframe.

Formula
DL = T / (n * pi * r^2)
Term
Digital Twin
Group
data_ai
Definition

A dynamic virtual representation of a physical system, kept synchronised with it by live sensor data, and used to simulate, predict, and optimise. The distinction from a dashboard is that a twin runs a model of the system's behaviour and can answer counterfactual questions; a dashboard only shows what happened.

Citations
sciencedirect-dt-aiot-2023 hamzah-dt-water-review-2024 pmc-dt-rainbow-trout-2023
Note

This distinction is worth defending explicitly in the thesis, because 'digital twin' is used loosely enough in industry to mean nothing at all.

Term
Precision Aquaculture
Group
data_ai
Definition

Applying the control-engineering logic of precision agriculture to fish farming: observe, interpret, decide, act — with instruments rather than intuition, at the resolution of the individual unit rather than the whole farm.

Citations
globalseafood-precision-aqua
Term
Edge Computing
Group
data_ai
Definition

Executing computation on or near the sensing device rather than in the cloud. Non-negotiable here: rural Philippine aquaculture sites have unreliable connectivity, and a bloom-detection model that requires backhaul is a model that fails on the day it is needed.

Citations
ai-aqua-review-2024
Term
Sensor Fusion
Group
data_ai
Definition

Combining measurements from multiple heterogeneous sensors to produce an estimate better than any single sensor could give. Here: fusing point in-situ probe profiles with area-wide multispectral imagery, so that the probe calibrates the image and the image extrapolates the probe.

Term
Ground Truth
Group
data_ai
Definition

Directly measured reference data against which a model or a remote-sensing retrieval is validated. The reeled probe is the OmniDrone's on-board ground truth generator, which is architecturally the most interesting thing about the vehicle.

Term
Federated Learning
Group
data_ai
Definition

Training a shared model across many data holders without centralising their raw data. Relevant because farm production data is commercially sensitive and farmers have sound reasons to refuse to hand it over.

Citations
ai-aqua-review-2024
Term
Explainable AI
Abbrev
XAI
Group
data_ai
Definition

Methods that render a model's reasoning legible to a human decision-maker. Load-bearing in this product: an alert telling a farmer to spend money on emergency aeration will be ignored unless it says why.

Citations
ai-aqua-review-2024
Term
Technology Readiness Level
Abbrev
TRL
Group
business
Definition

A 1–9 scale describing the maturity of a technology from basic principles observed (1) to proven in operational environment (9). Used throughout this repository to prevent the pitch from implying more maturity than exists.

Citations
nasa-trl
Term
Feed Conversion Ratio
Abbrev
FCR
Group
aquaculture
Definition

Mass of feed supplied divided by mass of animal weight gained. Lower is better. Typically 1.0–2.5 in finfish aquaculture.

Formula
FCR = feed_supplied_kg / biomass_gain_kg
Citations
innovasea-fcr-guide ussec-fcr-power
Note

The economic lever the OmniDrone's feeding function targets. Feed is commonly around 60 percent of operating cost, so an FCR improvement is close to a pure margin improvement.

Term
Specific Growth Rate
Abbrev
SGR
Group
aquaculture
Definition
Percentage increase in body mass per day, computed on natural logs.
Formula
SGR = 100 * (ln(W_final) - ln(W_initial)) / days
Term
Survival Rate
Group
aquaculture
Definition

Proportion of stocked individuals harvested alive. The headline KPI a farmer actually cares about, and the one the OmniDrone's value claim ultimately reduces to.

Term
Biosecurity
Group
biology_health
Definition

The set of practices preventing the introduction and spread of pathogens into and between culture units. Directly constrains the OmniDrone design: a probe dipped in pond A and then pond B is a pathogen vector with rotors.

Citations
worldbank-disease-risk-2014
Note

This is the most commonly overlooked objection to multi-site probe drones, and the repository treats it as a first-class design driver rather than an afterthought.

Term
Total Allowable Catch
Abbrev
TAC
Group
regulatory
Definition
A management-set upper limit on the quantity of a stock that may be landed in a period.
Term
Illegal, Unreported and Unregulated Fishing
Abbrev
IUU
Group
regulatory
Definition

Fishing conducted in contravention of applicable rules, or unreported or unregulated. Named here because aerial surveillance is an adjacent capability with real dual-use implications — see ethics-data-privacy.json.

Term
Fisheries Management Area
Abbrev
FMA
Group
regulatory
Definition

A spatial management unit delineated under Philippine fisheries policy for science-based, ecosystem-approach management.

Term
Ecosystem Approach to Aquaculture
Abbrev
EAA
Group
regulatory
Definition

A management framework integrating aquaculture into the wider ecosystem such that development proceeds within ecological limits and equitably across stakeholders.

Citations
fao-eaa-guidelines
Term
Blue Transformation
Group
business
Definition

FAO's strategic framing for expanding aquatic food systems sustainably: intensify aquaculture responsibly, manage fisheries effectively, and upgrade value chains.

Citations
fao-sofia-2024
