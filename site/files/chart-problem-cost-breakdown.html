The Structure of Aquaculture Losses

Visibility versus addressability — NOT a peso breakdown

chart-problem-cost-breakdown ↗Projection raw JSON ↗
inquirer-taal-fishkill-2023 pna-tilapia-fishkill innovasea-fcr-guide worldbank-disease-risk-2014 fao-nutrient-dynamics-eutrophic

Deliberately rendered as a structured matrix, NOT a pie chart. A pie of peso values would imply we know the proportions, and we do not. What we DO know is which loss categories are visible versus hidden, and which the platform addresses versus does not — noting that 'addressable by OmniDrone' below describes Phase 3 hardware capability specifically; several rows (feed waste, monitoring labour, cost of never learning) are also partially addressable earlier, through Phase 1/2 logging and AI guidance, before any hardware is involved. The most important cell may be the invisible-and-addressable one: chronic sub-lethal loss, which is unmeasured, possibly the largest, and squarely in the platform's capability.

Rendering Guidance

Recommended

A 2x2 matrix — visibility (high/low) on one axis, addressability (high/low) on the other — with categories plotted as cells. The high-value quadrant is invisible-AND-addressable (chronic loss, cost of never learning).

Forbidden

Do NOT render as a pie chart of peso values. We do not have defensible proportions and a pie would fabricate them. See cost-of-inaction.json#/chart_hint.

The Deliberate Absence

There is no total-addressable-loss number, on purpose. Constructing one would multiply sourced figures by invented assumptions into an authoritative-looking number with order-of-magnitude error. See cost-of-inaction.json#/what_this_file_deliberately_does_not_contain.
