Water Quality Standards & Thresholds

water-quality-standards v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ?Assumption ◆Confidence: low raw JSON ↗
⚠THE MOST IMPORTANT WARNING IN THE REPOSITORY

The numerical thresholds below are INDICATIVE PLACEHOLDERS drawn from general aquaculture practice. They have NOT been sourced to primary DENR administrative orders or to species-specific primary literature. THESE NUMBERS DRIVE ALERT THRESHOLDS. An incorrect threshold here becomes a false 'all clear' or a false alarm in production, and a false all-clear can cost a harvest. This is the single highest-consequence set of placeholders in the entire repository. Do not wire these into any alerting logic until they are replaced with sourced, species- and life-stage-specific values verified by an aquaculture scientist.

Phase Framing Note

These thresholds matter across all three phases, but with rising stakes: Phase 1 uses them loosely, as plain-language guidance behind a farmer's self-reported reading; Phase 2's predictive analytics reason over them at the model level; Phase 3's continuous sensor telemetry and automated alerting (the primary audience for this file) is where an incorrect threshold becomes an operational incident. The warning below was written for that Phase 3 stakes level and should be read that way.

Why Thresholds Are Harder Than They Look

Points
  • Thresholds are species-specific AND life-stage-specific. A fingerling and an adult of the same species tolerate different conditions.
  • Toxicity thresholds are coupled. Ammonia toxicity depends on pH and temperature; nitrite toxicity depends on chloride. A single ammonia threshold with no pH context is meaningless.
  • Regulatory criteria (DENR) and biological criteria (what actually harms the fish) are DIFFERENT numbers with different purposes. The system may need to report against both.
  • Chronic and acute thresholds differ. A level that is lethal in an hour differs from one that impairs growth over a cycle — and the chronic one may matter more economically (see the sleeper hypothesis in cost-of-inaction.json).

The Alerting Implication

Statement
The alert thresholds in data-dictionary.json currently reference these indicative values.
The Rule
Those alert thresholds are PROVISIONAL and must be re-derived from sourced species data before any customer relies on them. An alert is a promise that a number means something. Making that promise on an unsourced threshold is the failure mode this whole file exists to prevent.
The Configuration Principle

Thresholds must be per-site and per-species configurable, set by someone who knows the stock — not hard-coded global constants. A tilapia fingerling threshold applied to an adult milkfish pond is a wrong alarm in both directions.

Secchi Depth Note

Why It Is Here

The Secchi disc is the oldest and most trusted field measure of transparency, and every extension worker understands it.

The Opportunity

It gives the OmniDrone a free ground-truth calibration target that farmers already trust. Preserving it in the product — reporting an equivalent Secchi depth alongside the turbidity and chlorophyll numbers — is a cheap trust-building bridge between the instrument and the operator's existing intuition.

What Must Be Obtained

  • DENR water quality DAO and the classification of each target water body.
  • Species- and life-stage-specific DO, ammonia, nitrite, temperature, and pH tolerances from primary literature for tilapia, milkfish, and the shrimp species — verified by an aquaculture scientist.
  • BFAR's operational thresholds and fish-kill investigation criteria.
  • Local reference values for each target lake (Taal's are distinct because of volcanic sulfur input).
