Data Pipeline

data-pipeline v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

The processing path from sensor to decision. Stages are ordered; each stage's failure behaviour is specified, because in a monitoring system a silent pipeline failure is worse than a loud one.

The Governing Rule

A monitoring system that fails silently is worse than no monitoring system, because someone is relying on it. Every stage below must fail loudly or degrade visibly. There is no acceptable silent failure anywhere in this pipeline.

Stages

Acquisition

Stage
1
Location
edge — aircraft
Does

Read sensors, timestamp UTC, tag with depth from the pressure transducer, tag with position and accuracy.

Fails By

Sensor fault flags the channel and continues. The sortie is not aborted for one dead channel — a profile with seven good channels is worth having.

Silent Failure Risk

A fouled sensor does not report a fault. It reports plausible, drifting, confidently wrong numbers. This is the pipeline's most dangerous failure and no stage here catches it — only the scheduled reference check against a laboratory standard does. That check is therefore not a maintenance chore; it is a data integrity control.

QC at source

Stage
2
Location
edge — aircraft
Does

Gross range, spike, rate-of-change, flat-line, and partner-presence tests. Flags applied per QARTOD codes.

Citations
qartod-manual
Why At the Edge

Flagging at source means the flag travels with the data forever. QC applied later in the cloud cannot reconstruct what the sensor was doing at the moment of the reading.

The Flat Line Test

Deserves special mention: a sensor that has stopped responding and started repeating its last value is the failure mode that most resembles good data. A flat line in a diel DO series is physically impossible and it is the cheapest fouling detector available.

Edge inference and reduction

Stage
3
Location
edge — aircraft
Does
NDWI mask, NDCI field, anomaly detection, compression.
Why Here

Raw multispectral imagery is too large to upload over intermittent cellular. And a bloom detection that requires backhaul fails on the day the network is congested — which, in a lake town during an incident, is exactly the day it will be.

Constraint
Every watt costs endurance.

Transfer to dock

Stage
4
Location
edge
Does

Local high-bandwidth link on docking. Full raw imagery moves here; only reduced products go further.

Fails By
Retry. Data stays on the aircraft until acknowledged.

Store and forward

Stage
5
Location
edge — dock
Does
Buffer 7+ days locally. Forward when connectivity permits. Idempotent, resumable sync.
Why It Is Not Optional

The link will be down. The pre-dawn profile — the most valuable data the system produces — must survive it. A dock that loses the dawn profile because the tower was congested has failed at the one thing it exists for.

Requirement Id
REQ-DOCK-005
Clock Note

A dock offline for days still needs UTC accuracy or the diel analysis is corrupted on reconnection. Local RTC discipline matters more than it looks.

Ingestion validation

Stage
6
Location
cloud
Does

Schema conformance, partner presence, monotonicity, mandatory-field checks. REJECTS rather than repairs.

Why Reject

A dataset containing quietly repaired records cannot be defended in an audit, a dispute, or a thesis. Rejected records go to the dock's error queue where a human sees them.

Detail
See telemetry-schema.json#/ingestion_validation

Climatology QC

Stage
7
Location
cloud
Does
Compare against this site's historical distribution for this season and this hour.
Why It Cannot Be At the Edge
It needs the site's history, which lives in the cloud.
The Cold Start Problem

A new site has no climatology, so this test is inert for the first season. New sites are therefore the ones with the weakest QC — precisely when we know the least about the water and are most likely to be fooled. Copernicus history partially mitigates this and is free; use it.

Fusion

Stage
8
Location
cloud
Does

Fit the site-and-day-local NDCI-to-chlorophyll regression from same-sortie probe points. Apply across the masked field. Propagate uncertainty.

Why This Is the Core
This stage is the product. See system-architecture.json#/the_fusion_core.
Fails By

If fewer than the minimum probe points exist, or the regression's confidence interval exceeds a threshold, the concentration field is NOT produced. The NDCI field is published as a relative index instead, clearly labelled as such. Refusing to produce a bad map is a feature.

The Normal Case
Fitting on 3-4 points. The confidence interval will often be wide. Show it.

Derived indices

Stage
9
Location
cloud
Does
Thermocline depth, Schmidt stability, oxygen debt, diel amplitude.
Fails By

Partial profiles produce nulls with `inputs_complete: false`, never an interpolated guess. A stability index computed from a truncated column is not comparable to one from a full column, and quietly computing it anyway would corrupt the time series that is our entire moat.

Twin update

Stage
10
Location
cloud
Does

Update measured state, recompute interpolation with its uncertainty field, decay stale state.

Alerting

Stage
11
Location
cloud primary, dock fallback
Does
Evaluate rules. Dispatch with evidence attached.
The Critical Design Decision

Safety-critical alerts are RULE-BASED, not model-based. A threshold on measured DO fires locally at the dock with no cloud and no model. Model-based alerts are advisory and degrade gracefully when the cloud is unreachable.

Why

The alert that matters most must have the fewest dependencies. A DO-below-threshold alert should survive a cloud outage, a model regression, and a bad deploy. Coupling it to an ML inference service is an architecture that fails at 04:30.

Suspect Data Rule

QC flag 3 data is shown to humans but does NOT fire automated alerts. A false alarm from a fouled sensor at 04:30 costs more trust than a delayed alert costs fish — because after the third false alarm, the tenth alert is ignored, and the tenth may be real.

Presentation

Stage
12
Location
cloud + local dashboard
Does
Twin, dashboard, mobile, reports.

The Alert Fatigue Problem

Statement

This is a safety issue disguised as a UX issue, and it will kill the product's value long before it kills a fish.

Mechanism

Every false alarm spends trust. Trust is not renewable at the rate we spend it. After the third 04:30 alert that turns out to be a fouled sensor or a floating coconut, the manager mutes notifications. Then the real one arrives and nobody sees it.

The Design Response
  • Tune the anomaly model for PRECISION over recall, deliberately accepting misses. A missed dead fish is found two hours later; a false alarm at 04:30 is unrecoverable trust damage.
  • Suspect-flagged data never auto-alerts.
  • Every alert carries its evidence — the image crop, the profile chart, the threshold it crossed. An alert that cannot explain itself will be ignored the third time.
  • Escalate by severity, not by volume. Three advisories should not produce three notifications.
  • Track the alert-to-action rate as a first-class product metric. If people stop acting on alerts, the product has failed regardless of how accurate the sensors are.

Data Retention

Raw

Indefinite. It is the asset and the moat — three years of profiles on a lake nobody else has measured is worth more than the aircraft.

Imagery

Reduced products indefinite. Raw frames tiered to cold storage after processing; deleted only under the imagery privacy policy.

The Privacy Exception

Raw imagery containing identifiable people must be handled under the retention limits in ethics-data-privacy.json, regardless of its scientific value. This is a genuine tension: the frames most useful for training are the ones over the busiest, most populated shorelines.

What Is Deliberately Not Automated

  • Dispensing. Human authorisation per mission, always.
  • Declaring water safe. The system reports what it measured and what it did not measure. Absence of a detection is not a clearance.
  • Overriding a QC flag. If a human decides a suspect reading is good, that is a new record with an author, not an edit.
  • Changing a customer's alert thresholds. We do not tune someone else's risk tolerance from our side of the network.
