Data Pipeline

data-pipeline v2.0.0 updated 2026-07-18 ◎Target raw JSON ↗

The processing path from observation to decision, across every phase. A Phase 1/2 farm's path runs from a farmer's thumb to a logging service; a Phase 3 farm's path runs from a sonde to a fusion engine. Both converge on the same shared cloud stages — ingestion validation, alerting, presentation — because they are, underneath, the same platform. Stages are ordered; each stage's failure behaviour is specified, because in a monitoring system a silent pipeline failure is worse than a loud one.

The Governing Rule

A monitoring system that fails silently is worse than no monitoring system, because someone is relying on it. Every stage below must fail loudly or degrade visibly. There is no acceptable silent failure anywhere in this pipeline — and 'the farmer stopped logging and the app didn't say anything about it' is exactly as much a silent failure as a fouled sensor reporting plausible numbers.

App Layer Pipeline

Note

Phase 1/2. This is the pipeline behind the free app and the Premium tier, and it exists independently of any hardware.

Stages

Capture

Stage
A1
Location
client — phone/web app
Does

Farmer enters a manual water-quality reading, logs a feeding, submits a disease photo, or chats with the assistant.

Fails By

Entry is cached locally the instant it is made. Nothing is lost to a dropped connection or a closed app.

Local cache and offline queue

Stage
A2
Location
client
Does
Holds unsynced entries indefinitely until connectivity returns.
Why It Is Not Optional

The rural connectivity problem is not unique to a lakeshore dock — a smallholder's phone has the same problem, and the entry that matters most (a mortality event, a sudden bad reading) is exactly as likely to happen with no signal as a pre-dawn sortie is.

Sync

Stage
A3
Location
client to backend
Does
Idempotent, resumable upload of queued entries when connectivity permits.
Clock Note

A phone offline for days still needs accurate timestamps on `measured_utc`, or trend and reminder logic is corrupted on reconnection — the same clock-discipline requirement as the Phase 3 dock, applied to a phone.

Ingestion validation

Stage
A4
Location
cloud — shared with Phase 3
Does

Schema conformance, farm/pond resolution, mandatory-field checks. REJECTS malformed entries rather than repairing them.

Detail

See telemetry-schema.json#/ingestion_validation. The same rejection discipline applies to a manual entry as to a sensor sample; a dataset with quietly repaired self-reports is exactly as indefensible as one with quietly repaired sensor data.

Model inference (where applicable)

Stage
A5
Location
cloud
Does

Disease-identification-photo runs against a submitted photo; the assistant grounds and responds to a chat message.

Fails By

A model failure returns an honest 'unable to analyse this photo' rather than a guess. See ai-ml-models.json.

Premium analytics and predictive scoring

Stage
A6
Location
cloud
Does

For Premium farms, the analytics engine and the predictive-health-anomaly model read the farm's accumulated logged (and/or sensor) history.

Fails By

Below a data-completeness threshold, the service returns 'insufficient logged history' rather than a thin-looking trend or score. See ai-ml-models.json#/models/predictive-health-anomaly.

Alerting

Stage
A7
Location
cloud — shared with Phase 3
Does

Evaluate rule-based thresholds against manually logged values exactly as against sensor values.

Why

A farmer who logs a dangerously low DO reading deserves the same threshold alert a sensor farm gets, using the same alerting service, not a second-class notification path.

Presentation

Stage
A8
Location
client + cloud
Does

Home view, water-quality log, feeding reminders, community, learning, and (Premium) analytics/recommendations/marketplace/consultations.

Stages

Acquisition

Stage
1
Phase
3
Location
edge — aircraft
Does

Read sensors, timestamp UTC, tag with depth from the pressure transducer, tag with position and accuracy.

Fails By

Sensor fault flags the channel and continues. The sortie is not aborted for one dead channel — a profile with seven good channels is worth having.

Silent Failure Risk

A fouled sensor does not report a fault. It reports plausible, drifting, confidently wrong numbers. This is the pipeline's most dangerous failure and no stage here catches it — only the scheduled reference check against a laboratory standard does. That check is therefore not a maintenance chore; it is a data integrity control.

QC at source

Stage
2
Phase
3
Location
edge — aircraft
Does

Gross range, spike, rate-of-change, flat-line, and partner-presence tests. Flags applied per QARTOD codes.

Citations
qartod-manual
Why At the Edge

Flagging at source means the flag travels with the data forever. QC applied later in the cloud cannot reconstruct what the sensor was doing at the moment of the reading.

The Flat Line Test

Deserves special mention: a sensor that has stopped responding and started repeating its last value is the failure mode that most resembles good data. A flat line in a diel DO series is physically impossible and it is the cheapest fouling detector available.

Edge inference and reduction

Stage
3
Phase
3
Location
edge — aircraft
Does
NDWI mask, NDCI field, anomaly detection, compression.
Why Here

Raw multispectral imagery is too large to upload over intermittent cellular. And a bloom detection that requires backhaul fails on the day the network is congested — which, in a lake town during an incident, is exactly the day it will be.

Constraint
Every watt costs endurance.

Transfer to dock

Stage
4
Phase
3
Location
edge
Does

Local high-bandwidth link on docking. Full raw imagery moves here; only reduced products go further.

Fails By
Retry. Data stays on the aircraft until acknowledged.

Store and forward

Stage
5
Phase
3
Location
edge — dock
Does
Buffer 7+ days locally. Forward when connectivity permits. Idempotent, resumable sync.
Why It Is Not Optional

The link will be down. The pre-dawn profile — the most valuable data the system produces — must survive it. A dock that loses the dawn profile because the tower was congested has failed at the one thing it exists for.

Requirement Id
REQ-DOCK-005
Clock Note

A dock offline for days still needs UTC accuracy or the diel analysis is corrupted on reconnection. Local RTC discipline matters more than it looks.

Ingestion validation

Stage
6
Phase
1-3, shared
Location
cloud
Does

Schema conformance, partner presence, monotonicity, mandatory-field checks, farm/pond resolution. REJECTS rather than repairs.

Why Reject

A dataset containing quietly repaired records cannot be defended in an audit, a dispute, or a support conversation. Rejected sensor records go to the dock's error queue; rejected app-layer records go to the client's sync error queue. Either way, a human sees them.

Detail
See telemetry-schema.json#/ingestion_validation

Climatology QC

Stage
7
Phase
3
Location
cloud
Does
Compare against this site's historical distribution for this season and this hour.
Why It Cannot Be At the Edge
It needs the site's history, which lives in the cloud.
The Cold Start Problem

A new site has no climatology, so this test is inert for the first season. New sites are therefore the ones with the weakest QC — precisely when we know the least about the water and are most likely to be fooled. Copernicus history partially mitigates this and is free; use it.

Fusion

Stage
8
Phase
3
Location
cloud
Does

Fit the site-and-day-local NDCI-to-chlorophyll regression from same-sortie probe points. Apply across the masked field. Propagate uncertainty.

Why This Is the Core
This stage is the product's Phase 3 core. See system-architecture.json#/the_fusion_core.
Fails By

If fewer than the minimum probe points exist, or the regression's confidence interval exceeds a threshold, the concentration field is NOT produced. The NDCI field is published as a relative index instead, clearly labelled as such. Refusing to produce a bad map is a feature.

The Normal Case
Fitting on 3-4 points. The confidence interval will often be wide. Show it.

Derived indices

Stage
9
Phase
3
Location
cloud
Does
Thermocline depth, Schmidt stability, oxygen debt, diel amplitude.
Fails By

Partial profiles produce nulls with `inputs_complete: false`, never an interpolated guess. A stability index computed from a truncated column is not comparable to one from a full column, and quietly computing it anyway would corrupt the time series that is our entire moat.

Analytics, prediction, and twin update

Stage
10
Phase
1-3, shared
Location
cloud
Does

For Phase 3 farms: update measured twin state, recompute interpolation with its uncertainty field, decay stale state. For Phase 1/2 Premium farms: update the analytics and predictive-health views over logged history, with the same staleness/sparsity decay logic applied to log entries instead of sensor state.

Alerting

Stage
11
Phase
1-3, shared
Location
cloud primary, dock fallback for Phase 3
Does

Evaluate rules against sensor data or manually logged data. Dispatch with evidence attached.

The Critical Design Decision

Safety-critical alerts are RULE-BASED, not model-based, at every phase. A threshold on measured or logged DO fires with no model in the loop. Model-based alerts (predictive-health-anomaly, kill-risk-forecast) are advisory and degrade gracefully when the cloud or the model is unreachable or under-confident.

Why

The alert that matters most must have the fewest dependencies. A DO-below-threshold alert should survive a cloud outage, a model regression, and a bad deploy — whether the DO reading came from a sonde or a test strip. Coupling it to an ML inference service is an architecture that fails at 04:30.

Suspect Data Rule

QC flag 3 sensor data is shown to humans but does NOT fire automated alerts. Self-reported (manual) data is never QC-flagged in the QARTOD sense, but obviously implausible manual entries (e.g. a pH of 25) are similarly excluded from automated triggers and flagged for the farmer to confirm or correct. A false alarm from a fouled sensor, or a fat-fingered log entry, at 04:30 costs more trust than a delayed alert costs fish — because after the third false alarm, the tenth alert is ignored, and the tenth may be real.

Presentation

Stage
12
Phase
1-3, shared
Location
cloud + client app + local dashboard
Does

App home view, logging forms, community/learning, Premium analytics/recommendations/marketplace/consultations, and — for Phase 3 — twin, enterprise dashboard, mobile, reports.

The Alert Fatigue Problem

Statement

This is a safety issue disguised as a UX issue, and it will kill the product's value long before it kills a fish — at any tier.

Mechanism

Every false alarm spends trust. Trust is not renewable at the rate we spend it. After the third 04:30 alert that turns out to be a fouled sensor or a floating coconut, the manager mutes notifications. A parallel version exists in the app tier: after the third feeding reminder or logging nudge that feels irrelevant or nagging, the farmer mutes those too, and the data flywheel Phase 2/3 depend on stalls.

The Design Response
  • Tune the anomaly and disease-ID models for PRECISION over recall, deliberately accepting misses. A missed dead fish is found two hours later; a false alarm at 04:30 is unrecoverable trust damage.
  • Suspect-flagged or implausible-value data never auto-alerts.
  • Every alert or reminder carries its evidence or its reason — the image crop, the profile chart, the threshold it crossed, or the schedule it is following. An alert that cannot explain itself will be ignored the third time.
  • Escalate by severity, not by volume. Three advisories should not produce three notifications, and a feeding reminder should not compete for attention with a genuine water-quality warning.
  • Track the alert-to-action rate AND the reminder-to-log rate as first-class product metrics. If people stop acting on either, the product has failed regardless of how accurate the sensors or models are.

Data Retention

Raw

Indefinite, across every phase. It is the asset and the moat — for a Phase 3 farm, three years of sensor profiles on a lake nobody else has measured; for a Phase 1/2 farm, the accumulated history of its own logged cycles, which is the exact dataset Phase 2's analytics and predictive models are built to need.

Imagery

Reduced products indefinite (Phase 3). Raw frames tiered to cold storage after processing; deleted only under the imagery privacy policy.

Photos

Disease-identification and community-post photos retained per the same imagery/personal-data privacy discipline as drone footage, regardless of the fact that a farmer, not a drone, captured them.

The Privacy Exception

Raw imagery or photos containing identifiable people must be handled under the retention limits in ethics-data-privacy.json, regardless of scientific value or product usefulness. This is a genuine tension at both ends of the platform: the drone frames most useful for training are the ones over the busiest shorelines, and the disease photos most useful for training are the ones that most often also capture a farmer's hands or face.

What Is Deliberately Not Automated

  • Dispensing. Human authorisation per mission, always (Phase 3).
  • Declaring water safe, at any tier. The system reports what it measured or what a farmer logged, and what it did not. Absence of a reading is not a clearance.
  • Diagnosing a disease. The photo-ID model suggests candidates; a vet or expert diagnoses.
  • Overriding a QC flag or a self-reported value. If a human decides a suspect or implausible reading is good, that is a new record with an author, not an edit.
  • Changing a customer's alert thresholds. We do not tune someone else's risk tolerance from our side of the network.
  • Settling a marketplace dispute or a consultation fee disagreement without a human in the loop.
