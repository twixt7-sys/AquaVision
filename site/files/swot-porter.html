Note

So What
Strategy Implied

The strengths cluster around depth-plus-imaging; the weaknesses cluster around maturity, endurance, and regulation. That combination argues for: (1) prove the probe claim first, because it is the only defensible thing; (2) plan for a buoy hybrid rather than against it, because our own matrix says the hybrid wins; (3) start the BVLOS conversation immediately, because it is the longest pole; (4) sell aeration efficiency before selling catastrophe prevention, because we cannot fly in the weather where catastrophes happen.

The Uncomfortable Read

An honest reading of this SWOT says the OmniDrone is a coverage instrument for chronic management, not a catastrophe-prevention system — because we are grounded during the storms that cause catastrophes. That is a smaller, less thrilling story and it is probably the true one. It is also a better business, because chronic management pays every cycle. The pitch should follow the analysis rather than the drama.

Forces
Force
Threat of New Entrants
Intensity
high
Reasoning

Multirotor airframes are commoditised, sondes are off-the-shelf, and multispectral cameras are purchasable. The barriers are the winch integration, the calibration workflow, and the regulatory certificate — real but not insurmountable, and none of them are patents.

Our Defence

Accumulated site-specific data, local relationships, and regulatory position. None of which we have yet. Our moat is a plan, not an asset.

Honest Note
A well-funded team could replicate the hardware in eighteen months. What they could not quickly replicate is three years of Taal Lake profiles. That argues for deploying early and cheaply to start the data clock, even at poor margins.
Force
Bargaining Power of Buyers
Intensity
high
Reasoning

Buyers have a free alternative: doing nothing, which is what they do now. They have no budget line to defend and no switching cost to overcome, which sounds good and is not — it means there is nothing to displace, so every sale is a fresh act of persuasion.

Our Defence
Demonstrated peso outcome in the first cycle. Nothing else works.
Force
Bargaining Power of Suppliers
Intensity
medium
Reasoning

Sensor suppliers for aquaculture-grade sondes are concentrated, and the optical DO and fluorometer market has few credible vendors. Battery and flight controller supply is competitive. Chinese airframe component supply is cheap and abundant but carries geopolitical and export-control exposure.

Our Defence

Design for sensor interchangeability from the start. A probe architecture locked to one vendor's sonde hands them our margin.

Force
Threat of Substitutes
Intensity
high
Reasoning

Buoys, USVs, satellites, manual sampling, and the farmer's own experience are all substitutes, and each is better than us on some axis. Experience in particular is free and has kept these farms alive for decades.

Our Defence

The specific combination. And genuine humility about the scale threshold below which a buoy simply wins.

Force
Competitive Rivalry
Intensity
low today, rising
Reasoning

No dominant commercial drone-sonde product exists in this market. That is either an opportunity or a signal that the market is not there — and the honest position is that we do not yet know which.

Note

Many academic prototypes, no commercial winner, is a pattern that rewards careful reading. Founders explain it away; they should not.

Overall Industry Attractiveness

Structurally difficult. High entrant threat, high buyer power, high substitute threat, and a customer base with no existing budget. The attractive part is not the industry structure — it is the growth of the underlying sector and the absence of an incumbent in this specific niche. Enter with clear eyes.

Pestel

Political
  • LGU control of cage permits makes local politics a gating factor on every deployment.
  • BFAR and DENR mandates create both a customer and a regulator.
  • Fish kills are politically salient events in lake municipalities, which cuts both ways.
Economic
  • Smallholder capital scarcity is the binding commercial constraint.
  • Feed price volatility drives the FCR value proposition.
  • Aquaculture credit is thin and expensive because risk is unquantified — which is our long-term wedge.
Social
  • Distrust of technology vendors, earned by the sector's history.
  • Low digital literacy shapes the dashboard, not just its UX.
  • Drone overflight of lakeside communities is a social licence question decided over months of conversation, not by a privacy policy.
  • Occupational drowning risk is normalised in a way outsiders find shocking.
Technological
  • Multispectral sensor cost and mass have fallen to viability.
  • Edge inference at a few watts is now practical.
  • Battery energy density remains the binding endurance constraint and improves slowly.
  • Free Copernicus data is available and unexploited.
Environmental
  • Increasing frequency of thermal and rainfall extremes worsens the problem.
  • Eutrophication of shared water bodies is a commons tragedy with an information vacuum at its centre.
  • Volcanic activity at Taal is a locally distinctive chemistry driver.
  • Typhoons are a design condition, not an edge case.
Legal
  • CAAP RPAS rules gate the operating concept; BVLOS requires specific authorisation.
  • Medicine dispensing crosses into regulated veterinary territory.
  • Data privacy obligations attach to imagery of people and property.
  • Liability for a missed warning is genuinely unresolved and needs legal advice, not founder optimism.
