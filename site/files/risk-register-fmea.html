Risk Register & FMEA

risk-register-fmea v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ?Assumption ◆Confidence: medium raw JSON ↗

Method

FMEA. RPN = Severity x Likelihood x Detectability, each 1-10. Higher RPN = higher priority. Detectability: 1 = certain to be caught before harm, 10 = undetectable until harm occurs.

Note

Scores are the authors' engineering judgement, not measured failure rates. They are a PRIORITISATION tool, not a probability claim. Recompute after every design change and after every real incident. The value is in the ranking, not the absolute numbers.

The Philosophy

The risks that kill ventures are rarely the dramatic ones. They are the ones with high SEVERITY and high DETECTABILITY-difficulty — the failures nobody sees coming until the harm is done. This register is sorted to surface those, not the obvious ones.

The Phased Scope

This register now spans all three phases. R-01 through R-15 are the Phase 3 hardware/field FMEA and are unchanged. R-16 through R-18 are Phase 1/2 software and business risks, scored with the same method and the same discipline — an untested freemium funnel gets named as bluntly as an untested winch.

R-01
Sensor fouling produces plausible but wrong readings; a farmer acts on bad data.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Biofilm drift on optical/electrode sensors, undetected between reference checks.
Effect: False 'all clear' or false alarm. A false all-clear that precedes a real kill is the worst outcome the product can produce — we told them they were safe.
Mitigation: Weekly reference check (the only real control); flat-line and climatology QC tests; drift detection; dry-dock storage that reduces fouling versus a moored buoy.
Note: HIGHEST RPN in the register, and it is not a dramatic failure — it is a silent one. This is the risk that most deserves attention and least attracts it. A monitoring product's deepest liability is confidently wrong data.

R-02
Unvalidated kill-risk score produces a false negative; farmer defers action and loses stock.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Shipping a predictive model before validation under commercial pressure.
Effect: We convert the farmer's cautious uncertainty into false confidence, and they act on it. Direct harm caused by us.
Mitigation: Do not ship the score until validated. Show the mechanism in plain language instead. Hard organisational rule that the scientist has veto over predictive claims.
Note: The mitigation is organisational discipline, not technology, which makes it fragile — it must survive the commercial pressure documented in ai-ml-models.json. Listed at severity 10 because a false negative here can cost a livelihood.

R-18
Self-reported Phase 1 water-quality and farm logs (manual/photo entry, no sensor verification) are inaccurate, inconsistent, rounded, or gamed, and Phase 2 predictive models are trained on this base.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: No hardware verification exists at Phase 1; farmers may mis-measure, estimate, forget entries, or enter optimistic values, and there is no reference check analogous to the weekly sonde calibration that catches drift in Phase 3.
Effect: Phase 2 predictive health and analytics models inherit noisy or biased training data. This is the software-era version of R-01: a plausible but wrong number that someone acts on, except here there is no dedicated sonde and no reference-check control to catch it.
Mitigation: Plausibility and range checks on entry; gentle prompts rather than blocking; cross-reference against community/regional benchmarks to flag outliers; photo-based entries verified where possible by the same disease-ID computer vision already in the product; when a farm graduates to Phase 3 sensors, use the overlap period to calibrate and score the reliability of that farm's prior self-reports rather than trusting them uniformly.
Note: SECOND-HIGHEST raw RPN in the register, and it is R-01's direct software analogue — the philosophy above (silent data corruption over dramatic failure) applies just as much to a self-reported log as to a fouled sensor. There is no equivalent of the reference check here yet; this is an open control gap, not a solved one.

R-05
Aircraft with deployed probe snags on cage structure over people.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Submerged, invisible netting and mooring lines.
Effect: Uncontrolled aircraft directly above a working cage where people are present. Potential injury.
Mitigation: Tension anomaly detection; slack-and-retry; tether cutter; shrouded rotors; station siting away from structure; no-profile zones; operator ground-clearance during sorties.
Note: The shrouds (which cost mass and endurance) pay for themselves here. This is the risk that justifies not cutting them.

R-14
Unit economics do not close; cost to serve exceeds willingness to pay.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Underestimated maintenance labour, per-site sonde cost, dock cost, battery opex, and dispersed geography.
Effect: Negative contribution margin at scale. Growth accelerates losses.
Mitigation: Real BOM quotes in Phase 0. Measured (not modeled) unit economics in the Phase 4 pilot. Density-based site qualification. Cooperative amortisation.
Note: See unit-economics.json, which refuses to state a number precisely because this risk is unquantified. High RPN and it hides inside a spreadsheet that looks fine until the assumptions are tested.

R-08
Loss of social licence in a lake community.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Perceived surveillance, a crash, a wildlife incident, or simply deploying without community engagement.
Effect: The community makes operation socially impossible. In a lake town, this is decided over months of conversation and is nearly irreversible once lost.
Mitigation: Community notification at commissioning; no person-detection; no IUU-enforcement use; transparent purpose; benign design language; local hiring.
Note: High RPN and hard to detect until it is too late. Social licence is not recoverable at the rate it is lost. Under-weighted by technical teams because it is not an engineering problem.

R-12
Technician disengagement degrades data quality.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Technician perceives the system as surveillance of their performance; stops charging, cleaning, reporting faithfully.
Effect: Silent data degradation; the deployment fails and nobody knows why.
Mitigation: Never surface a technician-performance metric. Frame the system as the technician's instrument. Local hiring and training. Include them in the value story.
Note: High RPN driven by detectability — a disengaged technician does not file a ticket. This is a design-and-culture control, and it must survive an owner asking for the performance dashboard. See customer-personas.json.
 657 210 84 …Mitigating field_ops
R-04
Target operators will not pay at any price we can offer.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Most operators currently spend ~zero on monitoring; we are creating a budget line, not taking share.
Effect: No revenue. The venture fails regardless of technical success.
Mitigation: Phase 0 farmer discovery with real willingness-to-pay testing (paid pilots, not survey questions). Lead with the checkable aeration-cost ROI. Cooperative and financing channels for smallholders.
Note: This is a market risk, not a technical one, and it is the one most likely to actually kill the company. It is detectable early (Phase 0) if the team is honest in discovery — which is why the Phase 0 gate has a kill criterion for it.

R-15
Enabling over-stocking — the product makes the underlying problem worse.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Better instruments let operators push density closer to the edge.
Effect: Higher stocking, higher systemic risk, and complicity in the very problem we sell against.
Mitigation: Carrying-capacity analytics that surface the systemic cost; cooperative/regulator framing; not marketing the product as a licence to intensify.
Note: An ethical risk that is also a real second-order effect. Deserves the thesis ethics chapter. Under-detectable because it manifests as customer 'success' (higher stocking) before it manifests as harm.

R-03
CAAP declines or indefinitely delays BVLOS authorisation.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: External regulatory decision outside our control.
Effect: The autonomous dawn-sortie use case — the flagship — becomes illegal or requires a pilot at 04:30, destroying the unit economics.
Mitigation: Engage CAAP from Phase 0. Build the safety case early. Stress-test the business model against VLOS-only economics so BVLOS is upside, not a load-bearing assumption.
Note: Detectability is low (3) — we will see this coming, which is the one mercy. But it gates the margin, so it is on the critical path regardless.

R-06
Aircraft ditches; lithium pack contaminates a food-production water body.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Motor failure, severe weather, winch entanglement, or link loss over water.
Effect: Environmental incident in the water the customer farms. Reputational and regulatory harm beyond the lost asset.
Mitigation: Positive buoyancy; lithium containment; locator beacon; water-aware failsafe; consider hexarotor for motor-out survivability.
 854 160 64 …Mitigating cto + embedded_lead
R-09
Tethered-probe pendulum instability makes the aircraft uncontrollable in useful wind.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Swinging pendulum mass couples into the flight controller as an unmodeled disturbance; natural frequency shifts with tether length.
Effect: The real wind limit collapses; mission profiles fail; the platform becomes fair-weather only.
Mitigation: Pendulum feedforward control; tether damping; flight-test characterisation early; accept a lower wind limit if necessary.
Note: The single largest technical unknown. Detectable early (Phase 2 flight test) — which is why the winch and pendulum work is on the critical path.
 863 144 96 !Open cto + embedded_lead
R-17
Free-tier farmers churn before converting to Phase 2 paid tiers or before enough longitudinal data accumulates to make the free tier itself useful.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: No sustained logging habit forms; a farm's production cycle runs months, which is longer than most consumer apps' engagement window; free substitutes (a notebook, a group chat) are 'good enough' for a beginner.
Effect: Undercuts both revenue (no conversion to Premium/marketplace/consultations) and the Phase 2 data asset — predictive health models need the longitudinal logs that churned users never produced.
Mitigation: Habit-forming cadence (feeding/logging reminders tied to real husbandry tasks, not notifications for their own sake); visible personal value (trend charts on the farmer's own data) before any upsell; community features that create a reason to return independent of the core logging task.
Note: This is the freemium-model equivalent of R-04 — untested willingness to stay, not just willingness to pay. Detectable via cohort retention curves early, which is why instrumenting retention from the first Phase 1 release matters.

R-13
Toxicity overclaim leads to consumption of contaminated product.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: UI or marketing blurs the pigment-vs-toxin distinction; operator reads low chlorophyll as 'safe to harvest'.
Effect: Food safety incident. People eat contaminated shellfish or fish.
Mitigation: Hard rule: never display a toxicity indicator. Chlorophyll and phycocyanin always framed as 'sample the water', never as clearance. Recommend lab assay on elevated readings.
Note: Low likelihood only because of disciplined framing. The severity is maximal because the harm is to a consumer's health, not a customer's margin.

R-11
Weather grounds the aircraft exactly when risk peaks.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Monsoon storms trigger overturn events AND exceed the flight envelope.
Effect: The catastrophe-prevention claim is undercut — we cannot fly during the events we sell against.
Mitigation: Reposition as chronic-management + pre-event measurement (measure the loaded gun before the storm). Fixed-buoy hybrid for continuity through weather. Simulation core to project through the gap.
Note: High likelihood (8) — this is not a maybe, it is a certainty. The mitigation is honest positioning, not engineering. Trying to sell around it rather than reposition around it is the trap.
 682 96 60 …Mitigating cto + ceo
R-16
Phase 1 app activation rate is too low — installs do not convert into a completed farm profile or a first logged reading.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Onboarding friction, unclear value before enough data has accumulated to show anything back to the user, uneven digital literacy and connectivity among smallholder segments.
Effect: The acquisition engine fills the top of the funnel but not the middle; CAC is spent without an activated base, and there is no user base to feed the Phase 2 conversion funnel or the Phase 2 ML models.
Mitigation: Guided, short farm-setup wizard; show a useful result (even a generic one) before asking for the first real log; community-led onboarding through existing fish-farming groups rather than cold acquisition; track activation as a first-class metric from day one.
Note: Low detectability difficulty (2) is the good news — activation is a directly measured funnel metric, visible immediately, unlike the silent hardware failures above. The risk is real but at least it cannot hide.

R-07
Autonomous or erroneous dispensing over-doses a water body.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Model error, sensor error, or software fault triggering discharge.
Effect: Chemical harm to stock. A poisoned pond.
Mitigation: No autonomous dispensing, ever. Per-mission human authorisation. Firmware dose ceiling not overridable from the dashboard. Mechanical interlock. Load-cell logging.
Note: Low likelihood BECAUSE of the hard-coded controls. Remove any one of them and the likelihood jumps. The controls are the reason this RPN is low, not an inherent property of the system.

R-10
Mass budget does not close; the aircraft cannot carry the mission.
Cause · effect · mitigation
Cause: Component masses exceed the paper targets, especially the sonde and winch.
Effect: The concept as described is not buildable on a viable multirotor.
Mitigation: Per-mission payload configuration; reposition dispensing as treatment-only; accept a heavier MTOM bracket; descope thermal. This is Phase 0 paper arithmetic — resolvable before spending on hardware.
Note: Low detectability difficulty (2) is the good news: it is arithmetic available today. The mass budget in omnidrone-specifications.json already shows it does not close fully-configured, which is why per-mission configuration is baseline rather than optimisation.

The Pattern

The top residual risks are NOT the dramatic ones (crash, poisoning). They are silent data corruption, commercial non-viability, and second-order harms — and the pattern now holds across the whole platform, not just the hardware: R-18 (unverified self-reported farm logs) is the Phase 1/2 software mirror of R-01 (drifted sensors), and it now outranks it. Dramatic risks attract engineering attention and get mitigated hard; quiet risks do not, so their residual scores stay high. A team that only worries about crashes — or only about churn — is worrying about the wrong things.

How To Use This Register

  • In the pitch: show R-04, R-14, and R-03 unprompted. Volunteering your top commercial and regulatory risks with mitigations is more convincing than a slide claiming there are none.
  • In the thesis: this is the limitations-and-risk chapter, and R-15 anchors the ethics discussion.
  • In engineering: the residual RPN ranking is the priority order. Work the top of the list.
  • Recompute after every real incident. A register that never changes is a register nobody uses.
