UN Sustainable Development Goal Alignment

sdg-alignment v1.0.0 updated 2026-07-17 ?Assumption raw JSON ↗

Note

SDG alignment is easy to overclaim — every startup maps itself to half the goals. This file distinguishes goals the product GENUINELY and DIRECTLY serves from ones it touches tangentially, and it flags where the product's second-order effects could run AGAINST a goal. An honest SDG map includes the tensions.

Phase Context

Statement

AquaVision is now three layers, and the SDG story differs by layer. Phase 1 (free app: guided setup, AI assistant, water-quality logging, disease ID, learning modules, community) is the mechanism that reaches smallholders who previously had NO guidance at all — this is where SDG 1, 2, and 4 land hardest, and it does so without the affordability paradox that constrains the hardware layer. Phase 2 (premium subscription, marketplace, expert consultations) is where SDG 8/9 livelihood effects strengthen as farms convert to paid tools. Phase 3 (OmniDrone/IoT/PondTwin, enterprise) is the layer this file's ORIGINAL analysis was written for — SDG 14 (depth-resolved water monitoring) and the environmental tensions below still apply there, but Phase 3 is priced for commercial/enterprise operators, so its equity contribution is weaker than Phase 1's.

Honest Caveat

Do not let the free Phase 1 tier launder the Phase 3 equity problem. A smallholder using the free app for water-quality logging is not the same as a smallholder benefiting from drone-based depth monitoring — those remain two different products with two different reach profiles.

Primary Alignment

Zero Hunger

Sdg
2
Target
2.4 — sustainable food production systems and resilient agricultural practices
How Directly
strong
Mechanism

Aquatic food is a major and growing protein source; reducing losses and improving yield in aquaculture directly increases food availability. Aquaculture is now the majority of aquatic animal production.

Citations
fao-sofia-2024
Honest Caveat

The contribution is real but bounded by our addressable fraction of the sector and by whether farms act on the information. Do not claim to 'solve hunger'.

Life Below Water

Sdg
14
Target

14.2 — sustainably manage and protect marine and coastal ecosystems; 14.1 — reduce marine pollution

How Directly
strong
Mechanism

Measuring aquaculture's effluent load and supporting carrying-capacity management reduces the eutrophication of shared water bodies. Pollution detection supports 14.1.

Honest Caveat

The strongest environmental claim, BUT it depends on the carrying-capacity use case actually being adopted, which requires cross-farm data-sharing that is unresolved (see ethics-data-privacy.json). And note the over-stocking paradox below — the product could also enable intensification.

Responsible Consumption and Production

Sdg
12
Target
12.2 — sustainable management and efficient use of natural resources
How Directly
moderate-strong
Mechanism

Reducing feed waste (feed is ~60 percent of operating cost and a major environmental load) improves resource efficiency. Precision dosing reduces chemical and antimicrobial use.

Citations
innovasea-fcr-guide
Honest Caveat

The antimicrobial claim cuts both ways — easier dosing can mean more dosing. See the tension below. Note: at Phase 1/2 this mechanism runs through farmer behaviour change (better feeding decisions from logged data and AI guidance), not sensor precision — a softer but much more widely reachable version of the same claim.

Quality Education

Sdg
4
Target

4.4 — increase the number of people with relevant skills for decent work and entrepreneurship; 4.7 — knowledge and skills needed to promote sustainable development

How Directly
strong (Phase 1/2 specifically)
Mechanism

The Phase 1 free app is, functionally, an extension service delivered as software: an AI-assisted fish-farming guide, structured learning modules, photo-based disease identification, and a farmer community — reaching beginners and smallholders who had no prior access to aquaculture agronomy guidance at all. This is a NEW primary alignment created by the phased pivot, not present in the old hardware-only story.

Honest Caveat

Educational reach is not the same as educational EFFECT. An AI assistant answering questions is not equivalent to extension-agent contact, and 'learning module completed' is an activity metric, not a competency or outcome metric. See measuring_impact_honestly in social-economic-impact.json — this claim needs the same counterfactual discipline as the others, not an exemption because it feels more benign.

Secondary Alignment

No Poverty

Sdg
1
How Directly
moderate (strong at Phase 1/2)
Mechanism

For smallholders, a single kill event can be financially catastrophic. Preventing or mitigating it protects livelihoods. Continuous records could unlock credit and insurance for the currently uninsurable. Critically, the Phase 1 free tier now gives this population basic record-keeping, feeding guidance, and disease-ID alerting even before any hardware or paid tier is involved — resolving, for this specific mechanism, the affordability barrier the old hardware-only story could not.

Honest Caveat

The credit/insurance upside and the catastrophic-kill-prevention upside from Phase 3 depth monitoring still depend entirely on cooperative/institutional channels and remain unresolved by affordability. The Phase 1 free-tier contribution is real but narrower: better everyday decisions and record continuity, not depth-resolved early warning. Don't conflate the two.

Citations
worldbank-disease-risk-2014

Decent Work and Economic Growth

Sdg
8
How Directly
moderate
Mechanism

Removes the dangerous pre-dawn boat run (a real drowning risk), and stabilises aquaculture incomes (SDG 8.8 — safe working environments). At Phase 2, the marketplace and paid expert consultations create a more direct livelihood channel: better input prices/access via the marketplace, and professional advice smallholders previously could not reach or afford individually.

Honest Note
The occupational-safety angle is genuine and underused, and it is specifically a Phase 3 hardware benefit. It is a human argument, not just an efficiency one. The marketplace/consultation livelihood angle is Phase 2 and unproven — no transaction volume exists yet (see kpi-definitions.json phase_1_2_saas_kpis, all awaiting_data).

Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

Sdg
9
How Directly
moderate
Mechanism

Brings precision-agriculture-grade instrumentation to a sector and geography that lacks it (Phase 3), and, earlier and more broadly, brings basic digital record-keeping and data-driven farm management tools to smallholders who currently run entirely on paper or memory (Phase 1/2).

Honest Caveat

The Phase 1/2 infrastructure contribution is software adoption, which is a much lower bar than the Phase 3 instrumentation claim — worth stating plainly rather than letting the two blur together.

The Tensions An Honest Sdg Map Must Include

Note

This section is what separates a real SDG analysis from a marketing exercise. Every one of these is a way the product could work AGAINST a goal it claims to serve.

Tensions
Tension
Over-stocking (against SDG 14)
Detail

Better instruments may let operators push stocking density higher, worsening the eutrophication and carrying-capacity problems the product claims to help. A tool sold on environmental stewardship could enable environmental degradation.

Our Response

Carrying-capacity analytics aimed at collective governance; not marketing as a licence to intensify. Partial mitigation only. See risk R-15.

Tension
Antimicrobial use (against SDG 3, Good Health)
Detail

Easier dosing can mean more prophylactic antimicrobial use, contributing to antimicrobial resistance — a serious public health problem.

Our Response

Dose ceilings, human authorisation, precision placement to reduce total dose. Weak controls against a determined operator. See ethics-data-privacy.json.

Tension
Equity (against SDG 10, Reduced Inequalities)
Detail

The phased model changes but does not resolve this tension. Phase 1 (free) genuinely opens the door to smallholders who could never afford the old hardware-first product. But Phase 2's premium tier and Phase 3's enterprise hardware are still priced for farms that have already outgrown manual tracking — so the platform could still stratify: smallholders permanently on the free tier while capitalised farms buy their way to predictive analytics, marketplace leverage, and drone-based monitoring. A three-tier product can encode inequality just as easily as a single expensive product can, only with a free layer as cover.

Our Response

Cooperative and institutional channels remain the mechanism for equitable reach INTO Phase 2/3, and the free Phase 1 tier is itself a genuine, if partial, equity mechanism it did not have before. Neither is automatic. Whether Phase 1 usage among smallholders actually converts into better outcomes — not just into a large free user base that subsidises paid customers — is unmeasured. See kpi-smallholder-reach in kpi-definitions.json.

Tension
Privacy and surveillance (against multiple goals)
Detail

Aerial imagery over communities, and the latent surveillance capability, could harm the people the product is meant to serve.

Our Response
No person-detection, no enforcement use, data-minimisation. See ethics-data-privacy.json.
Tension
Environmental footprint of the hardware (against SDG 12)
Detail

Lithium batteries, electronic waste, and the risk of a ditched drone contaminating a food-production water body are real environmental costs of the solution itself.

Our Response

Lithium containment, ditching provision, and honest lifecycle accounting. A product sold on stewardship must account for its own footprint. See environmental-impact.json.

Why This Matters

An SDG map with only benefits is a sustainability-washing exercise, and sophisticated impact investors and thesis committees recognise it instantly. Including the tensions — with honest, sometimes-partial responses — is what makes the alignment credible.

How To Use This

For Impact Investors

Lead with SDG 2, 14, 12, and now 4 (the genuine primary alignments — 4 specifically for the Phase 1/2 free-education story), then volunteer the tensions. An impact investor who sees you have thought about the over-stocking paradox AND the free-tier-as-cover-for-stratification risk trusts the rest of your claims.

For the Thesis

This is the significance-of-the-study section. The tensions belong in the discussion chapter as limitations, not hidden.

The Discipline

Never map the product to a goal it does not directly serve just to fill the grid. Four strong alignments plus honest tensions beats seventeen weak ones. Adding SDG 4 because Phase 1 genuinely does deliver education-shaped value is legitimate; do not let the phased pivot become an excuse to add SDGs 10, 5, or others the product does not directly serve.
